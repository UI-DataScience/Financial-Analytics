Reported net income and\ndiluted EPS for the nine months ended August 31, 2003 were $989 million and\n$1.42, respectively.\n\n      Net revenue yields (net revenue per available lower berth day) for the\nthird quarter of 2004 increased 10.9 percent compared to net revenue yields in\nthe prior year, primarily due to higher cruise ticket prices and on-board\nrevenue and, to a lesser extent, the weak U.S. dollar relative to the euro and\nsterling. Net revenue yields as measured on a local currency basis ("constant\ndollar basis") increased 8.3 percent over the same period last year. Gross\nrevenue yields increased 10.1 percent.\n\n      Net cruise costs per available lower berth day ("ALBD") for the third\nquarter of 2004 were up 3.2 percent compared to costs for the same period last\nyear, primarily due to the impact of the weak dollar and higher fuel costs. On a\nconstant dollar basis, net cruise costs per ALBD increased 0.8 percent from the\nsame period last year largely due to higher fuel costs. Gross cruise costs per\nALBD increased 4.4 percent compared to the prior year.\n\n      Commenting on the third quarter results, Carnival Corporation & plc\nchairman and CEO Micky Arison said that he continues to be very pleased with the\ncompany\'s performance. "This record-breaking quarter is a testament to the\nsuccess of the merger with P&O Princess Cruises last year. It is our best\nquarter ever and the first time we have earned more than a billion dollars in a\nsingle quarter. All of our major cruise brands in North America and Europe are\nperforming well, building confidence in our outlook for the combined group for\n2005," Arison added.\n\n', u'\n\n      During the third quarter, in a move to optimize asset utilization for the\ngroup, Carnival Cruise Lines\' Jubilee was transferred to P&O Cruises Australia,\nmore than doubling the size of the brand\'s operations in the region. The ship\nwill be renamed Pacific Sun and will be the largest cruise ship based year-round\nin Australia. Pacific Sun is undergoing a major refurbishment before beginning\nseven- to 14-day South Pacific cruises from Sydney.\n\n      Also during the quarter, the company began the restructuring of Cunard\nLine\'s North American organization, which will be relocated to Santa Clarita,\nCalif., where Princess Cruises is based, by December 2004. As a result, Cunard\nwill share administrative, finance and IT functions with Princess in an effort\nto streamline operations and maximize efficiencies. This follows a similar\nrestructuring in the United Kingdom, where Cunard\'s UK operations were combined\nwith those of the other Carnival Corporation & plc UK brands. As previously\nannounced, the relocation is expected to reduce future overhead costs by\napproximately $20 million on an annual basis.\n\nComments on Full Year and Fourth Quarter 2004\n\n      Looking forward, Arison said that he is equally pleased with the outlook\nfor the full year 2004. "We are on track to achieve a record increase in revenue\nyields this year of approximately 9 percent, while absorbing an extraordinarily\nhigh 17 percent capacity increase," Arison added.\n\n      For the fourth quarter of 2004, advance booking levels are higher versus\nprior year\'s levels on a capacity adjusted basis, with pricing also ahead of\nlast year. As a result, the company expects that net revenue yields for the\nfourth quarter of 2004 will increase approximately 7 to 9 percent (5 to 7\npercent on a constant dollar basis), compared to last year\'s fourth quarter,\ndespite the negative effect of Hurricane Frances. Net cruise costs per ALBD in\nthe fourth quarter of 2004 are expected to be up 6 to 8 percent (4 to 6 percent\non a constant dollar basis), compared to 2003 primarily due to higher fuel\ncosts, costs associated with the Cunard reorganization and costs related to the\nhurricane. Based on these estimates, the company expects earnings per share for\nthe fourth quarter of 2004 to be in the range of $0.30 to $0.32. The company\'s\nguidance includes the estimated impact of Hurricane Frances of between $0.03 to\n$0.04 per share, as well as the cost of the aforementioned Cunard relocation of\nbetween $0.01 to $0.02 per share, both of which will primarily impact the 2004\nfourth quarter. The company\'s current guidance is based on an exchange rate of\n$1.23 to the euro and $1.82 to sterling.\n\n      The company has one ship scheduled to enter service during the fourth\nquarter of 2004. Costa Cruises\' 2,702-passenger Costa Magica, which will be one\nof the largest European passenger vessels, will operate a series of European\ncruises beginning November 10, 2004.\n\n', u'\n\n      The company has scheduled a conference call with analysts at 10 a.m. EDT\n(15.00 London time) today to discuss its 2004 third quarter earnings.
