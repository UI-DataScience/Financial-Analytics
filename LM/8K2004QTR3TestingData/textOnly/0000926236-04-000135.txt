Reports First Quarter Results\n\n\n * Servicing Portfolio Reaches $1.5 Billion\n\n * Steve Khoshabe and Anthony Schweiger added to Board of Directors\n\n\n Oak Brook, IL, Thursday, September 9, 2004 -- United Financial Mortgage\n Corp. (Amex: UFM or the "Company") today announced results for the first\n quarter ended July 31, 2004.\n\n\n First Quarter Results\n\n Revenues for the quarter ended July 31, 2004 decreased $6.9 million to $15.6\n million from $22.4 million for the quarter ended July 31, 2003.  Net income\n was $.9 million, or $.15 per diluted share (based on 6,148,940 fully diluted\n shares outstanding) for the most recent quarter, as compared with $1.8\n million, or $.43 per diluted share (based on 4,123,196 fully diluted shares\n outstanding), for the corresponding period last year.\n\n\n Mortgage Banking Business\n\n As of July 31, 2004, the mortgage loans in the Company\'s mortgage loan-\n servicing portfolio had an aggregate unpaid principal balance of $1.5\n billion, up from $939 million from the same date last year.  Income from\n mortgage loan-servicing increased by $561 thousand to $783 thousand for the\n quarter ended July 31, 2004, a 253% increase from the same period last year.\n The weighted average coupon interest rate of the mortgage loans in the loan-\n servicing portfolio was approximately 5.4% as of July 31, 2004.\n\n The Company funded $471 million in mortgage loans during the quarter ended\n July 31, 2004, a $547 million decrease compared to the quarter ended July\n 31, 2003.\n\n Commenting on mortgage operations in the Company\'s first quarter, Steve\n Khoshabe, President and Chief Executive Officer of the Company stated,\n "Despite facing the continued challenges of today\'s mortgage environment,\n we were able to deliver solid earnings and grow our book value to $4.97\n per share."\n\n Mr. Khoshabe added, "We continue to implement our growth strategy of\n increasing our servicing portfolio, growing our core origination platform,\n and diversifying our business.  During the first quarter, we opened two\n new retail offices in offices in Chicago, Illinois and Wilmington, North\n Carolina and a wholesale operations center in Charlotte, North Carolina.\n In addition, we have also added six retail branch offices through our\n acquisition of Vision Mortgage Group, Inc.\n\n With our balanced approach of continuing to selectively build our\n mortgage loan-servicing portfolio, expanding both organically and through\n acquisitions, controlling costs, and diversifying our business, we believe\n we are well positioned for continued growth in fiscal 2005."\n\n\n Other News\n\n At the Company\'s annual meeting held on September 8, 2004, all proposals\n submitted to the shareholders of the Company were approved.  Accordingly,\n the Board of Directors of the Company is now comprised of seven directors.\n The new directors are Messrs. Steve Khoshabe, the Company\'s President and\n Chief Executive Officer and Anthony Schweiger.  Mr. Schweiger will serve as\n the Company\'s Audit Committee Chairmen and qualifies as an audit committee\n financial expert in accordance with applicable laws.  Currently, Mr.\n Schweiger serves as the President and Chief Executive Officer of The\n Tomorrow Group, LLC, a governance and management consulting firm.  Since\n 1992, he has been a director and the Governance Chair of Radian Group Inc.,\n a NYSE traded global provider of credit enhancement products. He also serves\n on Radian\'s Audit and Executive Committees. Since 2001, Mr. Schweiger has\n served as a director of Paragon Technologies, Inc., an American Stock\n Exchange traded provider of automated solutions for material flow\n applications. He has also been an investor and director of Input\n Technologies, LLC, a supplier of human-to-machine interface products and\n services since February 1998. From 1983 until 1993, Mr. Schweiger served\n as Chief Executive Officer of Meridian Mortgage Corporation. In his capacity\n as a consultant, Mr. Schweiger advises various service and technology\n businesses on governance, operational and strategic issues.
