Completion of the transaction is subject to customary closing conditions,\nincluding  regulatory  approval of the transfer of  Centennial  Cable TV\'s cable\nfranchises, and is expected to occur in early 2005.\n\nCentennial  Cable TV  operates a digital  cable  television  system  that serves\napproximately  73,000  subscribers and passes over 300,000  contiguous  homes in\nsouthern  and  western  Puerto  Rico.  For the fiscal  year ended May 31,  2004,\nCentennial  Cable  TV\'s  "Adjusted   Operating   Income"  ("AOI")   contribution\nrepresented less than 5% of Centennial  Communications\'  total consolidated AOI.\nAOI  is  net  income  (loss)  before  income  tax  expense  (benefit),  interest\nexpense-net, loss on impairment or disposition of equipment and depreciation and\namortization.\n\n"This has proven to be a good time to monetize our cable television business and\nthis transaction  accelerates the de-leveraging of the Company," said Michael J.\nSmall, chief executive officer of Centennial.\n\nThe Company  expects  this to be a tax-free  transaction,  the proceeds of which\nwould be used to fund the build out of the  Lansing and Grand  Rapids,  Michigan\nlicenses  the  Company  expects to acquire  from AT&T  Wireless as well as other\ncapital requirements  contemplated in the Company\'s fiscal year 2004 guidance of\n$160 million in capital  expenditures. Welsh,\nCarson Anderson & Stowe and an affiliate of the Blackstone Group are controlling\nshareholders of Centennial.
