The Company reported net income of $18.0\nmillion, or $.44 per diluted share, for the year ended June\xa030, 2004, compared\nto net income of $8.8\xa0million, or $.22 per diluted share, for the year ended\nJune\xa030, 2003.\n\n\n', u'\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0The resort operations are generally seasonal. The resorts historically\nexperience greater revenue, costs and income in the third and fourth quarters\nof the fiscal year ended June\xa030 due to increased occupancy and room rates\nduring the winter and spring months. Historically, 16%, 23%, 36% and 25% of\nannual revenue has been derived during the first, second, third and fourth\nfiscal quarters, respectively. Additional details relating to the comparative\noperating results for the three and twelve months ended June\xa030 are set forth\nbelow.\n\n\n', u'COMPARATIVE RESULTS OF OPERATIONS', u'\n', u'Revenue', u'\n', u'\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0Leisure and recreation revenue totaled $80.3\xa0million and $76.6\xa0million for\nthe three months ended June\xa030, 2004 and 2003, respectively, and $313.3\xa0million\nand $290.2\xa0million for the year ended June\xa030, 2004 and 2003, respectively.\nThe increase in revenue for the three and twelve months ended June\xa030, 2004,\ncompared to the three and twelve months ended June\xa030, 2003, resulted primarily\nfrom increases in occupancy at each of the Company\x92s properties as well as\nincreased revenue derived from non-room sources such as food and beverage\nrevenue, yachting and marina revenue, and spa revenue. Management believes\nincreases in resort occupancy are largely the result of an improving economic\nlandscape, significant property enhancements, the high quality of the Company\x92s\nassets, a commitment to quality customer service resulting in strong guest\nloyalty and a favorable market mix that combines high-end leisure travelers and\ncorporate groups.\n\n\n', u'Operating Expenses', u'\n', u'\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0Cost of leisure and recreation services totaled $35.6\xa0million, or 44% of\nrevenue, for the three months ended June\xa030, 2004, compared to cost of leisure\nand recreation services of $34.4\xa0million, or 45% of revenue, for the three\nmonths ended June\xa030, 2003. Cost of leisure and recreation services totaled\n$137.4\xa0million, or 44% of revenue, for the year ended June\xa030, 2004, compared\nto cost of leisure and recreation services of $130.3\xa0million, or 45% of\nrevenue, for the year ended June\xa030, 2003. Cost of leisure and recreation\nservices primarily consisted of direct costs to service rooms, marinas, food\nand beverage operations, retail establishments, spas and other amenities at the\nresorts.\n\n\n', u'\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0Selling, general and administrative expenses (\x93S,G&A\x94) totaled $20.6\nmillion, or 26% of revenue, for the three months ended June\xa030, 2004, compared\nto S,G&A of $22.0\xa0million, or 29% of revenue, for the three months ended June\n30, 2003. S,G&A totaled $85.8\xa0million, or 27% of revenue, for the year ended\nJune\xa030, 2004, compared to S,G&A of $86.0\xa0million, or 30% of revenue, for the\nyear ended June\xa030, 2003. S,G&A includes, among other items, administrative\npayroll costs, selling and marketing expenses, energy\n\n\n', u'4\n', u'\n', u' PAGEBREAK ', u'\n', u'\n', u'\xa0', u'\n', u'\n', u'and property costs, insurance, real estate taxes, franchise agreement fees\nand other administrative expenses. The increase\nin depreciation expense for the year ended June\xa030, 2004, compared to the year\nended June\xa030, 2003, was primarily the result of an increase in depreciation\nexpense following the completion of room renovations at the Registry Resort and\nEdgewater Beach Hotel (which were completed midway through the prior fiscal\nyear) and a marina renovation at the Bahia Mar Resort & Yachting Center (which\nwas completed in October\xa02003).\n\n\n', u'Interest Expense', u'\n', u'\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0\xa0Interest expense totaled $5.2\xa0million for both the three months ended June\n30, 2004 and 2003.
