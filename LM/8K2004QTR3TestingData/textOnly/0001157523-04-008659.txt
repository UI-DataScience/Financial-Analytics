Announces Fiscal 2004 Annual and Fourth Quarter\nResults\n\n     ATLANTA--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Sept. 21, 2004--SunLink Health Systems, Inc.\n(AMEX: SSY) today announced net earnings of $14,305,000 or $2.03 per fully\ndiluted share for the quarter ended June 30, 2004, compared to net earnings of\n$461,000 or $0.09 per fully diluted share for the quarter ended June 30, 2003.\nFor the year ended June 30, 2004, SunLink reported net earnings of $13,426,000\nor $2.15 per fully diluted share compared to net earnings of $553,000 or $0.10\nper fully diluted share for the year ended June 30, 2003. An after-tax gain\nincluded in discontinued operations of approximately $16,366,000 on the sale of\nSunLink\'s Mountainside Medical Center is included in the results for the quarter\nand year ended June 30, 2004.\n     SunLink reported a loss from continuing operations for its fourth fiscal\nquarter ended June 30, 2004 of $1,087,000 or a loss of $0.15 per fully diluted\nshare compared to earnings of $849,000 or $0.16 per fully diluted share for the\nquarter ended June 30, 2003. For the year ended June 30, 2004, SunLink reported\na loss from continuing operations of $1,267,000 or a loss of $0.20 per fully\ndiluted share compared to earnings from continuing operations of $1,560,000 or\n$0.29 per share for the year ended June 30, 2003. Included in the loss from\ncontinuing operations for the quarter and year ended June 30, 2004 is $1,904,000\nof loss related to the early repayment of debt.\n     The Company\'s operating profit from continuing operations for the quarter\nended June 30, 2004 was $909,000 (which includes $397,000 from the HealthMont\nfacilities which were acquired in October 2003) compared to an operating profit\nfor the quarter ended June 30, 2003 of $1,317,000. Operating profit for the\nquarter ended June 30, 2003 included $1,026,000 for positive prior year\nthird-party payor settlements. The quarter ended June 30, 2004 had no prior year\nthird-party payor settlements. The operating profit for the year ended June 30,\n2004 was $4,315,000 compared to $4,702,000 for the year ended June 30, 2003. The\noperating profit of the HealthMont facilities for the year ended June 30, 2004\nwas $53,000.\n     Consolidated net revenues from continuing operations for the quarters ended\nJune 30, 2004 and 2003 were $31,000,000 and $21,289,000, respectively, an\nincrease of 45.6% in the current year\'s quarter. The increased net revenues\nresulted from a 21.3% increase in same store equivalent admissions, a 19.7%\nincrease in same store surgeries, an 11.6% increase in same store admissions and\nthe net revenues from the two HealthMont hospitals acquired in October 2003. Net\nrevenues excluding the two newly acquired HealthMont hospitals increased\n$2,066,000 or 9.7% in the current year\'s quarter compared to the prior year\nfourth quarter.\n     Consolidated net revenues from continuing operations for the fiscal years\nended June 30, 2004 and 2003 were $112,436,000 and $80,742,000, respectively, an\nincrease of 39.3% in the current year. The increased net revenues resulted\nprimarily from the two HealthMont hospitals acquired in October 2003, a 15.4%\nincrease in same store equivalent admissions, a 14.1% increase in same store\nadmissions and a 6.1% increase in same store surgeries. Net revenues excluding\nthe two newly acquired HealthMont hospitals increased $9,938,000 or 12.3% in the\ncurrent year compared to the prior year.\n     In the year ended June 30, 2004, earnings from discontinued operations of\n$14,693,000 resulted primarily from the gain on the sale of Mountainside Medical\nCenter in June 2004. In the year ended June 30, 2003, the Company reported a\nloss from discontinued operations of $1,007,000 which included a $1,562,000\nasset impairment charge related to Mountainside Medical Center.\n     Robert M. Thornton, chief executive officer of SunLink, stated, "By\nmaintaining our focus on SunLink\'s core competency in exurban and rural\ncommunities, our hospitals have generated strong growth. This has been\ncomplemented by the HealthMont acquisition and our ability to attract and retain\nan exceptional group of physicians and support staff at all of our facilities.\nOver the past 24 months, the company added 45 net additional physicians to the\nmedical staffs of its hospitals (including five additional physicians in the\nmost recently completed quarter) who contributed to much of the increased\nvolume." Thornton continued, "We continue to be committed to organic growth and\nwill continue to seek new opportunities to increase our hospital portfolio\nthrough a disciplined acquisition program."\n     SunLink Health Systems, Inc. currently operates seven community hospitals\nand related businesses in the Southeast and Midwest. Each SunLink facility is\nthe only hospital in its community. SunLink\'s operating strategy is to link\npatients\' needs with dedicated physicians and health professionals to deliver\nquality, efficient medical care in each community it serves.
