(Nasdaq:RHAT), the world\'s leading provider of open source\nsolutions to the enterprise, today announced financial results for the second\nquarter ended August 31, 2004.\n     Revenue for the second quarter of fiscal 2005 was $46.3 million, 60% higher\nthan the second quarter of fiscal 2004 and a sequential increase of 11% when\ncompared to $41.8 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2005.\n     For the second quarter of fiscal 2005, the company reported net income of\n$11.8 million, or $0.06 per share. This represents an increase of 225% over net\nincome in the same quarter a year ago, and an increase of 8% over net income of\n$10.9 million, or $0.06 per share, in the prior quarter.\n     The company generated $30.8 million, or $0.16 per share, in cash flow from\noperations during the second quarter of fiscal 2005. The company ended the\nquarter with cash and investments totaling $997.7 million.\n     In the second quarter of fiscal 2005, the company increased its deferred\nrevenue balance to $99.7 million, a sequential increase of $11 million, or 12%,\nas compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2005.\n\n     Highlights for the second quarter include:\n\n   Sales of subscriptions of Red Hat Enterprise Linux reached 144,000\n   units, including:\n     115,000 subscriptions to enterprise IT servers, which includes\n      new subscriptions and renewals.\n     29,000 new subscriptions to HPC/hosting marketplace and desktops.\n   Operating profit increased to $6.7 million, or 15% of total\n   revenue.\n   10% addition to the workforce as the company expands globally.\n\n     "We continue to see strong demand for our offerings, as reflected in our\nresults. The company executed successfully across the global business", stated\nCharlie Peters, Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer.\n\n     About Red Hat, Inc.\n\n     Red Hat, the world\'s leading open source and Linux provider. Red Hat\nalso offers support, training, and consulting services to its customers\nworldwide and through top-tier partnerships.
