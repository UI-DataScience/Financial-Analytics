Reports Fiscal 2004 Results\n\nIrvine,  CA,  September  9,  2004  - Irvine, CA - Lantronix, Inc. (NASDAQ: LTRX)\ntoday  reported  results  from its continuing operations for its fiscal year and\nfourth  quarter  ended  June  30,  2004:\n\n- -     Revenues  for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2004 were $48.9 million, flat\nwith  the  same  period  last  year  when  the company recorded $49.4 million in\nrevenues.\n\n- -     Net  loss  was reduced to $(15.7) million, or $(0.28) per share for fiscal\n2004,  compared  with  net loss of $(47.5) million, or $(0.88) per share for the\nprevious  fiscal  year.  Results  for fiscal 2004 included a $5.0 million charge\nrelated  to  the  company\'s  remaining  investment  in  Xanboo,  an  early stage\ntechnology  company.\n\n- -     Operating  loss  was  reduced  to $(5.6) million for fiscal 2004, compared\nwith  operating  loss  of  $(36.5)  million  for  fiscal  2003.\n\n- -     Revenue  from  the company\'s core product lines was up 14% for the quarter\nended  June  2004  compared  to  the  same  period  last  year.\n\n- -     Revenue  from  the  Device  Networking products was up 31% for the quarter\nended  June  30,  2004  compared  to  the  same  period  last  year.\n\nFor  the fiscal year ended June 30, 2004, the company reported revenues of $48.9\nmillion  and  net  loss from continuing operations of $(5.6) million, or $(0.19)\nper  share, compared with revenues of $49.4 million and net loss from continuing\noperations  of  $(37.4)  million,  or $(0.69) per share for the same period last\nyear.\n\nNet loss from discontinued operations of the company\'s Premise subsidiary, which\nwas  sold  during  the  third fiscal quarter, was $(5.0) million, or $(0.09) per\nshare, and $(10.1) million, or $(0.19) per share for the fiscal years ended June\n30,  2004,  and  2003,  respectively.\n\nFor  the  fourth fiscal quarter ended June 30, 2004, Lantronix recorded revenues\nof  $11.9  million and net loss from continuing operations of $(6.9) million, or\n$(0.12)  per  share, compared with revenues of $11.8 million and a net loss from\ncontinuing  operations  of  $(13.0)  million, or $(0.24) per share, for the same\nperiod last year.   Results for the quarter ended June 30, 2004, included a $5.0\nmillion  charge for the write-off of a long-term asset, related to the company\'s\ninvestment  in Xanboo, an early stage technology company, compared to impairment\nand  restructuring  charges  of  $3.0  million  for  the  same period last year.\n\nNet income from discontinued operations was $138,000, or $0.00 per share for the\nquarter  ended  June  30,  2004,  compared with a net loss of $(6.1) million, or\n$(0.11)  per  share  for  the  quarter  ended  June  30,  2003.\n\nNet  loss  was  $(6.8)  million,  or  $(0.12) per share, and $(19.1) million, or\n$(0.35)  per share for the quarters ended June 30, 2004, and 2003, respectively.\n\nCash,  cash  equivalents  and  marketable  securities  decreased  approximately\n$685,000  during  the  quarter  ended June 30, 2004, compared with a decrease of\n$459,000  for  the  same  period  a year earlier, and in line with the company\'s\nearlier guidance of cash usage in the range of $1 million. The company indicated\nthat  it  would  have  cash  usage  in the range of $2.2 to $2.8 million for the\ncurrent  quarter  ending September 30, 2004, as a result of the payment of notes\nincurred  in  connection  with  the  Stallion acquisition two years earlier, tax\naudit payments, and higher expenses for the quarter related to the launch of new\nproducts.  Cash  usage  is  anticipated to return to the range of $1 million per\nquarter  in  the second fiscal quarter ending December 31, 2004, and the company\naffirmed  its  current  cash flow breakeven to be in the revenue range of $14 to\n$15  million  per  quarter.\n\n"We  experienced  flat overall revenue for the recently completed year, however,\nthis result masks a significant shift in sales towards our core product lines of\nDevice  Networking  and IT Management, and away from legacy non-core offerings,"\nstated  Marc  Nussbaum,  president  and  CEO.
