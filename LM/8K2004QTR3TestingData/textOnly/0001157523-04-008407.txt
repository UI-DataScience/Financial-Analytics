Reports Third Quarter Fiscal 2004 Financial\nResults\n\n    NEW YORK--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Sept. 9, 2004--Take-Two Interactive\nSoftware, Inc. (NASDAQ:TTWO) today announced financial results for its\nthird quarter and nine months ended July 31, 2004.\n    Net sales for the third quarter ended July 31, 2004 were $160.9\nmillion, compared to $152.1 million for last year\'s third quarter. Net\nloss for the quarter was $14.4 million, compared to net income of $5.7\nmillion last year, with a net loss of $0.32 per share compared to net\nincome per share of $0.13 last year.\n    Net sales for the nine months ended July 31, 2004 were $689.7\nmillion, compared to $756.1 million for the same period a year ago, a\nperiod which included significant sales of the blockbuster title Grand\nTheft Auto: Vice City for PlayStation(R)2 and the then newly released\ntitles Midnight Club 2 for PlayStation 2 and Grand Theft Auto: Vice\nCity for PC. Net income of $2.7 million compared to $71.9 million in\nthe comparable period last year, with diluted net income per share of\n$0.06 compared to $1.68.\n\n    Guidance\n\n    Take-Two is updating its guidance for fiscal 2004 as follows:\n\n    --  For the fiscal year ending October 31, 2004, Take-Two\'s\n        guidance is $1.145 to $1.160 billion in net sales and $1.60 to\n        $1.62 in diluted net income per share.\n\n    Take-Two is issuing initial guidance for fiscal 2005 as follows:\n\n    --  For the fiscal year ending October 31, 2005, $1.2 to $1.3\n        billion in net sales and $2.00 to $2.20 in diluted net income\n        per share.\n\n    --  For the first quarter ending January 31, 2005, $420 to $460\n        million in net sales and $1.00 to $1.10 in diluted net income\n        per share.\n\n    Liquidity and Selected Balance Sheet Items\n\n    Take-Two used approximately $60 million in cash for operating\nactivities in the quarter. Working capital was approximately $351\nmillion at July 31, 2004, as compared with approximately $296 million\nat the same time a year ago. The Company had approximately $198\nmillion in cash as of July 31, 2004, as compared to $226 million in\ncash at July 31, 2003.\n\n    Publishing Highlights - Third Quarter\n\n    Rockstar\'s new title Red Dead Revolver, released in late April for\nPlayStation 2 and Xbox, was a significant contributor to third quarter\nresults. Designed by Rockstar San Diego, the developers of Rockstar\nGames\' successful Midnight Club and Smuggler\'s Run franchises, Red\nDead Revolver was the top-performing product in Take-Two\'s publishing\nbusiness this quarter and was a top ten title in the United States in\nMay, June and July according to NPDFunworld(SM). Rockstar\'s Grand\nTheft Auto: Vice City was released in Japan for the PlayStation 2 and\nPC in late May, under a license agreement with Capcom Co., Ltd.\nAccording to Media Create Co., Ltd., a leading market research firm\ncovering the Japanese console gaming market, the PlayStation 2 title\nhas sold over 400,000 copies as of the week ended August 29, 2004,\nmaking it one of the most successful debuts of a Western video game in\nJapan.\n    Rockstar also launched the PlayStation 2 Greatest Hits versions of\nMidnight Club 2 and Grand Theft Auto: Vice City, bringing Take-Two\'s\ncatalog of Greatest Hits products for PlayStation 2 to eight titles.\n    Global Star launched ESPN NFL 2K5 for both the PlayStation 2 and\nXbox in North America at the end of July, the first title released\nunder the co-publishing and exclusive distribution agreement with Sega\nCorporation for the ESPN Videogames sports titles. Global Star also\nshipped Army Men: Sarge\'s War and Dragon Tales.\n    To streamline Take-Two\'s publishing operations, the Company is\nreorganizing its publishing business into two labels: Rockstar Games\nand Global Star Software. Rockstar will continue to focus on premium\npriced, groundbreaking entertainment, while Global Star\'s portfolio\nwill be significantly broadened. In addition to publishing the ESPN\nVideogames sports line and all value priced titles, Global Star will\npublish both premium and value priced licensed properties, as well as\nthe products that were formerly under the Company\'s Gathering label.\n\n    Publishing Highlights - Fourth Quarter\n\n    Take-Two has an extensive product lineup planned this holiday\nseason, beginning in the fourth quarter. Rockstar will introduce Grand\nTheft Auto: San Andreas, the next iteration in the globally successful\nfranchise which has sold over 32 million units. Developed by\nworld-class designers Rockstar North, Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas\nwill be available exclusively for the PlayStation 2 and is expected to\nbe in stores in North America on October 26, 2004 and in Europe on\nOctober 29, 2004. The title\'s launch date was moved to allow\nadditional time for final testing of the game, which exceeds the size,\ndepth and scope of traditional videogames. Rockstar has been\nextensively previewing the game to the trade press, and the title has\nappeared on the cover of several key trade publications since it was\nrevealed to the public at this year\'s E3 industry tradeshow. Rockstar\nalso plans to release an extension of the Grand Theft Auto franchise\nfor the Game Boy Advance in late October.\n    Global Star\'s fourth quarter line-up leads with ESPN NHL 2K5 for\nboth the PlayStation 2 and Xbox, which is in North American retail\nstores now. Later this quarter Global Star will release ESPN NBA 2K5,\nalso for PlayStation 2 and Xbox. Other fourth quarter releases include\nConflict: Vietnam, the next game in the successful Conflict series, to\nbe distributed in North America on the PlayStation 2, Xbox and PC;\nKohan II: Kings of War for PC; Robotech: Invasion and Outlaw Golf 2\nfor PlayStation 2 and Xbox; and a Game Boy Advance title based on\nCartoon Network\'s hit series Codename: Kids Next Door. Gathering\'s\nproduct lineup for the fourth quarter includes Wings of War for the\nXbox and PC; and The Guy Game for PlayStation 2 and Xbox, which are\nall in retail stores now; and Vietcong: Purple Haze for PlayStation 2\nand Xbox.\n\n    Future Publishing Highlights\n\n    Midnight Club 3: DUB Edition, the third installment in the\nmulti-million unit selling, genre-defining Midnight Club racing\nfranchise developed by Rockstar San Diego, is planned for release on\nPlayStation 2 and Xbox in January 2005. The Warriors(TM), a new title\nbased on the Paramount Pictures feature film, is scheduled for launch\non PlayStation 2 in April 2005. Additional products planned for fiscal\n2005 from Rockstar Games include Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas for PC\nin the third quarter, as well as two new console titles based on\nproven brands from Rockstar\'s internal studios, in the third and\nfourth quarters. Rockstar also plans to release Grand Theft Auto: San\nAndreas in Japan in fiscal 2005.\n    Global Star\'s fiscal 2005 lineup includes the full line of ESPN\nVideogames sports titles, beginning with ESPN College Hoops in the\nfirst quarter, followed by ESPN Major League Baseball in the second\nquarter.\n    As recently announced, Global Star will be introducing videogame\nand PC titles based on the Warner Bros. Pictures film, Charlie and the\nChocolate Factory, directed by Tim Burton and starring Oscar nominated\nactor Johnny Depp as Willy Wonka. The launch of the videogames is\nscheduled to coincide with the Summer 2005 release of the feature\nfilm.\n    Other products planned for fiscal 2005 release by Global Star\ninclude Close Combat: First to Fight for Xbox and PC; Dora The\nExplorer: Super Star Adventures for Game Boy Advance; Stronghold 2 for\nPC; Ford Mustang: 40th Anniversary Edition for PlayStation 2 and Xbox;\na deluxe version of Outlaw Volleyball for the PlayStation 2 featuring\nnew content and enhancements to the franchise; and console versions of\nCodename: Kids Next Door, Spy vs. Spy, Motocross Mania 3 and Outlaw\nTennis.\n\n    Distribution Highlights\n\n    The Company\'s Jack of All Games subsidiary, the leading North\nAmerican distributor of videogame software, continues to capitalize on\nthe growing installed base of hardware and proliferation of software\ntitles and outlets to purchase software. Jack emphasizes sales of both\nexclusive and non-exclusive mid- and budget-priced software and serves\nas the exclusive distributor for all of Global Star\'s value-priced\nproducts.\n\n    Management\n\n    During the quarter, Take-Two added two new executives to its\nmanagement team and appointed a new member to its Board of Directors.\nSam Judd joined as Senior Vice President, Planning and Administration,\nwhere he oversees business planning, strategy development and\ninformation technology. Mr. Judd spent the past 11 years at Viacom\nInc., most recently leading the finance, customer service, warehouse,\nmanufacturing and supply chain functions for the Simon & Schuster\ndivision, a global publishing business. Mr. Judd\'s prior experience\nincludes finance and strategy consulting for a variety of clients,\nseveral years at PepsiCo Inc. in corporate and divisional financial\npositions and finance roles with IBM.\n    David Messenger joined as Senior Vice President, Human Resources.\nPrior to joining Take-Two, Mr. Messenger was leading Towers Perrin\'s\nchange management practice. He has extensive experience in human\nresources strategy and operations, compensation and performance\nmanagement, mergers and acquisitions, organization design and\nleadership development. Mr. Messenger\'s clients included Universal\nStudios, Universal Music, Sony Corporation and Sony Music.\n    As previously announced, Barbara A. Kaczynski was appointed as a\ndirector of the Company in July. Ms. Kaczynski chairs Take-Two\'s Audit\nCommittee and is considered a financial expert as defined by the\nSecurities and Exchange Commission. Ms. Kaczynski was most recently\nChief Financial Officer of the National Football League (NFL), where\nshe directed the operations of the finance, treasury, budgeting,\nplanning, internal audit and information technology organizations of\nthe NFL. Prior to the NFL, Ms. Kaczynski held several senior financial\npositions at various companies, including the position of Vice\nPresident of Finance and Controller for Time, Inc., where she spent\nalmost 10 years.\n\n    Management Comments\n\n    Richard W. Roedel, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer,\ncommented, "We are pleased with the progress we have made in building\nand diversifying our product portfolio through the introduction of\nseveral new Rockstar brands and the launch of the ESPN sports titles.\nOur product pipeline going into 2005, which includes titles based on\nproven franchises, new brands and licensed properties, is one of the\nstrongest in the Company\'s history. Combined with the expansion of our\nmanagement team and the strengthening of our internal operations, we\nbelieve we are building the foundation for long term, sustained\ngrowth. As a result, we are confident that the continued execution of\nour business strategy will enable Take-Two to maintain a leadership\nposition in the interactive entertainment industry and lead to an even\nstronger financial position going forward."\n\n    Conference Call\n\n    Take-Two will host a conference call today at 4:30 pm Eastern Time\nto review its results for the third quarter and to discuss its\noutlook. The Company publishes and develops products through its\nwholly owned labels Rockstar Games and Global Star Software; and\ndistributes products in North America through its Jack of All Games\nsubsidiary. Take-Two also manufactures and markets video game\naccessories in Europe, North America and the Asia Pacific region\nthrough its Joytech subsidiary. The Company maintains sales and\nmarketing offices in Cincinnati, New York, Toronto, London, Paris,\nMunich, Madrid, Vienna, Milan, Sydney, Breda (Netherlands) and\nAuckland. Take-Two\'s common stock is publicly traded on NASDAQ under\nthe symbol TTWO.
