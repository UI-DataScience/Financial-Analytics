<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>PAYCHEX, INC. REPORTS RECORD FIRST QUARTER RESULTS</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ROCHESTER, NY, September&nbsp;21, 2004 &#151; Paychex, Inc. (NASDAQ:PAYX) today
announced record net income of $87.7&nbsp;million, or $.23 diluted earnings per
share, for the quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, a 9% increase over net income of
$80.3&nbsp;million, or $.21 diluted earnings per share, for the first quarter last
year. Total revenues were $345.0&nbsp;million, a 12% increase over $309.3&nbsp;million
for the prior year first quarter.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>SERVICE REVENUES</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, service revenues, which include the
Payroll and Human Resource and Benefits product lines, were $334.2&nbsp;million, an
increase of 13% over $295.9&nbsp;million for the prior year first quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Payroll service revenue increased 10% in the first quarter of fiscal 2005 to
$280.4&nbsp;million. Positive year-over-year growth in Payroll service revenue
resulted from organic client base growth, increased utilization of ancillary
services, and price increases. As of August&nbsp;31, 2004, 89% of all clients
utilized the Company&#146;s tax filing and payment services and 63% utilized
employee payment services. Major Market Services revenue increased 30% for the
first quarter to $41.0&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Human Resource and Benefits service revenue increased to $53.8&nbsp;million for the
first quarter of fiscal 2005, a year-over-year increase of 30%. The increase
reflects growth in clients for Retirement Services, growth in client employees
served by the Company&#146;s comprehensive Paychex Administrative Services (PAS)&nbsp;and
Professional Employer Organization (PEO)&nbsp;bundled services, and the benefit of
revenue from the April&nbsp;2004 acquisition of Stromberg Time and Attendance
products. Retirement Services revenue increased 17% year-over-year in the
first quarter to $20.9&nbsp;million. Administrative fee revenue from PAS and PEO
products increased 48% in the first quarter to $16.0&nbsp;million.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>INTEREST ON FUNDS HELD FOR CLIENTS</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Interest on funds held for clients decreased 19% in the first quarter of fiscal
2005 to $10.8&nbsp;million, which was consistent with the Company&#146;s expectations.
The decrease was attributable to lower net realized gains on the sale of
available-for-sale securities and lower average interest rates earned,
partially offset by higher average portfolio balances resulting from client
base growth. Average portfolio balances for the first quarter of fiscal 2005
were $2.5&nbsp;billion compared with $2.3&nbsp;billion in the prior year quarter. The
average interest rates earned by the funds held for clients portfolio were 1.7%
for the first quarter of fiscal 2005, compared with 1.9% for the first quarter
of fiscal 2004. Net realized gains included in interest on funds held for
clients were $.2&nbsp;million for the first quarter of fiscal 2005, compared with net
realized gains of $2.7&nbsp;million for the prior year period.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>CONSOLIDATED EXPENSES AND OPERATING INCOME</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Consolidated operating, selling, general, and administrative expenses increased
11% in the first quarter over the prior year quarter. The increase is due to
increases in personnel, information technology, and other costs to support the
organic growth of the Company. Higher professional services expenses related
to pending legal matters also contributed to the increase in the first quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, operating income was $128.7&nbsp;million, an
increase of 12% over the same period last year. Operating income growth
continues to be impacted by the lower average interest rates earned by the
funds held for clients portfolio. Operating income (excluding interest on
funds held for clients) increased 16% year-over-year in the first quarter to
$117.9&nbsp;million. Operating income (excluding interest on funds held for
clients) as a percentage of

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>INVESTMENT INCOME, NET</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Investment income, net, decreased 43% year-over-year in the first quarter to
$2.3&nbsp;million, which was consistent with the Company&#146;s expectations. The
decrease is due to lower net realized gains on the sale of available-for-sale
securities and lower average interest rates earned, offset somewhat by higher
average portfolio balances. Average portfolio balances for the corporate
investment portfolio were approximately $529&nbsp;million for the first quarter of
fiscal 2005, compared with $386&nbsp;million in the prior year period. The average
interest rates earned for the corporate investment portfolio were 1.8% for the
first quarter of fiscal 2005, compared with 2.8% in the prior year period.
There were no net realized gains included in investment income in the first
quarter of fiscal 2005, compared with $1.5&nbsp;million in the prior year period.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>INCOME TAXES</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The effective income tax rate was 33.0% for the quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2004,
compared with 32.5% for the same period last year. The increase in the
effective tax rate is the result of lower levels of tax-exempt income on funds
held for clients and corporate investments.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">B. Thomas Golisano, Chairman, President, and Chief Executive Officer of
Paychex, said, &#147;Fiscal 2005 is off to a good start as our financial results for
the first quarter were in line with our expectations. We remain focused on
growing our client base, increasing utilization of our ancillary services, and
improving our profit margins. Estimated total revenue growth for fiscal 2005 is
expected to be in the range of 9% to 11%, accompanied by net income growth in
the range of 16% to 18%. In addition, we expect that growth in operating
income, excluding interest on funds held for clients, expense charges in fiscal
2004 for pending legal matters, and the net incremental PEO revenue benefit
recognized in fiscal 2004, to be in excess of 15%. These expectations are
based on current economic and interest rate conditions continuing with no
significant changes.&#148;



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><U>ABOUT PAYCHEX</U></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Paychex, Inc. is a leading national provider of payroll, human resource, and
benefits outsourcing solutions for small- to medium-sized businesses. The
company offers comprehensive payroll services, including payroll processing,
payroll tax administration, and employee pay services, including direct
deposit, check signing, and Readychex&#174;. Human resource and benefits outsourcing
services include 401(k) plan recordkeeping, workers&#146; compensation
administration, section 125 plans, a professional employer organization, and
other administrative services for business. Paychex was founded in 1971. With
headquarters in Rochester, New York, the company has more than 100 offices and
serves approximately 505,000 payroll clients nationwide.
