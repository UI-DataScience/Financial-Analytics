<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">BLYTH, INC. REPORTS RECORD 2<sup>nd</sup>
QUARTER SALES<br>
Net Sales Increase 5%; Soft North American Wholesale Market<br>
Negatively Impacts Profits</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">GREENWICH, CT, USA,
August&nbsp;25, 2004: Blyth, Inc. (NYSE:BTH), a leader in home d&#233;cor and home
fragrance products, today reported that Net Sales for the second quarter ended
July&nbsp;31, 2004 increased approximately 5% to $289.1 million compared with
$275.3 million a year earlier.&#160;
Operating Profit for the quarter declined to $19.0 million compared to
$20.7 million for the prior year period.&#160;
Net Earnings for the quarter were $9.4 million versus $11.2 million a
year earlier.&#160; Diluted Net Earnings Per
Share for the quarter were $0.21 per share compared to $0.24 for the same
period last year.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Net sales for the six
months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 increased approximately 10% to $648.0 million
from $587.3 million reported for the same period a year ago.&#160; Operating profit for the six months declined
approximately 3% to $53.3 million versus $55.0 million for the same period last
year.&#160; Net earnings were $27.1 million,
compared to $30.9 million for the prior year period.&#160; Diluted Net Earnings Per Share were $0.59 compared to $0.67 for
last year&#146;s first half.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Second quarter Net Sales
increased approximately 5% over last year primarily due to the additions of
Kaemingk, acquired late in the second quarter of last year, and Walter Drake,
acquired in December&nbsp;of last year, and to increased sales in the Direct
Selling segment, which together more than offset a decline in the Wholesale
segment.&#160; The decline in the second
quarter operating margin primarily relates to a sales shortfall in the North
American Wholesale business.&#160; This
shortfall is a result of retailer caution and, specifically, their reluctance
to commit to holiday product orders, which typically begin shipping in the
second quarter.&#160; The Company&#146;s operating
margin was also negatively impacted by increased interest expense due to the
Company&#146;s October&nbsp;2003 bond offering and expenses related to compliance
with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act.&#160; The
decline also reflects the negative impact of the seasonality of the Walter
Drake acquisition, the impact of increased raw material prices that primarily
affected Blyth&#146;s North American Wholesale business, and ongoing investments in
several organic strategic initiatives.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Robert B. Goergen,
Blyth&#146;s Chairman of the Board and CEO, commented, &#147;The North American wholesale
environment has deteriorated materially since the beginning of the year.&#160; The significant decline in holiday shipments
during the second quarter versus our historical norm highlights the reduced
confidence levels in the retail industry as a whole.&#160; Our</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>


<div style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">

<hr size="2" width="100%" noshade color="gray" align="left">

</font></div>

<font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">

<br clear="all" style="page-break-before:always;">
</font>

<p align="center" style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">European wholesale
business fared much better. However, with the additional obstacle of energy
price increases since the beginning of the year, growth in the Wholesale
segment will be difficult to achieve in fiscal 2005.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In the Wholesale segment,
second quarter net sales decreased approximately 10% to $103.3 million,
principally due to weak ordering volume across the entire North American
portion of the Wholesale segment and the divestiture of Jeanmarie Creations,
Blyth&#146;s decorative gift bag and gift tag business, which was sold in
April&nbsp;2004.&#160; These decreases more
than offset the benefit of nearly a full quarter of sales from Kaemingk.&#160; Second quarter operating loss of $4.3
million in the Wholesale segment increased $2.7 million compared to last year&#146;s
second quarter loss of $1.6 million and reflects both the sales shortfall and
increased raw material costs.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mr. Goergen continued,
&#147;In the Direct Selling segment, however, PartyLite&#146;s European markets continue
to experience double-digit growth, showing no slow down in the momentum built
last year and in the first quarter.&#160; In
the U. S market, PartyLite held its own despite consumers&#146; diminishing
discretionary income due to ever-increasing gasoline prices.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In
the Direct Selling segment, second quarter net sales increased approximately 5%
to $150.3 million as a result of continued strength in the European sales of
PartyLite&#174; brand products, which increased approximately 24%.&#160; PartyLite&#146;s U.S. sales were approximately
flat compared to last year.&#160; Second
quarter operating profit in the Direct Selling segment increased approximately
5% to $25.1 million from $23.8 million in the same period last year.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In the Catalog &amp;
Internet segment, second quarter net sales increased to $35.4 million,
attributable to the acquisition of Walter Drake.&#160; This segment experienced an operating loss of $1.8 million,
reflecting the seasonal nature of its business and costs associated with the
integration of Walter Drake&#146;s operations into those of Miles Kimball, which is
nearly complete.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Commenting on the Catalog
&amp; Internet segment, Mr. Goergen said, &#147;The Catalog &amp; Internet segment
is our newest distribution channel, and we are pleased that the business
remains on track to carry out its financial plan for the full year.&#160; We have great confidence in the management
team and are encouraged by the smooth integration of Walter Drake into the
operations of Miles Kimball.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The sum of the segment
amounts does not necessarily equal that reported for the quarter for Blyth
overall due to rounding.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Management today updated
its expectations for fiscal year 2005 Earnings Per Share.&#160; After giving effect to the impact of the
Dutch tender offer completed in July&nbsp;2004, EPS will be in the range of
$2.30 to $2.40.&#160; Cash flow from
operations is anticipated to be approximately $130 million for the full fiscal
year.&#160; The new guidance reflects the
softness in the North American Wholesale business, partially offset by the
favorable accretion from the recent Dutch tender offer.&#160; Also related to the Dutch tender offer and
the Company&#146;s full year EPS guidance, management noted that, under accounting
principles generally accepted in the U.S., quarterly computations of Earnings
Per Share must stand on their own, and therefore, the sum of the basic and
diluted Earnings Per Share for the four quarters of fiscal year 2005 may not
equal full-year basic and diluted Earnings Per Share.&#160; Basic and diluted Earnings Per Share</font></p>

<p align="left" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:left;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Blyth, Inc.,

primarily in the home fragrance, home d&#233;cor, seasonal decorations and gift
industry.&#160; The Company designs, markets
and distributes an extensive array of candles, home fragrance products,
decorative accessories, seasonal decorations and household convenience items,
as well as tabletop lighting and chafing fuel for the Away From Home or
foodservice trade.&#160; Blyth manufactures
most of its candles and sources nearly all of its other products.&#160; Its products are sold direct to the consumer
under the PartyLite&#174; brand, to retailers in the premium and specialty retail
