<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>CSK Auto Corporation Reports Fiscal 2004 Second Quarter Net Income Increases
28% Compared to Second Quarter of Fiscal 2003</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">PHOENIX, AZ, September&nbsp;2, 2004 &#151; CSK Auto Corporation (NYSE: CAO), the parent
company of CSK Auto, Inc., a specialty retailer in the automotive aftermarket,
today reported its financial results for the second quarter of fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Financial Results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Thirteen Weeks Ended August&nbsp;1, 2004


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net sales for the thirteen weeks ended August&nbsp;1, 2004 (the &#147;second quarter of
fiscal 2004&#148;) were $409.1&nbsp;million, compared to $418.5&nbsp;million for the thirteen
weeks ended August&nbsp;3, 2003 (the &#147;second quarter of fiscal 2003&#148;). Same store
sales decreased 2.5% in the second quarter of fiscal 2004 as compared to the
second quarter of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Gross profit was $191.3&nbsp;million, or 46.8% of net sales, in the second quarter
of fiscal 2004 as compared to $193.7&nbsp;million, or 46.3% of net sales, in the
second quarter of fiscal 2003. Gross profit, as a percent to sales, increased
over the second quarter of fiscal 2003 due to lower product acquisition costs
on selected items and our continued reduction in store inventory shrinkage as a
result of improved store procedures and enhanced inventory control systems.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Operating profit for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 decreased to $30.3
million compared to $35.2&nbsp;million for the second quarter of fiscal 2003. The
decrease in operating profit primarily relates to our decline in sales and
higher payroll rates and benefit-related expenses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Interest expense for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 decreased to $7.6
million from $13.3&nbsp;million in the second quarter of fiscal 2003 due to lower
interest expense achieved as a result of our January&nbsp;2004 refinancing.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net income for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 was $13.9&nbsp;million, or $0.30
per diluted share, compared to net income of $10.8&nbsp;million, or $0.24 per
diluted share, for the second quarter of fiscal 2003. Net income for the
second quarter of fiscal 2003 was negatively impacted by $4.3&nbsp;million of costs
related to debt retirement. On a comparable basis, net income was $13.9
million, or $0.30 per diluted share, for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 as
compared to $13.5&nbsp;million, or $0.30 per diluted share, for the second quarter
of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the second quarter of fiscal 2004, we repurchased approximately 1.3
million shares of our common stock for approximately $19.8&nbsp;million under our
previously announced $25&nbsp;million stock repurchase program. &#147;This is a
reflection of our Board&#146;s confidence in the Company&#146;s financial strength and
our overall commitment to our shareholders. We believe that the purchase of
shares of our common stock represents a highly effective use of our cash,&#148; said
Maynard Jenkins, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of CSK Auto Corporation.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are disappointed in our second quarter sales results,&#148; said Mr.&nbsp;Jenkins.
&#147;Since the beginning of the second quarter, we have experienced slower than
anticipated sales, particularly in our heat-related product categories. We
believe our sales have been negatively impacted by higher gas prices and milder
summer temperatures in many of our key markets.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">However, we remain positive about the strength and growth potential of the
retail automotive aftermarket industry. We plan to remain focused on our
long-term objectives of maximizing the productivity within our existing stores,
acceleration of our new store growth which will allow us to further leverage
our fixed expenses over an increasing store base, and debt reduction. Rather
than over-react to what we believe are short-term sales fluctuations, we plan
to continue to manage our business toward achievement of these longer-term
goals. We remain committed to our ongoing long-term merchandising and marketing
efforts and will continue to focus on successful implementation of our existing
strategies.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Twenty-six Weeks Ended August&nbsp;1, 2004


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net sales for the twenty-six weeks ended August&nbsp;1, 2004 (the &#147;first half of
fiscal 2004&#148;) were $806.1&nbsp;million, compared to $796.0&nbsp;million for the
twenty-six weeks ended August&nbsp;3, 2003 (the &#147;first half of fiscal 2003&#148;). Same
store sales increased 1.1% as compared to the first half of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Gross profit was $379.9&nbsp;million, or 47.1% of net sales, in the first half of
fiscal 2004 as compared to $368.7&nbsp;million, or 46.3% of net sales, in the first
half of fiscal 2003. We have continued to increase gross margin rates year
over year as a result of lower inventory acquisition costs and reduced store
inventory shrinkage as a result of improved store procedures and enhanced
inventory control systems.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Operating and administrative expenses were $319.2&nbsp;million in the first half of
fiscal 2004 as compared to $307.2&nbsp;million in the first half of fiscal 2003.
The increase in operating and administrative expenses is primarily the result
of our increased store count and higher payroll rates and benefit-related
expenses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Operating profit for the first half of fiscal 2004 totaled $59.8&nbsp;million, or
7.4% of net sales, compared to $61.4&nbsp;million, or 7.7% of net sales, for the
first half of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Interest expense for the first half of fiscal 2004 decreased to $15.8&nbsp;million
from $27.2&nbsp;million in the first half of fiscal 2003 due to lower interest
expense achieved as a result of our January&nbsp;2004 refinancing.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net income for the first half of fiscal 2004 was $26.8&nbsp;million, or $0.57 per
diluted share, compared to net income of $18.3&nbsp;million, or $0.41 per diluted
share, for the first half of fiscal 2003. As mentioned, net income for the
second quarter of fiscal 2003 was negatively impacted by $4.3&nbsp;million of costs
related to debt retirement. On a comparable basis, net income was $26.8
million, or $0.57 per diluted share, for the first half of fiscal 2004 as
compared to $21.0&nbsp;million, or $0.46 per diluted share, for the first half of
fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Outlook</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Based on current sales trends and in recognition of our 7.8% same store sales
increase in the second half of fiscal 2003, we would expect same store sales to
decline 2.0% to 3.5% during the third quarter and to be flat to negative 2% for
the fourth quarter. Assuming these results, we expect full year net income
between $52.0&nbsp;million and $56.0&nbsp;million (approximately $1.13 to $1.22 per
