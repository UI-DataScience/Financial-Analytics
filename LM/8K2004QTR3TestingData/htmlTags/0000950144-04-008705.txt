<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Piedmont Natural Gas Announces Third Quarter Results,<BR>
Stock Split, Regular Dividend, Reaffirms 2004 Guidance<BR>
and Initiates 2005 Guidance</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">CHARLOTTE, N.C., August&nbsp;27 /PRNewswire-FirstCall/ &#151; Charlotte-based Piedmont
Natural Gas (NYSE: PNY) announced results for its third fiscal quarter ended
July&nbsp;31, 2004. For the quarter, the Company reported a seasonal loss of $8.2
million and ($0.21) diluted loss per share, compared with a $9.7&nbsp;million loss
and a ($0.29) diluted loss per share for the same quarter in 2003. For the nine
months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, net income was $107.7&nbsp;million and diluted earnings
per share were $2.92, compared with $79.3&nbsp;million and $2.37 for the prior
nine-month period, respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">System throughput in the third quarter totaled 35.8&nbsp;million dekatherms,
compared with throughput of 17.6&nbsp;million dekatherms for the previous year&#146;s
quarter primarily due to the acquisition of North Carolina Natural Gas (NCNG)
on September&nbsp;30, 2003, and continued customer growth. Compared with the
historical Piedmont customer base, the NCNG customer base includes relatively
higher gas deliveries to lower margin industrial and power generation
customers.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Margin for the quarter totaled $69.7&nbsp;million compared with $49.3&nbsp;million for
the prior-year quarter. The margin increase is primarily attributable to the
NCNG acquisition, rate increases for the NCNG and Nashville Gas divisions that
became effective November&nbsp;1, 2003, and customer growth. Operations and
maintenance expenses increased from the prior-year quarter primarily due to the
acquisition of NCNG.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Other income, net of income taxes, was $3.1&nbsp;million for the quarter, compared
with $2.0&nbsp;million for the third quarter of the prior year. The increase is
primarily related to the avoidance of summer seasonal propane losses due to the
sale of the Company&#146;s propane interests during the first fiscal quarter of this
year. The Company&#146;s interest in SouthStar Energy Services LLC, which is
primarily engaged in the unregulated retail natural gas market in Georgia,
contributed $1.6&nbsp;million to net income in the third quarter compared with $1.5
million in the third quarter of the prior year, or $0.04 per share in both
quarters. Two other joint ventures, Pine Needle LNG and Cardinal Pipeline,
contributed combined earnings per share of $0.02 in the third quarters of 2004
and 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Our acquisition of NCNG is generating solid income growth for our
shareholders. We continue to enjoy good internal customer growth and focus on
our core natural gas distribution business while adding shareholder value with
attractive returns from complementary non-utility joint venture investments,&#148;
commented Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer Thomas E. Skains.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Stock Split and Dividend Declared</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Board of Directors today declared a 2-for-1 stock split of the Company&#146;s
common stock, subject to regulatory approval by the North Carolina Utilities
Commission. The stock split will be in the form of a 100% stock dividend to
shareholders of record at the close of business on October&nbsp;11, 2004. The
additional shares will be distributed beginning October&nbsp;29, 2004. The split
will not change the proportionate interest a shareholder has in the Company.
Upon completion of the split, the Company will have approximately 77&nbsp;million
shares outstanding.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;With individuals owning about 65% of our stock, we believe our trading range
after the split will make purchasing our stock even easier. We also would
expect the improved liquidity to help our institutional owners,&#148; commented Mr.
Skains.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fiscal 2004 Earnings Guidance Reaffirmed</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Piedmont Natural Gas reaffirms its fiscal year 2004 earnings guidance provided
on June&nbsp;4, 2004. The earnings from continuing operations is expected to be in
the range of $2.35 to $2.45 per diluted share on a pre-split basis. The fiscal
year 2004 earnings guidance on a post-split basis is $1.18 to $1.23 per diluted
share. Management continues to direct the earnings guidance towards the upper
end of the range.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The 2004 earnings guidance excludes non-recurring items related to the sale of
its propane interests and the resolution of certain disproportionate sharing
issues related to the SouthStar venture, both of which were reported in the
first fiscal quarter. The fiscal year 2004 earnings-per-share impacts of these
two items are expected to be increases of $0.08 and $0.04 on a pre-split basis,
and $0.04 and $0.02 on a post-split basis, respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The earnings guidance includes the earnings-per-share accretion related to the
NCNG acquisition, integration and permanent financing. Also included are
general rate increases for the NCNG and Nashville Gas divisions that became
effective November&nbsp;1, 2003, and management&#146;s assessment of the negative impacts
that high wholesale natural gas prices have on the Company&#146;s earnings. The
guidance also includes the effect of funding the Piedmont Natural Gas
Foundation announced in a separate press release.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Initiating Fiscal 2005 Earnings Guidance</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Piedmont Natural Gas is initiating its guidance for fiscal year 2005 earnings

a pre-split basis. The fiscal year 2005 earnings guidance range on a
post-split basis is $1.23 to $1.30 per diluted share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The 2005 earnings guidance assumes normal weather, a relatively stable economy
and wholesale natural gas prices during fiscal year 2005 in the range of prices
that have prevailed during fiscal year 2004. No general rate case activity is
expected that would impact 2005 earnings in any of the Company&#146;s jurisdictions.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The fiscal year 2005 utility capital budget is $163.7&nbsp;million, which includes
$6.2&nbsp;million related to Piedmont&#146;s 50% equity interest in Eastern North
Carolina Natural Gas Company. The capital budget also includes investments in
technology related to internal process improvements scheduled to begin
implementation during fiscal year 2005.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Piedmont Natural Gas Announces Organizational Changes</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Charlotte, N.C., August&nbsp;27 /PRNewswire-FirstCall/ </B> &#151; Thomas E. Skains,
Chairman, President and CEO of Piedmont Natural Gas (NYSE: PNY) announced today
several officer level organizational changes effective September&nbsp;1, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>June B. Moore, </B>formerly Vice-President &#151; Information Services, has been named
to the newly created position of Vice-President &#151; Customer Service and will
report directly to Mr.&nbsp;Skains. In her new position, Ms.&nbsp;Moore will assume
responsibility for all Customer Service operations in the company&#146;s service
area. This includes the company&#146;s Customer Contact Centers, Business Office
functions and processes.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>David L. Trusty</B>, formerly Vice-President &#151; Marketing, has been named
Vice-President &#151; Corporate Communications. He replaces Stephen D. Conner who
has announced his early retirement from the company. In his new capacity, Mr.
Trusty will assume responsibility for the company&#146;s Public and Media Relations,
Customer and Employee Communications, and Investor Relations. Mr.&nbsp;Trusty will
report to Mr.&nbsp;Kim R. Cocklin, Senior Vice-President, General Counsel and CCO of
Piedmont Natural Gas.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Ranelle Q. Warfield</B>, Vice-President &#151; Sales, will assume the additional
responsibility for Marketing as Vice-President &#151; Sales and Marketing. These new
responsibilities will include Marketing Research and Planning and Program
Development. Ms.&nbsp;Warfield will continue reporting to Mr.&nbsp;Franklin H. Yoho,
Senior Vice-President &#151; Commercial Operations of Piedmont Natural Gas.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Leslie Ennis</B>, formerly Director &#151; Business Information Solutions, has been
promoted to the position of Vice-President &#151; Information Services. She
replaces June Moore. In her new position, Ms.&nbsp;Ennis will report directly to
Mr.&nbsp;David J. Dzuricky, Senior Vice President and CFO of Piedmont Natural Gas.
