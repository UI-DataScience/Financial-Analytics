<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>PROGRESS SOFTWARE REPORTS THIRD QUARTER RESULTS</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Revenue up 15%; Operating Income and EPS up Sharply</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>BEDFORD, Mass., September&nbsp;16, 2004&#151;</B>Progress Software Corporation (PSC) (Nasdaq:
PRGS), a leading supplier of technology to develop, deploy, integrate and
manage business applications, today announced results for its third quarter
ended August&nbsp;31, 2004. Revenue for the quarter was $89.3&nbsp;million, up 15&nbsp;percent
(10&nbsp;percent at constant currency) from $77.7&nbsp;million in the third quarter of
2003. Software license revenue increased 21&nbsp;percent (16&nbsp;percent at constant
currency) to $32.9&nbsp;million from $27.2&nbsp;million in the same quarter last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On a generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP)&nbsp;basis, operating income
increased 26&nbsp;percent to $12.7&nbsp;million from $10.1&nbsp;million in the third quarter
of fiscal 2003. Net income increased 16&nbsp;percent to $8.5&nbsp;million from $7.3
million in the same quarter last year. Diluted earnings per share increased 16
percent to 22 cents from 19 cents in the third quarter of 2003. These results
include after-tax charges for amortization of acquired intangibles of $1.2
million for the third quarter of fiscal 2004 and $0.4&nbsp;million for the third
quarter of 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On a pro forma basis, excluding the charges for amortization of acquired
intangibles, operating income increased 36&nbsp;percent to $14.5&nbsp;million from $10.7
million in the third quarter of fiscal 2003. Pro forma net income increased
25&nbsp;percent to $9.7&nbsp;million from $7.8&nbsp;million in the same quarter last year.
Pro forma diluted earnings per share increased 25&nbsp;percent to 25 cents from 20
cents in the third quarter of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The company&#146;s cash and short-term investments at the end of the quarter totaled
$185.4&nbsp;million. During the third quarter, the company purchased approximately
403,000 shares of its stock at a cost of $7.8&nbsp;million. On September&nbsp;15, 2004,
the board of directors of PSC authorized the repurchase of up to 10&nbsp;million
shares of the company&#146;s outstanding common stock, at such times when the
company deems such purchases to be an effective use of cash, starting October
1, 2004. The company&#146;s existing repurchase authorization, under which 9.4
million shares remain available for repurchase, expires on September&nbsp;30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Progress Software Corporation continues to outperform other major software
companies,&#148; said Joseph W. Alsop, co-founder and chief executive officer of
PSC. &#147;Sonic Software, ObjectStore and DataDirect continue to perform well by
focusing on the increasing demand for real time data and the growing needs in
today&#146;s market for connectivity and integration. And the recently launched
Progress&#174; OpenEdge&#153; 10 Platform is enabling our partners to translate service
oriented architecture (SOA)&nbsp;theory into real applications that respond to the
needs of both large and small enterprises. We believe this demand for SOA-based
applications is the key driver behind the fastest ever adoption rate for a new
release of the OpenEdge product line.&#148;
