<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Smith &#038; Wesson Reports First Quarter Earnings Increase on Slightly Lower
Revenue and Full Year Projections.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Smith &#038; Wesson Holding Corporation (AMEX:SWB), parent of the legendary 152-year
old handgun maker Smith &#038; Wesson Corp., announced today that earnings for the
first fiscal quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 were $1.5&nbsp;million, or $.04 per diluted
share, compared with $585,000, or $.02 per diluted share, for the fiscal
quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2003. Net product sales for the quarter were $27.8
million, a $1.0&nbsp;million, or 3.5%, decline versus the fiscal quarter ended July
31, 2003. The results were in line with the estimates that the Company had
announced in August.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Firearms sales, the Company&#146;s core business, were $25.3&nbsp;million, an increase of
$547,000, or 2.2%, for the quarter versus the comparable quarter last year.
Non-firearms sales declined by $1.6&nbsp;million to $2.5&nbsp;million due to the
discontinued third-party machining business and lower handcuff sales.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The increase in net income was attributable to improved efficiencies, lower
spending and a $376,000 reduction to the product liability reserves. Gross
profit as a percentage of sales and licensing revenue increased from 30.6% for
the quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2003 to 33.2% for the quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Roy Cuny, President and CEO of Smith &#038; Wesson Holding Corporation, said, &#147;We
are very pleased with the expanding margins and improved operational
capabilities we have gained due to our strong dedication to improving the
manufacturing operation. The quarter clearly reflects that improvement goals
set over the course of the last six months are being realized.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cuny continued, &#147;Firearms sales were up slightly during the first quarter. Our
ability to provide innovative products and aggressive marketing promotions have
allowed us to increase market share and deepen our relationship with dealers
and our end customers. As we go forward, our continued improvements in
operational efficiency and our new marketing and sales teams will now provide
for both increased revenues and profitability. We are progressing in a
carefully planned manner and believe we will deliver value-driving results with
a combined revenue and earnings-focused growth plan.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cuny added, &#147;The demand for Model 500 revolvers continues to outpace our
production capability. We are purchasing additional equipment and expect to
double our production

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">of the Model 500 by the end of the second quarter. We have also decided to
increase our planned capital spending for the year to $9.0&nbsp;million, compared
with the $5.0&nbsp;million originally planned. The additional spending will address
the higher demand and process improvements.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">John Kelly, Chief Financial Officer of Smith &#038; Wesson Holding Corporation,
commented, &#147;Operating margins continue to improve as a result of the increased
productivity and cost savings. While we are still early in the process, the
Lean and Six Sigma initiatives are yielding favorable results, and we expect to
see further process improvements and cost reductions as we continue with these
efforts. We are also seeing the benefits of the restructuring that took place
in January of this year.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Kelly added, &#147;Refinancing efforts are currently underway, and we expect it to
be completed by the end of November.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company also announced that it expects sales for the fiscal year ending
April&nbsp;30, 2005 to increase by approximately 5% to 10% over the $117.9&nbsp;million
reported for the fiscal year ended April&nbsp;30, 2004. Net income is expected to
be between $5.4&nbsp;million and $6.4&nbsp;million, or between $.16 and $.19 per fully
diluted share compared with $1.4&nbsp;million, or $.04 per fully diluted share, for
the fiscal year ended April&nbsp;30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Commenting on the annual projections, Roy Cuny said, &#147;We are in the process of
developing our new marketing initiatives, including adding critical personnel
in the sales and marketing areas. We expect an increase in revenues in the
current fiscal year and expect sales to increase at a greater rate in fiscal
2006 as our marketing initiatives take hold.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">John Kelly added, &#147;Benefits from the current Lean and Six Sigma programs are
expected to intensify during the remainder of the fiscal year, with the full impact
being felt in the next fiscal year. We also plan to make a significant
investment in new equipment to increase productivity and expand our production
capacity.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">About Smith &#038; Wesson


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Smith &#038; Wesson Holding Corporation is the parent company of Smith &#038; Wesson
Corp., one of the world&#146;s leading producers of quality handguns, law
enforcement products and firearm safety and security products. Law enforcement
personnel, military personnel, target shooters, hunters, collectors, and
firearms enthusiasts throughout the world have used the Company&#146;s products with
confidence for more than 150&nbsp;years. Smith &#038; Wesson Corp. also manufactures and
markets Smith &#038; Wesson branded handcuffs. For more information, visit
http://www.smith-wesson.com.
