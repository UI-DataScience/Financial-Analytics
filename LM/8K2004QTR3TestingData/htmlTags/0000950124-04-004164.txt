<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>AMERICA WEST REPORTS AUGUST TRAFFIC</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PHOENIX&#151;America West Airlines (NYSE:AWA) today reported traffic statistics
for the month of August&nbsp;2004. Revenue passenger miles (RPMs) for August&nbsp;2004
were a record 2.2&nbsp;billion, an increase of 9.4&nbsp;percent from August&nbsp;2003. The
airline&#146;s capacity increased 8.1&nbsp;percent from August&nbsp;2003 to a record 2.6
billion available seat miles (ASMs). This resulted in a record passenger load
factor for August&nbsp;2004 of 83.1&nbsp;percent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Although we achieved record load factors in August, yields remain
depressed due to significant excess domestic capacity,&#148; said Scott Kirby,
executive vice president, sales and marketing. &#147;Historically, the industry&#146;s
and America West&#146;s domestic unit revenue decreases on average approximately
four to six percent from the second to the third quarter. Given that there has
been no improvement in the domestic pricing environment since the second
quarter, we anticipate a similar trend for America West this year.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE: Thursday, Sept. 2, 2004


<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>AMERICA WEST REPORTS AUGUST TRAFFIC</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PHOENIX&#151;America West Airlines (NYSE:AWA) today reported traffic statistics
for the month of August&nbsp;2004. Revenue passenger miles (RPMs) for August&nbsp;2004
were a record 2.2&nbsp;billion, an increase of 9.4&nbsp;percent from August&nbsp;2003. The
airline&#146;s capacity increased 8.1&nbsp;percent from August&nbsp;2003 to a record 2.6
billion available seat miles (ASMs). This resulted in a record passenger load
factor for August&nbsp;2004 of 83.1&nbsp;percent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Although we achieved record load factors in August, yields remain
depressed due to significant excess domestic capacity,&#148; said Scott Kirby,
executive vice president, sales and marketing. &#147;Historically, the industry&#146;s
and America West&#146;s domestic unit revenue decreases on average approximately
four to six percent from the second to the third quarter. Given that there has
been no improvement in the domestic pricing environment since the second
quarter, we anticipate a similar trend for America West this year.&#148;
