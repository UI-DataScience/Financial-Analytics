<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the third quarter of 2004, Archstone-Smith completed the sale of $591
million of non-core assets &#151; many to condominium converters at very attractive
capitalization rates &#151; representing 4,871 units. Including these dispositions,
Archstone-Smith has sold $790&nbsp;million of assets year-to-date in 2004, which
produced gross gains of approximately $102&nbsp;million, GAAP gains of $155&nbsp;million,
and an average unleveraged internal rate of return (IRR)&nbsp;of approximately
10.3%.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;The transactions underscore the significant embedded value in the
Archstone-Smith&#146;s irreplaceable portfolio,&#148; said R. Scot Sellers, chairman and
chief executive officer. &#147;We believe the market does not fully understand that
the unique quality of our assets and locations produce private market values
that are well in excess of any Wall Street analysts&#146; estimate of net asset
value per share for Archstone-Smith today.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Also during the third quarter of 2004, Ameriton completed the sale of $211
million of assets it developed for sale to institutional buyers, representing
1,494 units. In addition, Ameriton completed the sale of $93&nbsp;million of assets,
representing 566 units, that it previously acquired for re-sale after
completion of minor repositioning. The weighted average unleveraged pre-tax IRR
on the $304&nbsp;million of Ameriton sales was approximately 22%. The company&#146;s
previous guidance for 2004 Ameriton gains on dispositions was $0.14 &#151; $0.16 per
share and is now increased to $0.23 to $0.25 per share for 2004. Additional
detail on Ameriton&#146;s transaction activity is provided in the attached Schedule
1.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In addition to the dispositions that closed in the third quarter 2004, Ameriton
has an incremental $170&nbsp;million of anticipated sales under contract with
non-refundable earnest money deposits from buyers, which are scheduled to close
in 2005. These transactions are expected to contribute approximately $0.07 per
share to the company&#146;s 2005 earnings and FFO.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;These transactions demonstrate the significant value of the Ameriton franchise
to our shareholders,&#148; said Mr.&nbsp;Sellers. &#147;Ameriton has contributed approximately
$94&nbsp;million of after-tax gains to Archstone-Smith&#146;s FFO since 2000 and we are
pleased with the impressive track record we have established over the years. We
believe we are the only public apartment company pursuing this business on a
nationwide basis in a vehicle that is owned 100% by our shareholders.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Archstone-Smith&#146;s increased 2004 guidance contemplates the dilutive impact of
holding cash from its disposition activity. Currently, the company has
approximately $260&nbsp;million in cash, including cash on hand and cash held in
tax-deferred exchange accounts. The revised 2004 guidance also includes the
company&#146;s preliminary assessment of the recent hurricanes and tropical storm
damages sustained at its Florida communities, which it estimates to be $0.02 to
$0.03 per share. This guidance does not include the insurance recoveries the
company expects to receive as such amounts are not yet quantifiable.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Archstone-Smith (NYSE: ASN) is a recognized leader in apartment investment and
operations. With a current total market capitalization of $11.0&nbsp;billion,
Archstone-Smith owns and operates an irreplaceable portfolio of high-rise and
garden apartment communities concentrated in many of the most desirable
neighborhoods in the greater Washington, D.C. metropolitan area, Southern
California, the San Francisco Bay area, Chicago, Boston, Southeast Florida,
Seattle and the greater New York City metropolitan area. The company
continually upgrades the quality of its portfolio through the selective sale of
assets, using proceeds to fund investments with even better growth prospects.
Through its two brands, Archstone and Charles E. Smith, Archstone-Smith strives
to provide peace of mind for its customers &#151; backed by unconditional service
guarantees. As of August&nbsp;31, 2004, Archstone-Smith owned or had an ownership
position in 240 communities, representing 82,892 units, including communities
