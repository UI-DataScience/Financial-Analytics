<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>DEL MONTE FOODS COMPANY REPORTS<BR>
FISCAL 2005 FIRST QUARTER RESULTS</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Earnings In Line with Company Guidance</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SAN FRANCISCO, September&nbsp;2, 2004 </B>&#151; Del Monte Foods Company (NYSE: DLM) today
announced net sales of $626.0&nbsp;million and income from continuing operations of
$8.6&nbsp;million, or diluted earnings per share of $0.04, for the quarter ended
August&nbsp;1, 2004. This compares to net sales of $611.3&nbsp;million and income from
continuing operations of $13.5&nbsp;million, or $0.07 per diluted share, for the
prior year period. First quarter earnings per share were consistent with the
guidance the Company provided in its year-end earnings announcement.
Integration costs of $8.6&nbsp;million ($0.02 per share) and $7.0&nbsp;million ($0.02 per
share) are included in the results for the first quarter of fiscal 2005 and
fiscal 2004, respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are pleased with our performance in the first quarter, as we grew our top
line 2.4% and took pricing and cost reduction actions which successfully offset
inflationary pressures that were felt industry-wide,&#148; said Richard G. Wolford,
Chairman, President and CEO. &#147;As planned, we increased our investment in
marketing and new product introduction in the quarter, consistent with our
strategy and growth objectives. We expect these investments to fuel continued
momentum and long-term growth.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Reported operating income for first quarter of 2005 totaled $40.5&nbsp;million, a
decrease of $11.0&nbsp;million compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2004. This
decrease in operating income is due in part to marketing and new product
investments made to drive the business and position it for long-term growth. In
addition, the Company incurred higher production and logistics costs related to
higher steel, energy and raw materials prices that were offset by pricing
actions and cost reduction programs. During the first quarter of 2005, the
increase in Corporate expenses was due to higher corporate integration costs,
increased costs associated with the expensing of stock options and certain
legal expenses.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Outlook</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company expects sales growth in the second quarter of fiscal 2005 of
approximately 4% to 6% over net sales of $791.6&nbsp;million in the second quarter
of fiscal 2004, and diluted earnings per share of approximately $0.17 to $0.21
for the second quarter of fiscal 2005. This compares to $0.19 for the second
quarter of fiscal 2004. The Company expects these earnings results to be driven
by increased sales volume, favorable pricing and cost reduction initiatives,
offsetting inflationary and other cost increases. In addition, the Company
plans to increase its marketing and new product investment over the prior year.
We also expect integration costs of approximately $0.01 to $0.02 per share in
the second quarter of fiscal 2005, which compares to $0.02 per share in the
second quarter of fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company expects fiscal 2005 sales growth of approximately 1% to 2% over
fiscal 2004 net sales of $3,129.9&nbsp;million and reaffirms projected diluted
earnings per share from continuing operations in fiscal 2005 of approximately
$0.81 to $0.86, including integration expense of approximately $19&nbsp;million, or
$0.06 per share. This compares to $0.76 of diluted earnings per share from
continuing operations in fiscal 2004, which included integration expense of
$44.5&nbsp;million, or $0.13 per share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company also expects cash provided by operating activities, less cash used
in investing activities, to exceed $200&nbsp;million in fiscal 2005.
