ALIGN="center"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="3"><B>Casey&#146;s Reports Strong First Quarter Earnings </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Ankeny, Iowa, September 1, 2004&#151;Casey&#146;s General Stores, Inc. (Nasdaq symbol CASY) today reported a 14% increase in earnings for the first quarter of fiscal 2005 ended July 31, 2004. Basic earnings rose to
$0.32 per share from $0.28 for the first quarter of fiscal 2004. &#147;The increase was especially encouraging because earnings were measured against a very good quarter in the previous year,&#148; said Chairman and CEO Ronald M. Lamb. &#147;Gross
profit was up in all three business categories, and we are pleased with the early progress toward our 6 annual performance goals.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Gasoline</B>&#151;The Company&#146;s first annual goal is to increase same-store gasoline gallons sold at least 2% with an average margin of at least 10.5 cents per
gallon. In the first quarter, the Company sold 262.4 million gallons compared with 259.3 million in the same quarter a year ago. On a same-store basis, gallons sold were down 1.4%. &#147;That&#146;s understandable,&#148; Lamb explained, &#147;given
the continuing high retail gasoline prices affecting demand. Our July sales trends were positive, and we&#146;re hopeful they will continue their upward direction.&#148; The average margin per gallon was 11.8 cents, up from 9.8 cents for the
previous first quarter and well above the fiscal 2005 goal. Gasoline gross profit improved to $31 million from $25.5 million. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Grocery &amp; Other Merchandise</B> (G&amp;OM)&#151;Casey&#146;s second goal is to increase same-store G&amp;OM sales 2.9% with an average margin over 32%. Total
first-quarter sales were up 4.4% to $196.1 million; same-store sales increased 1.9%. The average margin was 31.3%. Gross profit rose to $61.3 million from $59.2 million. &#147;The gains we&#146;re making are promising,&#148; Lamb said, &#147;and we
believe point-of-sale data will continue to help us improve product selection and space allocation. Thanks in part to the store reset completed at the end of the quarter, all items in this category met our expectations. We have evidence our
strategies for managing cigarettes are working. Same-store cigarette sales comparisons were positive for July, the first time since September of 2002.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Prepared Food &amp; Fountain</B> (PF&amp;F)&#151;The next goal is to increase PF&amp;F same-store sales 6% with an average margin over 60%. Total PF&amp;F sales were
up 10% to $52.2 million for the quarter, and same-store sales were up 5.8%, nearly at goal. The average margin was 58.7%. Despite high cheese prices, gross profit was up a substantial 6.3% to $30.6 million. Management credited strategic pricing,
popular new products, and expanded use of point-of-sale data for the gross profit improvement. &#147;This category is performing exceptionally well,&#148; said Lamb. &#147;We will continue to monitor wholesale cheese prices and look for advantageous
buying opportunities.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Point of Sale</B> (POS)&#151;The fourth goal is

Our real task is deriving full value from the data the system generates. We are currently testing a data warehouse and business intelligence product to assist us further in using the data more effectively.&#148; Starting in October, the Company will
accelerate the rollout of POS to 12 per week. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Operating
Expenses</B>&#151;The fifth goal is to hold the percentage increase in operating expenses to no more than the percentage increase in inside sales. For the quarter, total inside sales were up 5.5% to $248.3 million. Operating expenses were up 8% to
$83.3 million due to a 27% increase in bank charges for customers using credit cards to purchase more expensive gasoline. The average retail price of gas was $1.83 this quarter versus $1.42 for the first quarter a year ago. &#147;This goal
represents our ongoing attention to return on investment at the operational level,&#148; said Lamb. &#147;The quarter&#146;s total gross profit grew in line with operating expenses, increasing 8% to $123.2 million.&#148; </FONT></P>
