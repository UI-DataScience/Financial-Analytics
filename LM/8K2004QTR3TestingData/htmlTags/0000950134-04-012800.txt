<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Michaels Stores Announces Record Second Quarter Results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">IRVING, Texas &#151; August&nbsp;25, 2004 &#151; Michaels Stores, Inc. (NYSE:MIK) today
reported record financial results for its second quarter and the six months
ended July&nbsp;31, 2004. Diluted earnings per share for the quarter were $.38, a 9%
increase over last year&#146;s $.35. These results include pre-tax costs of $4.9
million ($3.0&nbsp;million after tax) for the establishment of insurance reserves
resulting from the financial difficulties being experienced by one of the
Company&#146;s insurance providers. Excluding this item, diluted earnings per share
in the quarter were $.43, a 23% increase over last year. On a year-to-date
basis, diluted earnings per share were $.80 compared to $.65 for the same
period last year, a 23% increase. Excluding the charge related to the insurance
carrier&#146;s difficulties, diluted earnings per share year-to-date were $.85, a
31% increase over the same period last year.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for the second quarter was $26.7&nbsp;million, a 10% increase
compared to net income of $24.3&nbsp;million in the same period last year. Excluding
the insurance-related charge, net income for the quarter increased 23% to $29.8
million over the prior year. On a year-to-date basis, net income was $56.1
million compared to net income of $45.1&nbsp;million in the same period last year, a
24% increase. Excluding the insurance charge, year-to-date net income was $59.1
million, a 31% increase over last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Michael Rouleau, President and Chief Executive Officer, said, &#147;We are very
pleased with our record performance for the second quarter and the first half
of 2004. The significant investments we have made in our infrastructure give us
great confidence in our future. Although a number of external factors could
impact consumer spending, we remain optimistic regarding our potential for a
strong second half performance, including the Holiday selling season, and
another record year.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Operating Performance</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sales of $682.9&nbsp;million for the second quarter of 2004 were up 11% over
sales of $616.5&nbsp;million in the same period last year. Same-store sales were up
5% for the quarter. Year-to-date sales were $1.409&nbsp;billion, an 11% increase
over sales of $1.273&nbsp;billion in the same period last year. Same-store sales
were up 6% year-to-date.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;During the second quarter, the Company opened nine and relocated 10
Michaels stores, opened three and closed one Aaron Brothers store, and opened
three Recollections stores.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company&#146;s operating income totaled $47.5&nbsp;million or 7.0% of sales for
the quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, compared to operating income of $45.5&nbsp;million
or 7.4% of sales for the same period last year. Operating income for the first
half of 2004 was $99.5&nbsp;million or 7.1% of sales compared to $85.0&nbsp;million or
6.7% of sales in the same period of 2003. Operating income includes the
aforementioned $4.9&nbsp;million charge recorded in the second quarter related to
the establishment of insurance reserves reflecting the financial difficulties
at one of the Company&#146;s insurance carriers. In addition, workers&#146; compensation
expense for the quarter increased $4.5&nbsp;million

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Balance Sheet</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company&#146;s cash balance as of July&nbsp;31, 2004, the end of the second
quarter, was $307.4&nbsp;million, an increase of $170.2&nbsp;million over the same period
last year. Average inventory per Michaels store at the end of the second
quarter, inclusive of distribution centers, decreased 11% to $1.079&nbsp;million
from $1.208&nbsp;million for the same period last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company also announced that it has repurchased 913,200 shares of its
common stock during the second quarter under its stock repurchase plans at an
average price of $51.81 per share and has repurchased an additional 203,900
shares during the month of August under its stock repurchase plans at an
average price of $52.58 per share. As of August&nbsp;25, 2004, under its repurchase
plans, the Company is authorized to repurchase approximately 1.6&nbsp;million
additional shares plus such shares as may be repurchased with proceeds from the
future exercise of options under the Company&#146;s 2001 General Stock Option Plan.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Outlook</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company is projecting a same-store sales increase of 3% to 5% in the
second half of the year, contributing to a forecasted annual same-store sales
increase of 4% to 6% in 2004. Same-store sales in the third quarter are
expected to increase 3% to 5% overall with same-store sales increases of 4% to
6% in both August and October with relatively flat same-store sales in
September.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company&#146;s diluted earnings per share for the remainder of 2004 are
expected to be in the $.62 to $.65 range for the third quarter, in the $1.49 to
$1.55 range for the fourth quarter, and in the $2.92 to $3.05 range for the
full year, a 15% to 20% increase.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Michaels Stores, Inc. is the world&#146;s largest specialty retailer of

Company owns and operates 831 Michaels stores in 48 states and Canada, 161

insurance providers (&#147;the insurance reserve&#148;). Net income excluding the

reserve are not measures of performance calculated in accordance with generally
accepted accounting principles (&#147;GAAP&#148;). Net income excluding the insurance
reserve and diluted earnings per share excluding the insurance reserve should
not be considered in isolation of, or as a substitute for, the GAAP financial
measures net income or diluted earnings per share, respectively, as indicators
of the Company&#146;s performance.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company believes the presentation of net income excluding the insurance
reserve and diluted earnings per share excluding the insurance reserve is
relevant and useful information to investors because it allows investors to
view performance in a manner similar to the method used by management to track
operating performance from period to period and helps improve investors&#146;
ability to understand trends in the Company&#146;s operating performance.
