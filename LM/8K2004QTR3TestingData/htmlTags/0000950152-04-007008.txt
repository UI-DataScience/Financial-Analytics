<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For Immediate Release



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Worthington Reports Record First Quarter Results</B><BR>
Earnings Per Diluted Share: $0.66 vs. $0.07



<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><B>COLUMBUS, Ohio, September&nbsp;22, 2004 </B>&#150; Worthington Industries, Inc. (NYSE: WOR)
today reported record first quarter results for the three months ended August
31, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Highlights



<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Sales for the first quarter of fiscal 2005 were $769.3&nbsp;million, an increase of
54% from last year&#146;s $498.0&nbsp;million. First quarter net earnings were $57.9
million and earnings per diluted share were $0.66, compared to first quarter
net earnings of $5.9&nbsp;million, or $0.07 per diluted share, for the same period
last year.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Earnings for the current quarter were reduced by a $5.6&nbsp;million pre-tax charge
related to the sale of the Decatur, Alabama, cold mill and related assets.
This charge is mainly due to contract termination costs that could not be
accrued until the sale closed, which occurred on August&nbsp;1, 2004, and other
adjustments to the charge recorded at May&nbsp;31, 2004. The after-tax impact of
this charge was a reduction in net earnings of $3.5&nbsp;million or $0.04 per
diluted share.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">&#147;I am pleased to report our second consecutive quarter of record results,&#148; said
John McConnell, Chairman and CEO of Worthington Industries. &#147;We achieved these
results even though two of our key market segments, automotive and commercial
construction, were not at their peak. While we did experience some benefit
from selling lower priced inventory in a higher price environment, that impact
has lessened considerably, and this quarter&#146;s results would have been
record-breaking regardless. I look forward to what our Metal Framing segment
can accomplish as commercial construction &#151; particularly office construction
&#151; continues to pick up from what has been a multi-year low,&#148; concluded
McConnell.

<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Segment Results</B>


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">In the Processed Steel Products segment, quarterly net sales rose 58%, or
$166.6&nbsp;million, to $453.8&nbsp;million from $287.2&nbsp;million in the comparable quarter
of fiscal 2004. The increase in net sales was due to higher volumes (up 14%)
and pricing (up 39%). Excluding the impact of the $5.6&nbsp;million charge related
to the Decatur asset sale, operating income was significantly improved due to
higher volumes and a wider spread between selling prices and material costs.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">In the Metal Framing segment, net sales increased 69%, or $97.3&nbsp;million, to
$238.4&nbsp;million from $141.1&nbsp;million in the comparable quarter of fiscal 2004.
The increase was the result of higher pricing as volumes were down 10% from the
year ago quarter largely due to weather related and other project delays. The
wider spread between selling prices and material costs was responsible for a
significant improvement in operating income.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">In the Pressure Cylinders segment, net sales increased 10%, or $6.7&nbsp;million, to
$73.2&nbsp;million from $66.5&nbsp;million in the comparable quarter of fiscal 2004.
Unit volumes were up 2% overall as strength in the European market was
partially offset by softer domestic propane cylinder demand. A portion of the
increase in European revenues was the result of a weakened U.S. dollar which
boosted reported revenues in dollars by $1.6&nbsp;million. Operating income fell
due to costs related to the partial closure of the Portugal facility.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Worthington&#146;s joint ventures continued to perform well. Equity in net income
of the six unconsolidated affiliates totaled $13.3&nbsp;million for the quarter, up
68% from $7.9&nbsp;million in the year ago quarter. Results at all six joint
ventures were up significantly from the prior year.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Outlook</B>


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Relative to last year, economic and industry conditions have improved across
all customer segments except &#147;Big 3&#148; automotive, Processed Steel&#146;s largest
customer segment. &#147;Big 3&#148; vehicle production is projected to be down 7% for
the coming fiscal quarter compared to the same quarter last year but up 12%
from this quarter. In commercial construction, Metal Framing&#146;s primary market,
the U.S. Census Bureau&#146;s Index of Private Construction Spending confirms that
commercial construction activity has shown year-over-year improvement during
the last six months. This index serves as a leading indicator for the Metal
Framing business segment as metal framing products are generally used in the
latter stages of commercial construction projects.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Other</B>


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><I>Dividends declared</I>


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">On August&nbsp;19, 2004, the board of directors declared a quarterly cash dividend
of $0.16 per share payable September&nbsp;29, 2004, to shareholders of record
September&nbsp;15, 2004. This is the 147th consecutive quarter that Worthington has
paid a dividend since it became a public company in 1968.

<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Corporate Profile</B>


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Worthington Industries is a leading diversified metal processing company with
annual sales of more than $2&nbsp;billion. The Columbus, Ohio, based company is
North America&#146;s premier value-added steel processor and a leader in
manufactured metal products such as metal framing, pressure cylinders,
automotive past model service stampings, metal ceiling
grid systems and laser welded blanks. Worthington employs more than 8,000
people and operates 61 facilities in 10 countries.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Founded in 1955, the company operates under a long-standing corporate
philosophy rooted in the golden rule, with earning money for its shareholders
as the first corporate goal. This philosophy, an unwavering commitment to the
customer, and one of the strongest employee/employer partnerships in American
industry serve as the company&#146;s foundation.
