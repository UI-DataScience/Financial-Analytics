<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>TORO THIRD QUARTER NET EARNINGS UP 26.5%<BR>
ON 15.1% NET SALES GROWTH</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Company on Track for Record Full Year Net Sales and Earnings</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>LIVE CONFERENCE CALL<BR>
August&nbsp;24, 10:00&nbsp;a.m. CT</B><BR>
<u>www.thetorocompany.com/invest</u>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">BLOOMINGTON, Minn. (August&nbsp;24, 2004) &#151; The Toro Company (NYSE: TTC) today
reported record net earnings of $34.2&nbsp;million, or $1.33 per diluted share, on
net sales of $454.0&nbsp;million for its fiscal 2004 third quarter ended July&nbsp;30,
2004. In its fiscal 2003 third quarter, Toro reported net earnings of $27.0
million, or $1.03 per diluted share on net sales of $394.5&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the nine months ended July&nbsp;30, 2004, Toro reported net earnings of
$95.7&nbsp;million, or $3.68 per diluted share, on net sales of $1,315.6&nbsp;million.
In the comparable fiscal 2003 period, Toro reported net earnings of $76.0
million, or $2.92 per diluted share, on net sales of $1,186.3&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kendrick B. Melrose, The Toro Company Chairman and Chief Executive
officer, said the strong third quarter results keep the company on track to
deliver the best full year financial performance in its history. &#147;During our
third quarter, we continued to generate healthy sales growth in all segments,&#148;
said Melrose. &#147;And, as in our second quarter, we converted strong volume
growth into even stronger earnings growth by continuing to focus on overall
operating effectiveness, productivity improvement through lean manufacturing
initiatives, expense reductions through our &#145;no waste&#146; programs and focused
asset management. Our year-to-date gross margin remained above last year&#146;s
rate despite continued increases in steel and rising transportation and other
commodity costs.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Melrose said third quarter sales exceeded expectations in most business
categories, reflecting increased demand in a recovering economy and brand
strength in Toro&#146;s principal markets. &#147;During the third quarter,
shipments were particularly strong in our
landscape contractor, commercial, and international businesses,&#148; said
Melrose.
&#147;In addition, our lean manufacturing and no-waste initiatives are clearly
boosting profitability.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">2 &#151; Toro Third Quarter Net Earnings Up 26.5% on 15.1% Sales Growth

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SEGMENT RESULTS</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Segment data is provided in the table following the &#147;Condensed
Consolidated Statement of Earnings.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Professional</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the third quarter, professional segment sales increased 17.9% to
$287.9&nbsp;million. As in the prior quarter, segment sales increased in nearly all
product categories, although irrigation product sales fell short of the
company&#146;s expectations.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;International sales for the third quarter increased 16.4% compared with
the fiscal 2003 period.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Professional segment earnings for the third quarter were $54.3&nbsp;million, up
28.6% from the fiscal 2003 third quarter which benefited from leveraging of
expenses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Residential</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Residential segment sales for the third quarter totaled $144.2&nbsp;million, up
11.8% from the prior year third quarter. Product categories with the strongest
period-over-period sales growth included snow throwers and electric products.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;International sales for the third quarter increased 20.7% compared with
last year&#146;s third quarter. Most of the increase was attributable to strong
snow thrower and electric product demand.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>REVIEW OF OPERATIONS</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gross margin for the third quarter was 36.2% compared with 37.2% in the
prior year&#146;s third quarter. &#147;The margin decline was primarily the result of
increases in costs for steel, other commodities and transportation, which were
partially offset through our ongoing cost management and productivity
improvement efforts,&#148; said Melrose.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selling, general and administrative (SG&#038;A) expenses for the third quarter
were 24.6% of net sales, down from 25.8% in the fiscal 2003 third quarter.
Continued expense leveraging and reductions more than countered increases in
incentive expenses as well as additional expenses related to ongoing new
product development and other growth initiatives.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interest expense for the third quarter was $3.9&nbsp;million, down from $4.2
million in the comparable year-ago period as a result of lower average debt and
improved asset management.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The company&#146;s balance sheet continued to strengthen from increased
earnings and effective working capital management. Net inventories at the end
of the third quarter were $217.4&nbsp;million, down nearly 7.9% from the end of the
fiscal 2003 third quarter. Accounts receivable at the end of the third quarter
totaled $381.3&nbsp;million, up only 2.2%, significantly less than the consolidated
net sales increase of 15.1%.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>BUSINESS OUTLOOK</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Our strong performance through the first nine months of fiscal 2004
should continue through the year&#146;s final quarter and into fiscal 2005,&#148; said
Melrose. &#147;We expect our lean/no waste initiatives to yield strong
profitability improvement for this year as well as fiscal 2005. Moreover, we
believe continued investments in key growth initiatives will help ensure our
sales growth outperforms the market. Lastly, we fully expect to complete the
year with a solid balance sheet, strong operating cash flow, and low
inventories in the field.&#148; The company expects to report fiscal 2004 net
earnings per diluted share in the range of $3.82 to $3.90, excluding the
effects of the previously announced share repurchase program, on sales growth
of 9 to 10%.
