<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Meade Instruments Reports</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Second-Quarter Fiscal 2005 Results</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>IRVINE, Calif. &#150; September&nbsp;13, 2003 &#150; Meade Instruments Corp. (Nasdaq NM: MEAD)</B>
today reported results for the second quarter of fiscal year 2005, ended August
31, 2004. The Company&#146;s fiscal year 2005 ends on February&nbsp;28, 2005.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net sales for the three months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 were $22.5&nbsp;million versus
$28.3&nbsp;million in the comparable period a year ago. Excluding non-cash charges
for the Company&#146;s ESOP, net loss for the three months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 was
$0.8&nbsp;million, or ($0.04) per share, compared to ESOP-adjusted net income of
$0.1&nbsp;million, or $0.01 per diluted share, for the comparable period a year ago.
Including ESOP charges, second-quarter 2005 net loss was $0.9&nbsp;million, or
($0.05) per share, compared to net income of $40,000, or $0.00 per diluted
share, in the comparable period a year ago.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net sales for the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 were $42.1&nbsp;million versus
$52.8&nbsp;million in the comparable period a year ago. Excluding non-cash charges
for the Company&#146;s ESOP, net loss for the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 was
$1.9&nbsp;million, or ($0.10) per share, compared to ESOP-adjusted net loss of $0.5
million, or ($0.03) per share, for the comparable period a year ago. Including
ESOP charges, net loss for the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 was $2.0
million, or ($0.11) per share, compared to net loss of $0.7&nbsp;million, or ($0.04)
per share, in the comparable period a year ago.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Steven G. Murdock, president and CEO of Meade Instruments, said: &#147;We are
disappointed with the results of the second quarter. Continuing from the first
quarter, sales of our mid-priced and higher-priced telescopes in the U.S. have
been less than we expected. We believe telescope sales in the prior year were
higher because of the Mars opposition. However, even after adjusting for the
Mars event, sales have been weaker than expected in those price categories.
Sales of our other products have generally met our expectations.



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;
</DIV>

<!-- PAGEBREAK -->
<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>

<DIV style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',Times,serif">



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are engaged in a broad development effort that will result in new product
introductions across nearly every product line,&#148; continued Murdock. &#147;We expect
the mid-priced and higher-priced telescope market to rebound as we begin to
ship several new and innovative products into that market segment. We expect
several of those telescope products to begin shipping within the next ninety
days, and we anticipate that other new products will begin shipping over the
remainder of the fiscal year. While those higher-margin products move towards
production, we continue to focus on reducing expenses and improving labor
efficiencies.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Second-quarter gross margins &#151; which decreased to 24.8% from 28.9% in the
prior year &#151; principally reflect the effect of fixed costs on lower sales
volume,&#148; said Murdock.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Due to continued
limited visibility in the mid-priced and higher-priced
telescope markets, we expect that net revenues for fiscal 2005 will be $125
million to $135&nbsp;million, and that net income per diluted share, excluding ESOP
expense, will be $0.05 to $0.15,&#148; concluded Murdock.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In conjunction with the announcement, the Company&#146;s management will conduct a
teleconference to discuss these recent financial results and Meade Instruments&#146;
business outlook. To participate in the teleconference, which begins at 5:30
a.m. PDT (8:30 a.m. EDT) on Monday, September&nbsp;13, please call 877-869-7690 (or
706-634-1478) approximately 10 minutes prior to the teleconference start time.
Investors can also listen to the call live via the Internet at www.meade.com
and www.fulldisclosure.com. These websites will host an audio archive of the
call.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Meade Instruments is a leading designer and manufacturer of optical products
including telescopes and accessories for the beginning to serious amateur
astronomer. Meade offers a complete line of binoculars that address the needs
of everyone from the casual observer to the serious sporting or birding
observer. Meade also offers a complete line of riflescopes under the Simmons&#174;,
Weaver&#174; and Redfield&#174; brand names. The Company distributes its products
worldwide through a network of specialty retailers, mass merchandisers and
domestic and foreign distributors. Additional information on Meade is
available at www.meade.com.
