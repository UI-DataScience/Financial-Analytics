<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Hastings Entertainment, Inc. Reports Pre-Tax Income of $0.06 per Diluted Share
for 2Q 2004 Compared to $0.02 per Diluted Share 2Q 2003</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Pre-Tax Income for Year to Date 2004 of $0.31 Compared to a Pre-Tax Loss of
$0.08 per Share for 2003</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">AMARILLO, Texas, August&nbsp;23, 2004&#151;Hastings Entertainment, Inc. (NASDAQ: HAST),
a leading multimedia entertainment superstore retailer, today reported results
for the three and six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004. Net income for the second
quarter of fiscal 2004 was approximately $0.5&nbsp;million, or $0.04 per diluted
share, compared to approximately $0.2&nbsp;million, or $0.02 per diluted share for
the second quarter of fiscal 2003. For the six months, net income was
approximately $2.3&nbsp;million, or $0.19 per diluted share in fiscal 2004 compared
to a net loss of approximately $0.9&nbsp;million, or $0.08 per share for fiscal
2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Pre-tax income was approximately $0.7&nbsp;million and $3.7&nbsp;million, or $0.06 and
$0.31 per diluted share, for the three and six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004,
respectively. This compares with pre-tax income of approximately $0.2&nbsp;million
and a pre-tax loss of approximately $0.9&nbsp;million, or $0.02 per diluted share
and $0.08 per share, for the three and six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2003,
respectively.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Gross Profit. </I>For the second quarter, total gross profit dollars increased
approximately $3.2&nbsp;million, or 8.1%, to $43.7&nbsp;million from $40.5&nbsp;million for

increases in merchandise margin rates. As a percentage of total revenues,

human resource and occupancy costs associated with the operation of a greater
number of new, expanded and relocated superstores. As a percentage of total
revenues, SG&#038;A increased slightly to 34.7% for the current quarter compared to
34.5% for the same quarter in the prior year.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Gross Profit. </I>For the current six months, total gross profit dollars increased
approximately $9.3&nbsp;million, or 11.8%, to $88.3&nbsp;million from $79.0&nbsp;million for
the same period last year, primarily as a result of higher revenues and
increases in merchandise margin rates. As a percentage of total revenues,
gross profit increased to 35.4% for the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 compared
to 34.0% for the same period in the prior year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Selling, General and Administrative expenses (&#147;SG&#038;A&#148;). </I>SG&#038;A increased
approximately $4.8&nbsp;million to $83.6&nbsp;million for the current six month period
compared to $78.8&nbsp;million for the same period in the prior year, due primarily
to higher human resource and occupancy costs associated with the operation of a
greater number of new, expanded and relocated superstores. As a percentage of
total revenues, SG&#038;A decreased to 33.5% for the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004
compared to 33.9% for the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Stock Repurchase



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On September&nbsp;18, 2001, we announced a stock repurchase program of up to $5.0
million of our common stock. During the second quarter of fiscal year 2004, we
purchased a total of 34,500 shares at a cost of approximately $261,000, or
$7.58 per share. As of July&nbsp;2004, a total of 860,423 shares had been purchased
under the program at a cost of approximately $4.225&nbsp;million, for an average
cost of $4.91 per share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Fiscal Year 2004 Guidance



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Although our net income for the second quarter was below our internal
forecast, we are maintaining our guidance of $0.80 to $0.85 per share on a
pre-tax income basis and $0.50 to $0.53 per share on a diluted net income
basis,&#148; said Dan Crow, Vice President of Finance and Chief Financial Officer.
&#147;As previously mentioned in this release we incurred unplanned costs associated
with implementing our new warehouse system and we also incurred unplanned costs
incurred with hiring of our new Vice President of Human Resources and our new
Vice President of Marketing. The improvements in our merchandise margins
should continue through the remainder of the year and offset estimated declines
in rental video revenues along with our second quarter shortfall in net
income.&#148;
