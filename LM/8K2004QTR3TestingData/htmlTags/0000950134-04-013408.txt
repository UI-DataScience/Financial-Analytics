<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>DAVE &#038; BUSTER&#146;S, INC. REPORTS INCREASE IN<BR>
BOTH SECOND QUARTER NET INCOME AND SAME STORE REVENUES</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DALLAS-September&nbsp;8, 2004-Dave &#038; Buster&#146;s, Inc. (NYSE: DAB), a leading operator
of upscale restaurant/entertainment complexes, announced earnings for its
second quarter and 26&nbsp;weeks ended August&nbsp;1, 2004.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total revenue for the second quarter increased 1.7&nbsp;percent to $89.8&nbsp;million
from $88.3&nbsp;million in the prior year&#146;s comparable quarter. Food and beverage
revenue increased 3.1&nbsp;percent, and amusement and other revenue increased 0.3
percent. Revenue from comparable stores increased 0.5&nbsp;percent for the quarter.
Special event revenue on a comparable store basis was 13.4&nbsp;percent of total
revenue, compared to 12.2&nbsp;percent of total revenue in the second quarter last
year. Operating income for the quarter was $4.5&nbsp;million, compared to $4.0
million in the prior year. Net income for the quarter was $2.2&nbsp;million, or
$0.16 per diluted share, compared to $1.5&nbsp;million, or $0.11 per diluted share
for the same period last year. The number of diluted shares outstanding
increased 22.5&nbsp;percent to 16.486&nbsp;million shares in the current quarter,
compared to 13.458&nbsp;million shares in the second quarter of last year, primarily
as a result of the convertible debt issues during last fiscal year. During the
second quarter last year, the company incurred certain one-time charges
totaling $0.8&nbsp;million, or $0.04 per diluted share. Without these charges,
operating income would have been $4.7&nbsp;million last year and net income would
have been $2.0&nbsp;million, or $0.15 per diluted share.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total revenues for the 26-week period increased 2.7&nbsp;percent to $184.8&nbsp;million
from $179.9&nbsp;million for the comparable period last year. Food and beverage
revenue increased 3.0&nbsp;percent, and amusement and other revenue increased 2.5
percent. Special event revenue on a comparable store basis was 13.0&nbsp;percent of
total revenue, compared to 12.2&nbsp;percent of total revenue in the comparable
period last year. Operating income was $11.5&nbsp;million, compared to $10.6
million for the prior year. Net income was $5.8&nbsp;million, or $0.40 per diluted
share, compared to $4.5&nbsp;million, or $0.34 per diluted share, in the prior year.
The number of diluted shares outstanding increased 22.4&nbsp;percent to 16.376
million, for the 26&nbsp;weeks compared to 13.383&nbsp;million during the same period
last year.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We continue to improve the overall profitability of the company,&#148; remarked
Buster Corley, the company&#146;s CEO. &#147;We now have six consecutive quarters of
improved year-over-year earnings through a difficult economy. We are pleased
with our progress and have confidence in the D&#038;B team&#146;s ability to perform.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We obviously are very pleased with the second quarter results,&#148; stated Dave
Corriveau, President of the company. &#147;The construction of our new store in
Arcadia, CA is on target to open on September&nbsp;29, 2004. We are optimistic that
this store will be a very good performer for us.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We remain confident in our earnings guidance of $0.89 to $0.92 per diluted
share and revenue guidance of $379&nbsp;million to $382&nbsp;million for the fiscal year
which ends on January&nbsp;30, 2005,&#148; commented W. C. Hammett, the company&#146;s CFO.
