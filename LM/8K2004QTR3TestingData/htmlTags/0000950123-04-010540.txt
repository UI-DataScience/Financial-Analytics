<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>For immediate release:</B>


<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ALLOY ANNOUNCES SECOND QUARTER FINANCIAL RESULTS</B><BR>
<I>Management Revises Guidance for Key Financial Performance Measures<BR>
Initiates Program to Improve Sponsorship Business Profitability</I>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>New York, NY &#150; September&nbsp;1, 2004 </B>- Alloy, Inc. (Nasdaq:ALOY), a media,
marketing services, direct marketing and retail company primarily targeting the
dynamic Generation Y population, today reported revenues for the fiscal quarter
ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 of $86.6&nbsp;million and a net loss attributable to common
stockholders of $11.6&nbsp;million or $0.27 per diluted share. For this second
fiscal quarter, Alloy registered a $4.6&nbsp;million loss before interest and other
income/expense, income taxes, depreciation and amortization, stock-based
compensation expense, and restructuring charges (&#147;Adjusted EBITDA&#148;). For
additional financial detail, including the reconciliation of Adjusted EBITDA to
net income (loss)&nbsp;determined under GAAP, please refer to the financial tables
provided at the end of this release.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Total revenues for the second fiscal quarter increased 8% to $86.6
million, compared with $80.5&nbsp;million for the second quarter of fiscal 2003.
Second fiscal quarter net merchandise revenues of $43.7&nbsp;million were up 46%
compared with $30.0&nbsp;million for last year&#146;s second fiscal quarter. The
increase resulted from our acquisition of dELiA*s, which offset an overall
decline in revenues for Alloy&#146;s legacy catalog titles. Second fiscal quarter
sponsorship and other revenues of $42.9&nbsp;million were down 15% versus $50.5
million for the comparable period in our last fiscal year. The decrease was
primarily attributable to reduced sales in our promotions and specialty direct
marketing businesses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Second fiscal quarter gross profit increased to $42.1&nbsp;million, or 48.7% of
revenues, compared with $39.4&nbsp;million, or 48.9% of revenues, for the comparable
period last year, largely as a result of the overall increase in revenues.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Operating expenses were $51.4&nbsp;million for the second quarter of fiscal
2004 versus $39.9&nbsp;million for the second quarter of fiscal 2003. The increase
resulted primarily from the expenses of running the acquired dELiA*s operations
and the staff build-up in our merchandise operations, along with higher legal
and financial administration costs.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net loss for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 was $11.2&nbsp;million, compared
with a net loss of $0.3&nbsp;million for last fiscal year&#146;s second quarter. Net
loss attributable to common stockholders for the second quarter of fiscal 2004
was $11.6&nbsp;million, or $0.27 per diluted share, compared with net loss
attributable to common stockholders of $1.0&nbsp;million, or $0.02 per diluted
share, for last fiscal year&#146;s second quarter. Adjusted EBITDA transitioned
from earnings of $2.8&nbsp;million for the second fiscal quarter of 2003 to a loss
of $4.6&nbsp;million for the second fiscal quarter of 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alloy&#146;s name database now stands at 27.1&nbsp;million total names, of which
over 8&nbsp;million have a buying history with the company.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Commenting on the quarter, Matt Diamond, Chairman and Chief Executive
Officer stated, &#147;Our second fiscal quarter sponsorship business performance
compared unfavorably to last year. To address this challenge, we have embarked
on an aggressive plan to drive improved sales productivity and operating
margins in the near term. In our merchandise business, we continued to make
significant strides in strengthening operations while pursuing value-enhancing
strategies for the business in the 2005 fiscal year.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Looking ahead, Mr.&nbsp;Diamond concluded, &#147;Primarily due to our financial
performance in the second quarter of fiscal 2004, we have altered our full year
financial outlook. We are revising our merchandise revenue to the range of
$215&nbsp;million to $225&nbsp;million, and our sponsorship revenue to the range of $185
million to $195&nbsp;million. Our diluted net loss attributable to common
stockholders per share has been revised to a range of ($0.45) to ($0.55) and
our Adjusted EBITDA range is now $3&nbsp;million to $6&nbsp;million.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Total revenues for the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 increased 16% to
$174.4&nbsp;million compared with $149.9&nbsp;million for the six months ended July&nbsp;31,
2003. Net merchandise revenues for the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 of $88.0
million were up 47% versus $60.0&nbsp;million for the six months ended July&nbsp;31,

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">About Alloy


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Alloy, Inc. is a media, marketing services, direct marketing and retail
company primarily targeting Generation Y, a key demographic segment comprising
the more than 60&nbsp;million boys and girls in the United States between the ages
of 10 and 24. Alloy&#146;s convergent media model uses a wide range of media assets
to reach more than 25&nbsp;million Generation Y consumers each month. Through
Alloy&#146;s 360 Youth media and marketing services unit, marketers can connect with
the Generation Y audience through a host of advertising and marketing programs
incorporating Alloy&#146;s media and marketing assets such as direct mail catalogs,
college and high school newspapers, Web sites, school-based media boards, and
college guides. Alloy&#146;s promotional marketing arm, AMP Agency, is a lifestyle
marketer offering creative thinking for consumer brands through custom
marketing solutions both on and off-line, and specializing in mobile tours,
events and grassroots marketing. AMP was named 2004 &#147;Agency of the Year&#148; by
PROMO Magazine. Alloy generates revenue from its broad reach in the Generation
Y Community by providing marketers advertising and marketing services through
360 Youth and AMP Agency and by selling apparel, accessories, footwear, room
furnishings and action sports equipment directly to the youth market through
catalogs, Web sites and retail stores. For further information regarding Alloy,
