<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>EXHIBIT 99.1</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ROCKY MOUNTAIN CHOCOLATE FACTORY, INC. REPORTS 48 PERCENT<BR>
INCREASE IN SECOND QUARTER EARNINGS</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>COMPANY INCREASES CASH DIVIDEND 33% TO $0.32 ANNUALIZED RATE AND RAISES<BR>
FY2005 EARNINGS GUIDANCE</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DURANGO, Colorado (September&nbsp;28, 2004) &#151; Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory,
Inc. (Nasdaq/NMS: RMCF) (the &#147;Company&#148;), which franchises gourmet chocolate and
confection stores and manufactures an extensive line of premium chocolates and
other confectionery products, today reported record earnings for the second
quarter and first half of FY2005. The Company also announced that its Board of
Directors has approved a 33% increase in the Company&#146;s cash dividend to an
annualized rate of $0.32 per share.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>(Note: All per-share figures in this news release are adjusted for a 3-for-2
stock split in February&nbsp;2004 and a 10% stock dividend that was distributed to
shareholders in May&nbsp;2004).</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, revenues increased 7.3&nbsp;percent to $5.9
million, compared with $5.5&nbsp;million in the second quarter of FY2004.
Comparable-store sales at franchised retail outlets increased approximately 5.6
percent during the most recent quarter when measured against the three months
ended August&nbsp;31, 2003. The Company believes that the increase in comp-store
sales reflects a strengthening in the U.S. economy and an increase in customer
traffic at retail venues where franchised stores are located.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net earnings for the second quarter of FY2005 increased 47.6&nbsp;percent to
$1,003,000, compared with $680,000 in the prior-year period. Basic earnings
per share increased 50.0&nbsp;percent to $0.24 in the most recent quarter, compared
with $0.16 in the second quarter of FY2004. Diluted earnings per share
increased 46.7&nbsp;percent to $0.22 in the second quarter of FY2005, compared with
$0.15 in the prior-year period.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are very pleased to report another quarter of record earnings, along with
increased comp-store sales, for the three months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004,&#148; stated
Frank Crail, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Rocky Mountain Chocolate
Factory, Inc. &#147;We believe that our revenue gain of 7.3&nbsp;percent in the most
recent quarter does not reflect the true strength of our underlying business,
because last year benefited from a large order to a major warehouse club
customer in August. Because of requested scheduling, most of this year&#146;s order
from that customer will be shipped in our third fiscal quarter, rather than
August, and will thus be included in our third fiscal quarter results. We
project that our second quarter revenues would have posted an increase of
approximately 20&nbsp;percent if the warehouse club shipment had occurred in August
of both years.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Our franchisees opened four new stores in the second quarter and twelve new
stores during the first half of the fiscal year,&#148; observed Bryan Merryman,
Chief Operating Officer and Chief Financial Officer of the Company. &#147;We expect
approximately 20 new store openings during the third quarter, and full year
openings should reach the upper end of our target range of 35-40 units.
Franchisee interest in our new &#145;kiosk&#146; retail concept has accelerated sharply
in recent months, and we expect franchisees to be operating a total of
approximately 18 kiosks by the Christmas holiday shopping season.
We continue to be very pleased with the performance of our new &#145;in-line&#146; store
design and the &#145;kiosk&#146; concept in regional shopping mall environments.&#148;


<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;
</DIV>

<!-- PAGEBREAK -->
<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>

<DIV style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',Times,serif">
<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the second quarter of FY2005, franchisees opened new stores in Cerritos,
California; Raleigh, North Carolina; Mesquite, Texas; and Toronto Pearson
Airport in Toronto, Canada. Subsequent to August&nbsp;31, 2004, franchisees have
opened new stores in West Covina, California; Moline, Illinois; and Oklahoma
City, Oklahoma. The stores in Cerritos, California; and Moline, Illinois
represent the thirteenth and fourteenth &#145;kiosk&#146; units to open since February
2002.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, revenues increased 12.7&nbsp;percent to
$10.6&nbsp;million, versus $9.4&nbsp;million in the corresponding period of the previous
fiscal year. Comparable-store sales at franchised retail outlets for the
six-month period ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 increased approximately 5.6&nbsp;percent when
measured against the first half of FY2004. If the above mentioned warehouse
club orders had been fully shipped in August of 2004 and 2005, respectively,
revenues for the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2004 would have increased
approximately 20&nbsp;percent.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Net earnings increased 50.4&nbsp;percent to $1.6&nbsp;million in the six months ended
August&nbsp;31, 2004, compared with $1.1&nbsp;million during the same period in FY2004.
Basic earnings per share increased 48.0&nbsp;percent to $0.37 during the first half
of FY2005, versus $0.25 in the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2003. Diluted
earnings per share increased 45.8&nbsp;percent to $0.35 for the six-month period
ended August&nbsp;31, 2004, versus $0.24 in the six months ended August&nbsp;31, 2003.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;In light of the impressive earnings gains posted during the first two quarters
of Fiscal 2005 and the backlog in new franchisee store openings scheduled for
the third and fourth quarters, we are increasing our guidance for the fiscal
year to a range of a 25% to 30% increase in net income,&#148; noted Crail.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Our balance sheet and cash flows remain healthy,&#148; continued Merryman. &#147;Income
from operations totaled $1.6&nbsp;million in the second quarter, versus $1.1&nbsp;million
in the prior-year period. After reducing long-term debt by 20&nbsp;percent, or
$624,000, during the first half of the fiscal year, our balance sheet at August
31, 2004 reflected $3.0&nbsp;million of cash in the bank, a current ratio of 3.1
to 1.0, and a debt-to-equity ratio of 13.1%.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company also announced that its Board of Directors has approved a 33%
increase in its quarterly cash dividend to an annualized rate of $0.32 per
share. This represents the fourth time the quarterly dividend has been
increased since the Company&#146;s cash dividend policy was implemented in December
2003. On September&nbsp;16, 2004, the Company paid a quarterly cash dividend of
$0.06 per share to shareholders of record September&nbsp;2, 2004. Dividends
declared for each remaining quarter of the current fiscal year are expected to
be paid at the rate of $0.08 per share of common stock.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;The new annualized cash dividend rate of $0.32 per share would, if declared
September&nbsp;27, 2004, have provided a yield of approximately 2.8% on our closing
September&nbsp;27, 2004 common share price of $11.57,&#148; stated Crail. &#147;The Board
will continue to monitor future operating results to determine if the dividend
payout ratio should be changed in the future.&#148;

products. The Company and its franchisees currently operate 271 stores in 39
states, Canada, Guam and the United Arab Emirates. The Company&#146;s common stock
is listed on The Nasdaq National Market under the symbol &#147;RMCF&#148;.
