<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>BOCA RESORTS, INC. REPORTS FISCAL FOURTH QUARTER RESULTS</B>




<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boca Raton, Florida (August&nbsp;23, 2004) &#150; Boca Resorts, Inc. (NYSE:RST), an
owner and operator of luxury resorts in Florida, reported net income of $5.8
million, or $.14 per diluted share, for the three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004,
compared to net income of $3.2&nbsp;million, or $.08 per diluted share, for the
three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2003. The Company reported net income of $18.0
million, or $.44 per diluted share, for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared
to net income of $8.8&nbsp;million, or $.22 per diluted share, for the year ended
June&nbsp;30, 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The resort operations are generally seasonal. The resorts historically
experience greater revenue, costs and income in the third and fourth quarters
of the fiscal year ended June&nbsp;30 due to increased occupancy and room rates
during the winter and spring months. Historically, 16%, 23%, 36% and 25% of
annual revenue has been derived during the first, second, third and fourth
fiscal quarters, respectively. Additional details relating to the comparative
operating results for the three and twelve months ended June&nbsp;30 are set forth
below.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>COMPARATIVE RESULTS OF OPERATIONS</B></U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Revenue</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leisure and recreation revenue totaled $80.3&nbsp;million and $76.6&nbsp;million for
the three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and 2003, respectively, and $313.3&nbsp;million
and $290.2&nbsp;million for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and 2003, respectively.
The increase in revenue for the three and twelve months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004,
compared to the three and twelve months ended June&nbsp;30, 2003, resulted primarily
from increases in occupancy at each of the Company&#146;s properties as well as
increased revenue derived from non-room sources such as food and beverage
revenue, yachting and marina revenue, and spa revenue. Management believes
increases in resort occupancy are largely the result of an improving economic
landscape, significant property enhancements, the high quality of the Company&#146;s
assets, a commitment to quality customer service resulting in strong guest
loyalty and a favorable market mix that combines high-end leisure travelers and
corporate groups.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Operating Expenses</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cost of leisure and recreation services totaled $35.6&nbsp;million, or 44% of
revenue, for the three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared to cost of leisure
and recreation services of $34.4&nbsp;million, or 45% of revenue, for the three
months ended June&nbsp;30, 2003. Cost of leisure and recreation services totaled
$137.4&nbsp;million, or 44% of revenue, for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared
to cost of leisure and recreation services of $130.3&nbsp;million, or 45% of
revenue, for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2003. Cost of leisure and recreation
services primarily consisted of direct costs to service rooms, marinas, food
and beverage operations, retail establishments, spas and other amenities at the
resorts.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selling, general and administrative expenses (&#147;S,G&#038;A&#148;) totaled $20.6
million, or 26% of revenue, for the three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared
to S,G&#038;A of $22.0&nbsp;million, or 29% of revenue, for the three months ended June
30, 2003. S,G&#038;A totaled $85.8&nbsp;million, or 27% of revenue, for the year ended
June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared to S,G&#038;A of $86.0&nbsp;million, or 30% of revenue, for the
year ended June&nbsp;30, 2003. S,G&#038;A includes, among other items, administrative
payroll costs, selling and marketing expenses, energy

for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and 2003, respectively. The average debt
balance was $197.8&nbsp;million for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared to $217.8
million for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2003, while the average cost of borrowing
was 10.6% and 10.0% for the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and 2003, respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Provision for Income Taxes</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company recorded provisions for income taxes equal to 38.5% of pretax
income during the three and twelve months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>BALANCE SHEET DATA AND FINANCING TRANSACTIONS</B></U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At June&nbsp;30, 2004, the Company had cash and cash equivalents of $47.6
million. The Company preserved cash through certain income tax accounting
method changes. Under the Company&#146;s tax accounting policy, it uses tax lives
and methods for its property and equipment as permitted by the Internal Revenue
Service in various rulings and regulations, which resulted in a decrease in
income taxes currently payable. Furthermore, the Company received a refund of
income taxes paid in prior years totaling approximately $12.6&nbsp;million in April
2004. The income tax accounting method change has no impact on the Company&#146;s
total income tax expense for financial reporting purposes.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At June&nbsp;30, 2004, the Company had $190.1&nbsp;million principal amount of
outstanding 9.875% senior subordinated notes. The Company notified the trustee
under the outstanding senior subordinated notes that it intends to redeem the
notes on August&nbsp;27, 2004. The Company recently closed on a new $325.0&nbsp;million
senior credit facility. The senior credit facility consists of a term loan in
the principal amount of $200.0&nbsp;million and a revolving credit facility in the
principal amount of $125.0&nbsp;million. The proceeds of the term loan will be used
to redeem in full the Company&#146;s outstanding senior subordinated notes. The
$125.0&nbsp;million revolving credit facility replaces the Company&#146;s previous
revolver, which was scheduled to mature in June&nbsp;2005. The Company has no
borrowings under the new revolving credit facility.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVES</B></U>

high-speed Internet service (which was also implemented at each of the
Company&#146;s resort properties) and cable TV for each slip. The Company also
continued to enhance the amenity base at the Boca Raton Resort &#038; Club with the
complete renovation of the central pool area and pool-side restaurant, the
addition of global positioning systems for its fleet of golf carts, a

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>OUTLOOK</B></U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company expects RevPar to increase 2.0% to 3.0% and Total RevPar
(which includes ancillary non-room sources of revenue) to increase 4.0% to 5.0%
for the year ending June&nbsp;30, 2005, over the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004. The
Company further anticipates that net income will increase to between $19.0
million to $21.0&nbsp;million, or $.45 to $.50 per diluted share, for the year
ending June&nbsp;30, 2005. The earnings guidance for the year ending June&nbsp;30, 2005
includes a non-recurring charge associated with the early retirement of the
Company&#146;s 9.875% senior subordinated notes of approximately $13.1&nbsp;million,
which translates into $8.0&nbsp;million, or $.19 per diluted share, on an after-tax
basis.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;David S. Feder, President and Chief Operating Officer of Boca Resorts,
Inc. commented, &#147;We are very pleased with our operating performance for the
recently completed three-month period as well as for the full fiscal year. We
credit our strong financial turnout largely to a high quality portfolio of
properties and effective management of room inventory and pricing in the
various distribution channels, which has resulted in RevPar and Total RevPar
growth for eleven consecutive quarters. As the economic recovery strengthens
and consumer confidence consistently trends positive, we continue to benefit
from solid revenue progression, increases in market share and a favorable
market mix that combines high-end leisure travelers and corporate groups. In
addition to top line growth, we have focused on cost-saving measures that have
produced dramatic gains in our profit margins. Aside from improving results of
operations, we also maintain a strong balance sheet that provides us
considerable flexibility.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boca Resorts, Inc. owns luxury resort properties and golf courses in
Florida. The Company&#146;s Florida resort and golf portfolio includes the Boca
Raton Resort &#038; Club with its two golf courses in Boca Raton; the Registry
Resort at Pelican Bay, the Edgewater Beach Hotel and Naples Grande Golf Club in
Naples; and the Hyatt Regency Pier 66 Resort and Marina, the Radisson Bahia Mar
