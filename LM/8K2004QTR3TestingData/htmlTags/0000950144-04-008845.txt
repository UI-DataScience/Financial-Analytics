<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>CLAIRE&#146;S STORES, INC. REPORTS AUGUST<BR>
COMPARABLE STORE SALES INCREASE SEVEN PERCENT</B></U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PEMBROKE PINES, FL., September&nbsp;2, 2004. Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc. (NYSE:CLE)
reported today that comparable store sales for the four weeks ended August&nbsp;28,
2004 increased seven percent after increasing eight percent during the
corresponding four-week period last year. Total sales during the four-week
period ended August&nbsp;28, 2004 increased 13&nbsp;percent to $98,990,000 compared with
$87,620,000 for the comparable four-week period last year.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the first seven months of Fiscal 2005, sales increased 16&nbsp;percent to
$685,773,000 compared to sales of $592,325,000 for the comparable period last
year. Year to date, comparable store sales increased ten percent compared with
an increase of six percent during the first seven months of Fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bonnie Schaefer, Co-Chairman and Co-CEO of Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc. said,
&#147;The revitalization of our international operation, which consists of our
stores in the United Kingdom, Ireland, France, Switzerland, Austria and Germany
(the latter three, collectively referred to as SAG), is continuing to show
progress. Performance continues to be strongest in SAG, where our efforts have
had the greatest time to take effect. In the United Kingdom and Ireland, we
are continuing to implement Claire&#146;s best practices, roll-out new floor sets
and deliver enhanced customer service, all of which are generating positive
results. The turnaround in France, as previously predicted, still needs more
time, however, we believe our aggressive focus on improving the performance of
those stores is slowly leading to improvements in that country.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Marla Schaefer, Co-Chairman and Co-CEO of Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc. said, &#147;Our
North American business continued to perform well in August, at a time when we
are beginning to come up against increasingly difficult comparable store sales
comparisons. Results would have been even more robust if Labor Day fell in
August, as it did last year. Instead, the one week time lag resulted in lower
store traffic this August versus last. Back to school shopping got off to a
later start this August as families remain on vacation and schools in the
Mid-West and the Northeast will be opening approximately one week later than
last year. Our projection that consolidated comparable store sales for the
third fiscal quarter will rise by nine to ten percent took into consideration
the shift in Labor Day.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Company Overview</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc., is a leading international specialty retailer offering
value-priced costume jewelry and accessories to fashion-aware tweens, teens and
young adults through its two store concepts: Claire&#146;s and Icing by Claire&#146;s.
While the latter operates only in North America, Claire&#146;s operates
internationally. As of August&nbsp;30, 2004, Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc. operated
approximately 2,835 stores in the United States, Canada, Puerto Rico, the
Virgin Islands, the United Kingdom, Ireland, France, Switzerland, Austria and
Germany. Claire&#146;s Stores, Inc. operates through its subsidiary, Claire&#146;s
Nippon, Co., Ltd., 138 stores in Japan as a 50:50 joint venture with AEON, Co.,
Ltd. (fka JUSCO, Co. Ltd.), a $25&nbsp;billion specialty retailer headquartered in
Japan. The Company also licenses 55 stores in the Middle East under a
licensing and merchandising agreement with Al Shaya Co., Ltd. and two stores in
South Africa under similar agreements with The House of Busby Limited.
