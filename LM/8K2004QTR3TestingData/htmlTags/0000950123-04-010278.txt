<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>(BW) (BUFFETS HOLDINGS)
Buffets Holdings, Inc. Announces Results for the Year Ended
June&nbsp;30, 2004<br>and Sales Guidance for the First Quarter of 2005</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;EAGAN, Minn.&#151;(BUSINESS WIRE)&#151;August&nbsp;27, 2004&#151;Buffets Holdings, Inc.
(&#147;Buffets Holdings&#148;), the parent company of Buffets, Inc. (&#147;Buffets&#148;), today
reported operating results for its fiscal year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Buffets Holdings reported sales for the fiscal year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004,
of $942.8&nbsp;million, versus sales of $985.3&nbsp;million for the fiscal year ended
July&nbsp;2, 2003. While average weekly sales for 2004 of $49,949 were 2.0% higher
than 2003 average weekly sales, total sales declined by 4.3% in 2004. This
decline was due to a reduction in operating weeks, offset by an increased check
average. During 2004, Buffets closed 15 and opened three restaurants. These
activities, coupled with the impact in 2004 of the sale, opening and closure of
restaurants in 2003, resulted in a 6.2% decline in operating capacity during
2004. Same-store sales for 2004 increased by 1.3% as compared to those
reported for the prior year. This increase reflected a 1.6% decline in guest
traffic and a 2.9% increase in average check. The increase in average check
comprised a 2.0% price increase and a 0.9% average check increase due to a
reduction in free promotional and employee meals.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for 2004 was $8.0&nbsp;million, as compared to net income of $11.9
million for 2003. Net income for 2004 included pretax charges of approximately
$14.2&nbsp;million. These pretax charges comprised a $1.9&nbsp;million charge for the
impairment of long-lived assets, $2.2&nbsp;million of financing-related compensation
expense related to the
issuance of Buffets Holdings&#146;
13<SUP style="font-size: 80%; vertical-align: top;">7</SUP>/<SUP style="font-size: 80%; vertical-align: bottom;">8</SUP>% senior discount notes, a $4.8&nbsp;million
loss on the amendment and restatement of Buffets&#146; senior credit agreement and
a financing transaction in February&nbsp;2004 that was postponed due to market conditions, and a $5.3&nbsp;million loss
on the early extinguishment of approximately $29.6&nbsp;million of
Buffets&#146; 11&#188;%
senior subordinated notes. Net income for fiscal 2003 included pretax charges
of approximately $3.6&nbsp;million. These pre-tax charges consisted of a $5.9
million loss associated with the sale and leaseback of 30 restaurants and a
$4.8&nbsp;million charge for the impairment of long-lived assets, partially offset
by a pretax gain of $7.1&nbsp;million on the sale of 13 Original Roadhouse Grill
restaurants.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sales for the fourth quarter of 2004 (the 12-week period ended June&nbsp;30,
2004) totaled $224.4&nbsp;million, as compared to sales of $234.9&nbsp;million for the
comparable period in the prior year. While average weekly sales for the fourth
quarter of 2004 of $51,807 were 0.4% higher than average weekly sales for the
prior year period, total sales declined 4.5%. This sales decline was due to
the previously discussed reduction in Buffets&#146; operating capacity. Same-store
sales for the fourth quarter of 2004 were down 0.1%, as compared to those
reported for the comparable period in the prior year. This decline reflected a
2.7% increase in average check and a 2.8% decline in guest traffic.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for the fourth quarter of 2004 was $1.5&nbsp;million, as compared to
net income of $8.3&nbsp;million for the fourth quarter of 2003. Net income for the
fourth quarter of 2004 included pretax charges of approximately $6.6&nbsp;million.
These pretax charges comprised a $1.9&nbsp;million charge for the impairment of
long-lived assets, $2.2&nbsp;million of
financing-related compensation expense related to the issuance of Buffets
Holdings&#146; 13<SUP style="font-size: 80%; vertical-align: top;">7</SUP>/<SUP style="font-size: 80%; vertical-align: bottom;">8</SUP>% senior discount notes, and a $2.5&nbsp;million loss on the early
extinguishment of approximately $15.3&nbsp;million of Buffets&#146;
11&#188;% senior
subordinated notes. Net income for the fourth quarter of 2003 included a net
pretax gain of approximately $2.1&nbsp;million. This net pre-tax gain consisted of
a $0.4&nbsp;million loss associated with the sale and leaseback of three restaurants
and a $4.5&nbsp;million charge for the impairment of long-lived assets, offset by a
pretax gain of $7.1&nbsp;million on the sale of 13 Original Roadhouse Grill
restaurants.
