<p style="font-weight:bold;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;page-break-after:auto;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ITEM 2.02.&#160;&#160; DISCLOSURE OF RESULTS OF OPERATIONS AND
FINANCIAL CONDITION.</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On
August 26, 2004, during its second quarter earnings conference call, Ultimate
Electronics, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) disclosed a dollar amount representing how
much the Company was in excess of its EBITDA covenant (&#147;Covenant EBITDA&#148;) in
its Fourth Amended and Restated Loan and Security Agreement, dated July 27,
2004 (the &#147;Credit Agreement&#148;).&#160; The
Credit Agreement includes a covenant that requires the Company to have a
minimum cumulative Covenant EBITDA that begins on April 1, 2004 and is
calculated on a cumulative monthly basis until February 1, 2005.&#160; Starting February 1, 2005, the Covenant
EBITDA is calculated on a trailing twelve month basis.&#160; Covenant EBITDA is defined as consolidated
net earning (or loss), <u>minus</u> extraordinary gains, <u>plus</u> non-cash
impairment losses, interest expense, income taxes, and depreciation and
amortization for such period, as determined in accordance with Generally
Accepted Accounting Principles (&#147;GAAP&#148;).</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Under
the Credit Agreement, the Covenant EBITDA is required to be not less than
negative $6.5 million.&#160; As of July 31,
2004, the Covenant EBITDA was negative $5.1 million, resulting in a $1.4
million excess, or &#147;cushion,&#148; which number was incorrectly stated on the
earnings conference call as $1.8 million.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

are reconciliations of Net Loss to Covenant EBITDA and Covenant EBITDA to Cash
Flows from Operating Activities.&#160; Cash
Flows from Operating Activities is the most directly comparable GAAP measure to
Covenant EBITDA:</font></p>
