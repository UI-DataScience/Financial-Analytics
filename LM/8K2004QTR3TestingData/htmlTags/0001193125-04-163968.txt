Neogen Corporation (Nasdaq: NEOG) announced today that its operating income for the first quarter of its 2005 fiscal year, which ended Aug. 31, jumped 30% from the previous year&#146;s first quarter. Neogen&#146;s first quarter revenues were
$15,212,000 &#151; a 24% increase compared to the prior year. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">First quarter
net income per share was $0.19 as compared with $0.16 for the same period last year. Neogen&#146;s quarterly net income increased 22% to $1,584,000 from $1,302,000 in FY 2004. The quarter marked the 46th consecutive profitable quarter from
operations for the company, and was the 51st of the past 55 quarters when Neogen reported revenue increases as compared with the previous year. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;The first quarter was a great start for our new fiscal year,&#148; said James Herbert, Neogen&#146;s president. &#147;We continued our remarkable quarter-to-quarter
success of consistent growth in sales and profits, and also made significant progress in implementing our plan to ensure long-term growth. I&#146;m very proud of our management team and employee group.&#148; </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The first quarter also saw Neogen continue its progress toward controlling costs to improve
operating results. Sales and marketing expenses dropped to 21% of sales from last year&#146;s 24%. General and administrative expenses remained within Neogen&#146;s target range &#151; under 8% of sales, and gross margins increased for the third
straight quarter to 49.3% of sales. As a result, the company&#146;s operating income increased to 16% of sales compared with 15% in the prior year. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Neogen&#146;s Animal Safety Division led the company&#146;s first quarter revenue increase, with sales up 50% from $5,334,000 in FY 2004 to $8,012,000 in FY 2005. The
November 2003 acquisitions of Hacco and Hess &amp; Clark contributed substantially to the increase. However, the division also saw significant sales growth in its veterinary instrument products, up 20% over last year, and sales of Neogen&#146;s
proprietary K-Blue<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="1" COLOR="#000000"><SUP>&reg;<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2" COLOR="#000000"></FONT></SUP><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2" COLOR="#000000"></FONT></FONT> substrate, which was up 45%.

Neogen&#146;s unique vaccines sold primarily to the professional equine market. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="2">Integrating Hacco&#146;s rodenticides into Neogen&#146;s sales and marketing group yielded a 18% sales increase when compared to the same quarter under previous ownership. Neogen&#146;s veterinary instrument revenues were bolstered by
continuing improvement in sales to veterinary retailers, including Tractor Supply Company. Sales of Neogen&#146;s D3<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="1" COLOR="#000000"><SUP>&#153;<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"

market acceptance. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;The quarter provided solid indications that our
cost-saving initiatives are yielding results,&#148; said Lon Bohannon, Neogen&#146;s chief operating officer. </FONT>
</P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px">
<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We made some strategic decisions in the previous fiscal year that had a short-term adverse impact on our earnings knowing we would achieve long-term
benefits. In our first quarter, we started to realize some of those benefits.&#148; Neogen&#146;s Food Safety Division first quarter revenues were up 4.4% compared to the prior year. Lower overall sales for general sanitation products and tests to
detect naturally occurring toxins were offset by sizable increases in sales of Acumedia dehydrated culture media products, and food safety products to international markets. Acumedia sales increased 38% overall, led by strong sales into industrial,
pharmaceutical and food safety markets. International food safety sales jumped 33%, led by a 38% sales growth in Latin America for products such as rapid test kits for genetically modified soybeans and test kits to detect harmful pathogens, such as
<I>Listeria</I>. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Increasing sales of Acumedia products led Neogen to a
decision to consolidate its outdated Baltimore dehydrated culture media operations into newly-renovated space in its Lansing, Mich., facilities. The new facilities and processing equipment are expected to dramatically improve operating efficiency,
and increase capacity by 300 to 400%. Production of Acumedia products is expected to be fully integrated into the Lansing facilities before the end of Neogen&#146;s second fiscal quarter. The move is not expected to have a material effect on
earnings for the current fiscal year. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;Our solid first quarter results
