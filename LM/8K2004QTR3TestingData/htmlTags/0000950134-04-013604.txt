<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>CRAFTMADE INTERNATIONAL ANNOUNCES FISCAL 2004<BR>
FOURTH QUARTER AND YEAR-END RESULTS</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">COPPELL, TEXAS, September&nbsp;14, 2004 &#151; Craftmade International, Inc. (<B>Nasdaq:
CRFT</B>) today reported net income of $2,131,000 for its fiscal fourth quarter
ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared to net income of $1,918,000 for the fourth
quarter of fiscal year 2003. On a fully diluted basis, net income per share
increased to $0.41 per share from $0.36 per share for the corresponding period
a year ago. Weighted average diluted shares outstanding for the quarter were
5,207,000 compared to 5,402,000 for the same period last year.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Revenues for the quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 were $32,112,000 compared to
revenues of $27,142,000 reported for last year&#146;s fourth quarter. Fourth
quarter net sales from the showroom division increased 5.8%, or $793,000, to
$14,379,000 from $13,586,000 for the same period last year. The increase in
the showroom division was primarily due to the success of new product
introductions as well as strength in the overall housing sector of the U.S.
economy.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Fourth quarter net sales from the mass retail division increased 30.8%, or
$4,177,000, to $17,733,000 from $13,556,000 for the same period last year. The
increase was partially due to the strong performance of Design Trends&#146; portable
lamp program, which generated incremental revenue of $2,568,000 during the
period. In addition, the mass retail division benefited from initial store
orders for additional SKU&#146;s of outdoor lighting rolled out to its largest mass
retail customer during the quarter.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, the Company&#146;s revenues increased 11.2%, or
$12,205,000, to $121,238,000 from $109,033,000 last year. Earnings per share
on a fully diluted basis increased 15.4% to $1.42 for fiscal 2004, compared to
$1.23 per diluted share for fiscal 2003. Fiscal year 2004 net income increased
11.7%, or $800,000, to $7,646,000 from $6,846,000 for fiscal 2003. Weighted
average diluted shares outstanding for fiscal 2004 were 5,383,000 compared to
5,568,000 for fiscal 2003.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company&#146;s gross profit as a percentage of sales for the three months ended
June&nbsp;30, 2004, decreased to 29.8% of net sales from 34.0% of net sales in the
fourth quarter last year. Gross profit from the showroom division decreased to
39.0% of sales from 41.8% of sales in the fourth quarter of 2003. The decline
was partially due to price increases from the Company&#146;s ceiling fan
manufacturer that were not passed through to customers. The gross margin of
the mass retail division decreased to 22.4% of sales for the three months ended
June&nbsp;30, 2004, compared to 26.1% of sales in the fourth quarter of 2003. The
decline in the gross margin of the mass retail division was partially due to a
shift in the sales mix with a greater portion of revenue made up of direct
shipment sales, which carry lower gross margins and lower SG&#038;A expenses. In
addition, the mass retail division experienced an increase in markdown money
and price concessions provided to mass retail customers.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total selling, general and administrative expenses of the Company were
$4,717,000, or 14.7% of net sales for the fourth quarter, compared to
$4,994,000 or 18.4% of net sales for the same period in 2003. The decline in
SG&#038;A expenses was primarily related to a decline in the SG&#038;A expenses of the
mass retail division. The decrease in mass retail division&#146;s expenses was
partially related to cost savings derived from the closing of the California
office, which occurred in the second quarter of fiscal 2004, as well as a
change in the sales mix, as discussed above.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are pleased by our strong financial performance driven by both our showroom
and mass retail divisions, in spite of non-recurring legal and accounting
expenses incurred during the year. These expenses are related to legal and
accounting expenses necessitated by the changing regulatory environment,&#148; said
James R. Ridings, Craftmade&#146;s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.
&#147;Additionally, we believe the company&#146;s long-term outlook continues to be
strong, and anticipate that we will achieve earnings growth of 15% to 20% for
fiscal 2005.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As previously announced, pursuant to the provisions of FASB Interpretation No.
46 (FIN 46), <I>Consolidation of Variable Interest Entities </I>effective January&nbsp;1,
2004, the Company began to consolidate Design Trends and PHI and, as encouraged
by FIN 46, has restated its previously issued financial statements to reflect
Design Trends and PHI as consolidated entities.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company has filed for an extension in connection with the filing of its
Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 and, as a result, will file
its Form 10-K within the next 15&nbsp;days.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">A conference call to discuss the company&#146;s fourth quarter and fiscal 2004
results, as well as the company&#146;s outlook for fiscal 2005, is scheduled for
Tuesday, September&nbsp;14, at 10:00&nbsp;a.m. Central Time. To participate in this
call, dial 719-457-2730, and refer to confirmation code 961944. A replay of
the conference call can also be accessed via the company&#146;s Web site at
www.craftmade.com.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Craftmade International, Inc., founded in 1985 and based in Coppell, Texas, is
engaged in the design, distribution and marketing of a broad range of
proprietary ceiling fans, lighting products and related accessories. The
company distributes its premium products through a network of 1,600 showrooms
and electrical wholesalers through a national sales organization of more than
65 independent sales representatives. Through its Trade Source International
subsidiary, acquired in 1998, Craftmade distributes outdoor lighting, ceiling
fan accessories and an indoor lighting line to the mass merchandiser market,
which includes Lowe&#146;s, Home Depot and Wal-Mart.
