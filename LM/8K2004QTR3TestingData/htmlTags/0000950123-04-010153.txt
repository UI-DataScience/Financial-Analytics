<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>PARTY CITY CORPORATION ANNOUNCES FOURTH QUARTER<BR>
AND FISCAL 2004 RESULTS<BR>
~ Party City Reaches $1 Billion in Total Chain-wide Sales ~</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>Rockaway, New Jersey, August&nbsp;19, 2004 &#150; Party City Corporation (Nasdaq:
PCTY), </B>America&#146;s largest party goods chain, today announced its operating
results for the fourth quarter and fiscal year ended July&nbsp;3, 2004. Unless
otherwise stated, the financial results presented in this press release for the
fourth quarter and year ended July&nbsp;3, 2004 are based on a 14-week and 53-week
period, respectively, while the financial results for the fourth quarter and
year ended June&nbsp;28, 2003 are based on a 13-week and 52-week period,
respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>Quarterly results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net sales for Company-owned stores increased 4.2% to $120.6&nbsp;million for
the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004, up from $115.6&nbsp;million for the fourth
quarter of fiscal 2003. Total chain-wide net sales (which include aggregate net
sales for the collective group of Company-owned and franchise stores) increased
6.3% to $244.6&nbsp;million for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004, up from $230.2
million for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2003. Same-store net sales for
Company-owned stores decreased 4.7% (on a comparative 14-week basis<SUP style="font-size: 80%; vertical-align: top;">1</SUP>), compared
with an increase of 5.8% in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2003. Same-store net
sales for franchise stores decreased 1.5% (on a comparative 14-week basis),
compared with an increase of 4.8% in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004 was $2.1&nbsp;million, or
$0.11 per diluted share, compared with $586,000, or $0.03 per diluted share, in
the same period last year (which included charges

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for fiscal year 2004 was $13.9&nbsp;million, or $0.71 per diluted
share, compared with $12.1&nbsp;million, or $0.62 per diluted share, for fiscal
2003. Gross profit as a percentage of net sales for fiscal 2004 was 33.0%,
relatively flat with last year, with a 0.4% increase in gross merchandise
margin offset by an equal increase in occupancy costs as a percent of sales.
Merchandise clearance and inventory reduction initiatives throughout fiscal
2004 led to a 15.4% decrease in average inventory per store compared with the
same period last year. Store operating and selling expense as a percentage of
net sales for fiscal 2004 decreased to 22.8% from 23.3% for the same period
last year, primarily due to lower payroll expense. General and administrative
expense as a percentage of net sales for fiscal 2004 was 8.0% as compared with
7.0% for the same period last year primarily due to the $4.1&nbsp;million litigation
charge recorded in the third quarter of Fiscal 2004, relating to the tentative
settlement of a California class action litigation regarding overtime wage and
hour laws.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Franchise profit contribution increased 9.5% to $12.9&nbsp;million for fiscal
2004 as compared with $11.8&nbsp;million for the same period last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net interest expense decreased to $471,000 for fiscal 2004 from $4.0
million for the same period last year, primarily due to the repurchase of
outstanding senior notes during the second quarter of fiscal 2003 as well as
lower average borrowings during fiscal 2004.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter, our Company-owned net sales increased 4.2&nbsp;percent. This

stores joining the Company this past year, partially offset by the negative
same-store sales of 4.7&nbsp;percent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Same-store net sales decreased on a regional basis 2&nbsp;percent in the West, 3.7

new store in the same period last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Our cost of goods sold and occupancy expenses increased 1.5&nbsp;percent. This is

store operating expenses decreased 2.7&nbsp;percent for the quarter, to 26.4&nbsp;million
from 27.1&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Operating expenses as a percentage of sales decreased to 21.9&nbsp;percent from 23.4
percent last year as a result of increased sales, a decrease in store physical

efficiencies in payroll and advertising spending.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Store contribution increased to 12.3&nbsp;million from 7.9&nbsp;million last year. Mature
stores played an important role in the increase, rising 3.3&nbsp;million or 74

Halloween season produced a $600,000 increase compared to last year. The 12
stores in the Seattle market that were acquired in 2002 rose $250,000 in store
contributions, or 5.7&nbsp;percent of the increase compared to last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Our general and administrative expenses increased 19.1&nbsp;percent to 11&nbsp;million &#151;

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>PARTY CITY CORPORATION ANNOUNCES</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>CHIEF FINANCIAL OFFICER TRANSITION</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>Rockaway, New Jersey, August&nbsp;19, 2004 &#151; Party City Corporation (Nasdaq:
PCTY), </B>America&#146;s largest party goods chain, today announced that Linda M.
Siluk, the Company&#146;s Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, plans
to leave Party City in September&nbsp;2004 to pursue other opportunities.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Party City has chosen a successor who will take office in September. The
Company plans to announce the new Senior Vice President and Chief Financial
Officer in mid-September, once he has transitioned his responsibilities with
his current employer.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nancy Pedot, Chief Executive Officer of Party City Corporation, said, &#147;I
want to recognize and thank Linda Siluk for her contributions to our growth and
progress during her more than five years with Party City, including two years
as our Chief Financial Officer. We appreciate her willingness to assist with
this transition and wish her every success in her future opportunities.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ms.&nbsp;Pedot added, &#147;Our Board of Directors has successfully concluded a
comprehensive search for a new Chief Financial Officer. Our new CFO is a
highly qualified financial executive, and we are confident that his skills and
experience, along with our existing solid finance team, will help advance our
strategies to build shareholder value.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Party City Corporation is America&#146;s largest party goods chain. Party City
currently operates 249 Company-owned stores and has 257 franchise stores in the
United States and Puerto Rico. To learn more about Party City, visit the
Company&#146;s website at http://www.partycity.com.
