<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>Reynolds American Issues 2004 Earnings Guidance</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>WINSTON-SALEM, N.C. &#150; Sept. 15, 2004 &#150; </B>Reynolds American Inc. (NYSE: RAI) today
issued earnings guidance for 2004. This is the first full-year earnings
guidance issued by Reynolds American since becoming a publicly traded company
on July&nbsp;30, 2004, following a combination of the nation&#146;s No.&nbsp;2 and No.&nbsp;3
cigarette manufacturers, R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Company and the U.S. business of
Brown &#038; Williamson Tobacco Corporation (B&#038;W). Also on that date, Reynolds
American acquired Lane Limited from British American Tobacco, p.l.c. (BAT).


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;In the six weeks since we completed the merger, we&#146;ve made considerable
progress in integrating R.J. Reynolds and the U.S. business of B&#038;W,&#148; said Susan
Ivey, Reynolds American&#146;s chief executive officer. &#147;The marketing and sales
organizations have already been consolidated, and we have begun developing a
combined brand-portfolio strategy that we plan to implement in early 2005. I
am very pleased with the passion and commitment that all of our employees are
bringing to the new, combined company.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Dianne M. Neal, Reynolds American&#146;s chief financial officer, said that Reynolds
American&#146;s 2004 earnings guidance reflects the pre-merger results of R.J.
Reynolds Tobacco Holdings, Inc. (RJR)&nbsp;and the consolidated RAI earnings for
August through December.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Today&#146;s guidance includes previously announced cost reductions and
merger-related synergies,&#148; Neal said. &#147;The $1&nbsp;billion cost-reduction program
that RJR began in 2003 will be at least 90&nbsp;percent complete by year-end.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Additionally,&#148; she said, &#147;we are beginning to capture merger-related synergies
that will build to $550&nbsp;million to $600&nbsp;million through 2006. These
cost-savings initiatives, as well as incremental earnings from the new
businesses partially offset by merger-related costs, are contributing to
forecasted 2004 operating income of $1&nbsp;billion to $1.05&nbsp;billion, net income of
$645&nbsp;million to $675&nbsp;million, and diluted earnings per share of $5.75 to
$6.05.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On an after-tax basis, Neal noted that net income included an expected benefit
of approximately $90&nbsp;million associated with the resolution of prior-year tax
matters.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Neal said that Reynolds American expects to end the year with total debt of
$1.6&nbsp;billion to $1.7&nbsp;billion, and cash and short-term investments of
approximately $1.8&nbsp;billion. She also said that, as required by the company&#146;s
agreement with BAT, Reynolds American will repurchase shares to offset stock
options that are exercised through June&nbsp;2005. The company expects that less
than 1&nbsp;percent of its outstanding shares would be repurchased under this
agreement.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We have taken significant steps to begin creating positive momentum, and we
are pleased with our progress,&#148; Neal said. &#147;The creation of Reynolds American
Inc. provides a significant opportunity to build shareholder value, and we look
forward to reporting RAI&#146;s quarterly earnings results in late October.&#148;
