<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">(a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; At a special meeting of the Audit Committee of the Board of Directors of
the Company on September&nbsp;22, 2004, the Committee determined, based on the
advice of senior management and the Company&#146;s independent public accounting
firm, that the Company had not properly accounted for deferred compensation
payable to the Company&#146;s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer pursuant to an
employment agreement which had been entered into during the first fiscal
quarter of 2004. The employment agreement had been publicly disclosed on March
12, 2004 as Exhibit&nbsp;10.3 to the Company&#146;s Annual Report on Form 10-K for the
fiscal year ended December&nbsp;31, 2003. The Audit Committee concluded at the
September&nbsp;22 meeting that the Company&#146;s consolidated financial statements for
the quarters ended March&nbsp;31 and June&nbsp;30, 2004 should be restated and that the
previously disclosed financial statements for such periods should not be relied
upon.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The accounting error related to deferred compensation payments, totaling $3.0
million and payable ratably over 36&nbsp;months beginning in July&nbsp;2005, which should
have been recorded as current compensation expense in the quarter ended March
31, 2004. In revised interim financial statements expected to be filed on or
about September&nbsp;23, 2004 on Form 10-Q/A for the three months ended March&nbsp;31,
2004 and the six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, the discounted value, of $2.5
million for the three-month period ended March&nbsp;31, 2004 and $2.6&nbsp;million for
the six-month period ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, of these payments is reflected as
compensation expense, and corresponding adjustments have been recorded to the
Company&#146;s shareholders&#146; equity and earnings (loss)&nbsp;per share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In connection with its ongoing internal controls initiatives relating to the
Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, management of the Company is revising and enhancing
its controls relating to the approval of material contracts in order to prevent
similar errors in future.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">(b)&nbsp;and (c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not applicable.
