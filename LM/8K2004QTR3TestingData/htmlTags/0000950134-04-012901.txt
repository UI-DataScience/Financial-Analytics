<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ACE CASH EXPRESS REPORTS RECORD FISCAL 2004 RESULTS<BR>
PROVIDES FISCAL 2005 GUIDANCE</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;DALLAS (August&nbsp;26, 2004)&#151;ACE Cash Express, Inc. (NASDAQ:AACE) announced
fiscal 2004 net income of $17.1&nbsp;million or $1.49 per diluted share compared to
fiscal 2003 net income of $12.8&nbsp;million or $1.25 per diluted share, an increase
of 33.5&nbsp;percent and 18.7&nbsp;percent, respectively. Fiscal 2004 results included a
one-time charge of $0.25 per diluted share, related to the early extinguishment
of debt from the Company&#146;s successful secondary offering of common stock in
April&nbsp;2004. Excluding this charge, ACE&#146;s fiscal 2004 earnings per diluted
share increased 39.0&nbsp;percent to $1.74, from fiscal 2003 earnings per diluted
share of $1.25. During fiscal 2004, ACE&#146;s total revenue increased 5.3&nbsp;percent
to $246.7&nbsp;million compared to $234.3&nbsp;million in the prior year, due primarily
to a 4.1&nbsp;percent increase in check cashing fees, an 8.8&nbsp;percent increase in
loan fees and interest, and a 25.6&nbsp;percent increase in bill payment services.

opening of eleven ACE Cash Advance stores, and the testing of several new
marketing programs,&#148; said Jay B. Shipowitz, President and Chief Executive
Officer. &#147;In addition, we were able to substantially improve our balance sheet
with the

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fiscal Fourth Quarter Results</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fiscal fourth quarter 2004 net income was $2.2&nbsp;million or $0.16 per
diluted share compared to fiscal fourth quarter 2003 net income of $5.3&nbsp;million
or $0.52 per diluted share. Fiscal fourth quarter 2003 results included a
one-time gain of $0.27 per diluted share from liability insurance proceeds
related to the Goleta National Bank loan-related lawsuits. Fiscal fourth
quarter 2004 results included a one-time charge of $0.22 per diluted share
related to the early extinguishment of debt. Excluding these two items, ACE&#146;s
fiscal fourth quarter 2004 earnings per diluted share increased 57.1&nbsp;percent to
$0.39 compared to the fiscal fourth quarter 2003 earnings per diluted share of
$0.25. During the fiscal fourth quarter of 2004, ACE&#146;s total revenue improved
8.1&nbsp;percent to $58.1&nbsp;million versus $53.8&nbsp;million in the prior year period, due
primarily to a 23.5&nbsp;percent increase in loan fees and interest, and a 22.0
percent increase in bill payment services.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About ACE</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ACE Cash Express, Inc. is a leading retailer of financial services, including
check cashing, short-term consumer loans and bill payment services, and the
largest owner, operator and franchisor of check cashing stores in the United
States. As of June&nbsp;30, 2004, ACE had a network of 1,230 stores in 36 states
and the District of Columbia, consisting of 1,026 company-owned stores and 204
franchised stores. ACE focuses on serving unbanked and underbanked consumers,
many of whom seek alternatives to traditional banking relationships in order to
gain convenient and immediate access to check cashing services and short-term
consumer loans. ACE&#146;s website is found at www.acecashexpress.com.
