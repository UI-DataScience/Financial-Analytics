<B><P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">Item 1.01.  Entry Into a Material Definitive Agreement.</P>
</B><P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">Stage Stores, Inc. (the "Company") previously announced that Vivian McDonald would be retiring as Executive Vice President, General Merchandise Manager after approximately 20 years with the Company.  As a result of her retirement, the Company and Ms. McDonald signed a Severance Agreement - Release of All Claims (the "Severance Agreement") on August 25, 2004. The Severance Agreement became effective and enforceable on September 1, 2004.</P>
<P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">Consistent with the terms and conditions of her Employment Agreement with the Company dated January 30, 2002, the Severance Agreement provides that the Company
(i) will make specified severance payments to Ms. McDonald, and (ii) will continue health and life insurance coverage for Ms. McDonald for one year.  The Company does not believe that the amount of the severance payments to be paid Ms. McDonald and the cost of the benefits to be maintained on her behalf are material to the Company.</P>
<B><P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">Item 1.02.</B>  <B>Termination of a Material Definitive Agreement.</P>
</B><P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">Effective September 4, 2004, the Company terminated the Employment Agreement dated January 30, 2002 between the Company and Vivian McDonald (formerly, Baker) due to Ms. McDonald's retirement from the Company and the Severance Agreement described in Item 1.01.</P>

<B><FONT SIZE=5><P ALIGN="CENTER">STAGE STORES REPORTS AUGUST SALES</P>
<P ALIGN="CENTER">ANNOUNCES PARTICIPATION AT ROTH CAPITAL PARTNERS AND THOMAS WEISEL PARTNERS CONFERENCES</P>
<P ALIGN="CENTER"></FONT><I>-- Comparable store sales increase 8.1% --</I><FONT SIZE=5> </P>
</B></FONT>
<P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">HOUSTON, TX, September 2, 2004 - Stage Stores, Inc. (Nasdaq: STGS) today reported that total sales for the four-week period ended August 28, 2004 increased 47.8% to $105.7 million from $71.5 million in the prior year four-week period ended August 30, 2003.  Comparable store sales increased 8.1% versus a decrease of 5.7% last year.  The Company noted that total sales include sales for the Peebles stores in the current year's results only, while comparable store sales include comparable store sales for the Peebles stores in both years.  The Company also noted that the current year's comparable store sales benefited from the calendar shift in the Texas sales tax holiday weekend from the July period last year to the August period this year.</P>
<P ALIGN="JUSTIFY">The Company further reported that almost all of its categories of business had comparable store sales increases during August, with the only major category exceptions being cosmetics and home &amp; gifts.  Leading the way with double digit comparable store sales increases during the month were children's (+16.3%), footwear (+13.4%), special sizes (+11.4%), men's (+10.6%) and dresses (+10.2%).</P>
