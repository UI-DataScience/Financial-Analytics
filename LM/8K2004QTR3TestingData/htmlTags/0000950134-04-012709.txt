<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>REGIS REPORTS RECORD FOURTH QUARTER AND FISCAL YEAR 2004 RESULTS</B><BR>
- -Fiscal Year 2004 EPS Increased 19 Percent to $2.29-



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MINNEAPOLIS, August&nbsp;24, 2004 &#151; Regis Corporation (NYSE:RGS), the
global leader in the $140&nbsp;billion hair care industry, today reported
record financial results for its fiscal fourth quarter and year ended June
30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fourth Quarter Results</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Consolidated revenues increased 13&nbsp;percent to a record $509&nbsp;million
compared to $448&nbsp;million a year ago. Same-store sales increased 1.5
percent. Fourth quarter net income increased 21&nbsp;percent to $27.3&nbsp;million,
or $0.59 per diluted share. During the quarter, Regis Corporation added a
net total of 276 salons. The Company constructed 137 salons and
franchisees built 74 salons. In addition, 167 salons were acquired,
including 7 franchise buybacks. A total of 95 salons were closed or
relocated during the quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fiscal Year 2004 Results</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Consolidated revenues increased 14&nbsp;percent to a record $1.92&nbsp;billion
compared to $1.68&nbsp;billion during fiscal year 2003. Same-store sales
increased 2.6&nbsp;percent. Net income increased 22&nbsp;percent to a record $105
million, or $2.29 per diluted share. During the year, the Company
constructed 452 salons and franchisees built 268 salons. In addition, 411
salons were acquired, including 206 franchise buybacks. A total of 380
salons were closed or relocated during the year. As of June&nbsp;30, 2004,
Regis Corporation owned or franchised 10,162 salons compared to 9,617
salons as of June&nbsp;30, 2003, a net increase of 545 salons.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Fiscal 2004 represented another year of record performance for Regis
Corporation. Our flexible strategy of adding salons organically and by
acquisition allows us to generate consistent revenue growth year after
year,&#148; commented Paul D. Finkelstein, chairman and chief executive
officer. &#147;This strategy enables us to achieve, and sometimes exceed, our
long-term objective of low-to-mid teens earnings growth.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Finkelstein further commented, &#147;Strong cash flow generated in
fiscal 2004 allowed Regis Corporation to strengthen its balance sheet. We
reduced our debt-to-capitalization ratio by 440 basis points to 30.5
percent and have now reduced this ratio by over 1500 basis points in the
last four years.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fiscal Year 2005 Outlook</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Guidance for fiscal 2005 remains unchanged. Excluding the benefit
from future acquisitions, revenue for fiscal year 2005 is expected to
increase 7-to-8&nbsp;percent to nearly $2.1&nbsp;billion, with
