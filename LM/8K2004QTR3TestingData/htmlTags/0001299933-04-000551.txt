<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><FONT style="font-size=12pt"><B>COLE NATIONAL REPORTS STRONG OPERATING AND FINANCIAL RESULTS FOR SECOND QUARTER OF FISCAL YEAR<BR>
2004</B></FONT>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 12pt"><FONT style="font-size=9pt"></FONT>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 9pt; text-indent: 4%"><FONT style="font-size=11pt"><B>Cleveland, Ohio, September&nbsp;8, 2004 </B>&#151; Cole National Corporation (NYSE: CNJ), a leading
retailer of optical services and personalized gifts with over 2,900 locations throughout North
America and the Caribbean and one of the nation&#146;s largest providers of managed vision care
benefits, today announced results for the second quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004. The Company filed
its Form 10-Q for the quarter on September&nbsp;7, 2004.</FONT>



<P align="left" style="margin-left:5%; font-size: 11pt"><B>Financial and Operating Highlights for the Second Quarter of Fiscal 2004</B>


<P>
<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">

<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 11pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="7%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>Revenue rose in the second quarter of fiscal 2004 to $312.8&nbsp;million from $307.7
million in the second quarter of fiscal 2003. The Company reported net income of
$1.0&nbsp;million, or $.05 per diluted share, compared to a net loss of $5.5&nbsp;million, or
$.34 per diluted share, for the same period in 2003.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>
<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">

<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 11pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="7%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>The second quarter&#146;s pre-tax results include $5.5&nbsp;million in unusual items -
including expenses related to the Company&#146;s evaluation of strategic alternatives and
compliance costs associated with the Federal Trade Commission&#146;s request for
information &#151; compared to $11.4&nbsp;million of unusual expenses in last year&#146;s second
quarter.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>
<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">

<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 11pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="7%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>The Company&#146;s consolidated same store sales decreased 0.5% in the second quarter
of 2004. Same store sales in the Company&#146;s gift segment increased 1.9%. Same store
sales in the Company&#146;s vision segment decreased 1.3%.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>
<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">

<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 11pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="7%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>On July&nbsp;22, 2004 the Company&#146;s stockholders approved the merger agreement between
Luxottica Group and Cole National for a cash purchase price of $27.50, plus 4%
interest per annum from the approval date through the closing.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Larry Pollock, President and CEO, commented, &#147;Cole National posted strong operating and
financial results for the second quarter of fiscal 2004. Even after accounting for the fact that
unusual expenses decreased by $5.9&nbsp;million, operating earnings rose significantly over last year&#146;s
same period. This improvement was achieved despite the slowdown in the economy that affected many
retailers.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">&#147;Overall, same store sales at Cole Licensed Brands decreased 2.0% in the second quarter of
fiscal 2004. At Sears Optical, same store sales fell 4.0%, as our promotional offers were not as
impactful as in previous quarters and we did not repeat last year&#146;s contact lens offer. In the
second half of fiscal 2004, we have repositioned Sears Optical&#146;s merchandise assortment and plan
to drive transactions more aggressively by offering the customer greater value on a wider</FONT><FONT style="font-size=12pt">
</FONT><FONT style="font-size=11pt">array of frames and lenses.</FONT>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">&#147;Same store sales at Target Optical rose 4.7% in the second quarter of fiscal 2004,
reflecting our success in providing Target guests with the fashionable merchandise they seek at
reasonable prices. The average revenue per spectacle transaction increased by 2.4%.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">&#147;BJ&#146;s Optical&#146;s same store sales rose 11.0% in this year&#146;s second quarter. This positive
trend is a result of our improved in-store presentation, which emphasizes fashion and value to the
customer, and the continued popularity of our &#145;Two for $98.00&#146; promotional offer.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">&#147;At Pearle Vision, consolidated same store sales in the U.S. company-owned and franchise
stores decreased 1.7% in the second quarter of fiscal 2004, falling 2.0% at Company-owned stores
and 1.4% at franchise stores. However, average spectacle transaction rose as a result of the
increased sale of premium products and lens treatments such as non-glare lens coating. The Pearle
Vision Preferred Credit Card, which enables us to provide extended payment terms to our customers
for up to six months at no cost, continued to grow as a percent of sales, accounting for almost
14% of all sales at Company-owned stores during the quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">&#147;Cole Managed Vision turned in another strong performance in the second quarter of fiscal
2004. Revenue growth was driven by an increase in the number of capitated plans sold to
employers, health plans and associations, higher participation in existing voluntary plans, higher
revenue from fee-for-service programs, and increased laser procedure volume. Lower benefit
utilization of funded programs contributed to Cole Managed Vision&#146;s improved margin performance
compared to last year&#146;s second quarter.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Mr.&nbsp;Pollock continued, &#147;Things Remembered performed well during the second quarter of fiscal
2004, increasing same store sales 1.9% while the average revenue per transaction rose 8.2%. In
addition, the gross margin rate improved due to higher levels of personalization. Our direct
channel business, which includes catalogs and the Internet, generated substantial growth in
earnings and revenue that contributed to Things Remembered&#146;s improved operating earnings.
Membership in our Rewards Club continued to rise during the period, reaching 1.2&nbsp;million members
at the end of the second quarter. Our collection of products designed exclusively for Things
Remembered continued to be very well received.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Mr.&nbsp;Pollock added, &#147;I am very pleased with the hard work, dedication and focus our field and
home office associates have shown during the period that the merger has been pending. They have
continued to put the customer first and foremost, thus delivering products, service and
performance of which we can all be proud.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Pending Merger with Luxottica</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">As described in the Company&#146;s press release dated July&nbsp;22, 2004, Cole National&#146;s stockholders
voted to approve the merger agreement, as amended, with Luxottica Group S.p.A.. Pursuant to the
terms of an amendment to the Luxottica merger agreement dated July&nbsp;14, 2004, Cole National
stockholders will receive $27.50 per share in cash, plus an additional amount equal to 4% per annum
from July&nbsp;22 through the closing date of the merger, upon completion of the transaction.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">The Luxottica merger is subject to receipt of regulatory approvals and other customary
conditions. As previously announced, Cole National and Luxottica have committed to the Federal
Trade Commission not to close the transaction before September&nbsp;30, 2004 without its consent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Financial Results</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Revenues for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 rose 1.7% to $312.8&nbsp;million from $307.7&nbsp;million
during the second quarter of fiscal 2003. This increase was primarily attributable to revenue
increases in managed vision care and Things Remembered, partially offset by a 1.3% decrease in same
store sales in the Company&#146;s vision segment and a reduction in the number of Pearle Vision
corporate stores.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">The gross margin rate for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 increased to 62.9% from 62.5% in
last year&#146;s same period. Cole Vision&#146;s gross margin rate increased to 60.0% in the second quarter
compared to 59.4% in the second quarter of fiscal 2003. The gross margin rate for Cole Vision was
impacted by many factors, including higher contact lens gross margin rate, lower levels of
inventory shrink and obsolescence, higher manufacturing productivity, lower benefit utilization by
funded plan members at Cole Managed Vision and a reduction in the reserve percentage used for
managed vision underwriting gains to reflect recent experience. Partially offsetting the gains were
a higher mix of sales to franchisees, which carry a lower gross margin but offer other benefits,
including producing a more uniform merchandise assortment and consistent brand look, and increased
promotional activity at Pearle Vision. Things Remembered&#146;s gross margin rate increased in the
second quarter of fiscal 2004 as a result of an increase in personalization sales, which carry a
high gross margin rate.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Operating expenses as a percent of sales improved to 58.7% for the second quarter of fiscal
2004 compared to 62.8% in the same period last year. The improvement was driven by a lower level of
unusual expenses, productivity gains in store expense and non-store expense at Things Remembered
and Pearle Vision, and leverage gains in administrative expenses due to revenue increases at Cole
Managed Vision. These improvements were partially offset by a decrease in operating leverage in
Cole Licensed Brands due to negative same store sales in Sears Optical.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Net income for the second quarter of fiscal 2004 was $.05 per diluted share compared to a net
loss of $.34 per diluted share last year. The improvement was driven by improved operating income
in both segments and a lower level of unusual expenses. These trends were partially offset by lower
levels of foreign currency gains, lower interest income resulting from the repayment in the fourth
quarter of fiscal 2003 of certain loans previously made by the Company and a higher estimated
income tax rate resulting from the non-deductibility of certain transaction related expenses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Revenues for the first six months of fiscal 2004 increased 4.1% to $620.4&nbsp;million compared to
$595.9&nbsp;million in the first six months of fiscal 2003. The growth in revenue was primarily a
result of increases in managed vision care and a 2.2% increase in the vision segment&#146;s same store
sales and a 3.8% increase in Things Remembered&#146;s same store sales.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">The gross margin rate for the first six months of fiscal 2004 was 63.0%, up from 62.7% in the
first six months of fiscal 2003. This improvement was a result of the same factors that caused the
increase in gross margin rate in the second quarter of fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Operating expenses as a percent to sales improved to 60.4% for the first six months of fiscal
2004 compared to 63.3% in the same period last year. The decrease was the primarily the result of a
lower level of unusual expenses and operating leverage gains in both business segments.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Net income for the first six months of fiscal 2004 was $.03 per diluted share compared to a
net loss of $.73 per diluted share last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Non-GAAP Financial Measure</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">As a retailer, the Company believes that a measure of same store sales performance is
important for understanding its operations. Same store sales growth is a non-GAAP financial
measure of performance at stores open at least 12&nbsp;months, which includes deferred warranty sales
on a cash basis and undelivered customer orders, and does not reflect provisions for returns,
remakes, and certain other items. Adjustments to the cash basis sales information accumulated at
the store level are made for these items on an aggregate basis. This measure is consistent with
the measures previously used in the Company&#146;s reports. A reconciliation of same store sales to
revenue is set forth in Schedule&nbsp;II.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt"><B>Certain Operating and Expense Trends</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Consolidated same store sales in the Company&#146;s vision segment declined in the second quarter
of 2004, although there was variability within the Company&#146;s brands, ranging from single-digit
increases at Target Optical and low double-digit increases at BJ&#146;s Optical to low single-digit
decreases at Sears Optical and Pearle Vision. During August, the first month of the third quarter
of fiscal 2004, consolidated same store sales in the Company&#146;s vision segment increased in the low
double-digits. While there was growth in all brands, sales performance in August was driven
primarily by a successful promotion at Sears Optical. Things Remembered experienced a same store
sales decline in August of less than 1%. There can be no assurance that these same store sales
trends will continue for the remainder of the third quarter of fiscal 2004 or the remainder of the
fiscal year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt; text-indent: 4%">Costs associated with the California litigation, the merger agreement with Luxottica Group and
compliance with the FTC&#146;s request for additional information and documentary material with respect
to the Luxottica merger, and the SEC investigation are expected to continue in fiscal 2004.
However, many of the unusual expenses, such as the cost related to the settlement of the
stockholder litigation and audit fees related to the restatement, are not expected to recur.
Although there can be no assurances regarding the level of same store sales, the Company believes
its profit performance will improve in fiscal 2004 as a result of revenue growth, expense controls
and the non-recurrence of the aforementioned unusual expenses.
