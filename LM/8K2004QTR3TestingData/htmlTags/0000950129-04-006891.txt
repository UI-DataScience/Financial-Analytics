<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>STEWART ENTERPRISES REPORTS INCREASE IN EARNINGS<BR>
TO $.10 PER SHARE FOR THIRD QUARTER OF FISCAL 2004</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><HR size="1" noshade width="100%" align="left">


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">METAIRIE, LA, September&nbsp;8, 2004 . . . Stewart Enterprises, Inc. (Nasdaq NMS:
STEI) reported net earnings of $10.8&nbsp;million or $.10 per diluted share for the
quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004. Earnings from continuing operations for the
quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 were $10.4&nbsp;million or $.10 per diluted share,
including charges for separation pay of $1.1&nbsp;million ($0.6&nbsp;million after tax,
or $.01 per diluted share) offset by a reduction in the 2003 impairment charge
(included in other income) of $0.6&nbsp;million ($0.6&nbsp;million after tax, or $.01 per
diluted share). Excluding the charges for separation pay and the reduction in
the 2003 impairment charge, earnings from continuing operations would have also
been $10.4&nbsp;million or $.10 per diluted share.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2003, the Company reported a net loss of $2.3
million or $.02 per diluted share from continuing operations, resulting
primarily from a loss on early extinguishment of debt of $11.3&nbsp;million ($7.3
million after tax, or $.07 per diluted share) related to the redemption of all
of the outstanding Remarketable Or Redeemable Securities (&#147;ROARS&#148;) and a charge
for separation pay of $2.5&nbsp;million ($1.5&nbsp;million after tax, or $.01 per diluted
share). Excluding these charges, earnings from continuing operations would have
been $6.5&nbsp;million or $.06 per diluted share in the third quarter of 2003.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Earnings from continuing operations for the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004
were $34.5&nbsp;million or $.32 per diluted share, including charges for separation
pay of $3.2&nbsp;million ($2.0&nbsp;million after tax, or $.02 per diluted share),
partially offset by a reduction in the 2003 impairment charge (included in
other income) of $0.5&nbsp;million ($0.7&nbsp;million after tax, or $.01 per diluted
share). Earnings from continuing operations for the nine months ended July&nbsp;31,
2003 were $16.2&nbsp;million or $.15 per diluted share, including a loss on early
extinguishment of debt of $11.3&nbsp;million ($7.3&nbsp;million after tax, or $.07 per
diluted share) and a charge for separation pay of $2.5&nbsp;million ($1.5&nbsp;million
after tax, or $.01 per diluted share). Excluding these charges and the
reduction in the 2003 impairment charge, earnings from continuing operations
would have been $35.8&nbsp;million or $.33 per diluted share in the first nine
months of 2004 compared to $25.0&nbsp;million or $.23 per diluted share for the
comparable period in 2003, representing a 44&nbsp;percent increase.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Kenneth C. Budde, Chief Executive Officer, stated, &#147;The fiscal year continues
to be a successful one for our Company and our shareholders. I am very pleased
that we continued to produce solid results in the quarter and remain on track
to achieve the goals we established in December&nbsp;2003. When we developed our
key operating initiatives about a year ago, we defined the paths that we
thought would lead to long-term growth, and our results for three quarters have
been consistent with that vision. We have been successful in our execution of
those plans and, although funeral calls and trust earnings declined in the most
recent quarter, we experienced a 3.1&nbsp;percent increase in total revenues and a
200 basis-point improvement in both our cemetery and funeral margins. We are
particularly pleased that our effective control of costs throughout the
organization positively impacted our bottom line, despite economic challenges,
including weak financial markets and fewer deaths in some of our markets. In
addition, we continued to generate healthy preneed sales growth in both
segments.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the third quarter of fiscal year 2004, the Company&#146;s same store funeral
operations achieved a 2.3&nbsp;percent increase in the average revenue per
traditional funeral service performed and a 5.7&nbsp;percent increase in the average
revenue per cremation service. These gains were partially offset by a
year-over-year reduction in funeral trust earnings and an increase in the
proportion of non-traditional funeral services, including cremations, resulting
in an overall 1.0&nbsp;percent increase in the average revenue per funeral service
for the quarter. The contribution of trust earnings recognized as revenue upon
the delivery of preneed funerals was lower in 2004 than in the comparable
period of 2003 due to lower investment returns realized in the Company&#146;s
preneed funeral trust funds during previous years.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company is on track to achieve its fiscal year forecast of 2.0 to 3.0
percent growth in average revenue per funeral service, excluding any impact
from funeral trust earnings. For the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, the
Company&#146;s same store funeral operations achieved a 3.6&nbsp;percent increase in the
average revenue per traditional funeral service performed and a 4.9&nbsp;percent
increase in the average revenue per cremation service, resulting in an overall
1.6&nbsp;percent increase in the average revenue per funeral service performed,
including the contribution from trust earnings.

the Company&#146;s 228 same store funeral homes experienced a total decrease of 165
events out of the 46,467 total same store events performed over the nine-month
period. The decline in volume, combined with the reduction in funeral trust
earnings and the increase in the proportion of non-traditional services,
including cremations, resulted in a $1.5&nbsp;million decrease in funeral revenue
during the third quarter.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the third quarter of 2003, the Company increased its focus on preneed
funeral sales and preneed cemetery property sales resulting in solid increases
in both for four consecutive quarters. In the third quarter of 2004, the
Company reported a 4.6&nbsp;percent increase in preneed funeral sales and a 13.2
percent increase in cemetery property sales when compared to the third quarter
of 2003. During the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, preneed funeral sales
increased 9.9&nbsp;percent and cemetery property sales increased 9.3&nbsp;percent, which
is at the high end of the Company&#146;s stated goal of increasing preneed sales by
5 to 10&nbsp;percent during fiscal year 2004.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cemetery revenue increased $5.4&nbsp;million during the third quarter of 2004
compared to the third quarter of 2003 primarily due to the increase in cemetery
property sales, partially offset by a decrease in revenue from perpetual care
trust earnings. The cemetery margin for the quarter was 22.6&nbsp;percent,
representing a 200 basis-point increase over the third quarter of fiscal year
2003.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Mr.&nbsp;Budde added, &#147;Our cemetery segment has performed well this quarter. During
the quarter, we converted strong revenue growth into even stronger margin
growth by continuing to focus on our cost-saving initiatives.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cash flow from operations for the first nine months of 2004 was $68.6&nbsp;million,
and free cash flow was $55.7&nbsp;million, including a cash inflow of $33.2&nbsp;million
from a tax refund received during the first quarter of fiscal year 2004 and
cash outflows of $1.9&nbsp;million for separation pay. (See tables under
&#147;Reconciliation of Non-GAAP Financial Measures.&#148;) As of September&nbsp;1, 2004, the
Company had outstanding debt of $425.7&nbsp;million and cash and marketable
securities of $21.4&nbsp;million.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Mr.&nbsp;Budde concluded, &#147;Consistent with the first six months of this year, our
businesses performed well during our third quarter. Based on our year-to-date
performance, we believe we will achieve all of the financial targets we set for
the year. We have benefited from the effective implementation of our key
operating initiatives throughout the year. Our margins are up substantially,
and we continue to generate free cash, which is being used to further reduce
debt and repurchase stock. We fully expect to complete the year with a solid
balance sheet and cash flow, and our strong performance through the first nine
months of fiscal 2004 should continue through the fourth quarter and into
2005.&#148;
