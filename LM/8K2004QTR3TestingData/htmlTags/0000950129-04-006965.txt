<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>EDELBROCK CORP. REPORTS RESULTS FOR<BR>
FOURTH QUARTER AND FISCAL YEAR 2004</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">TORRANCE, Calif. &#150; September&nbsp;10, 2004 &#150; Edelbrock Corporation (Nasdaq NM:
EDEL) today reported sales and earnings for its fiscal 2004 fourth quarter
and year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, revenues totaled $38,030,000 as
compared to revenues of $32,678,000 in the same period of fiscal 2003.
Net income for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004 was $1,320,000, or $0.24
per diluted share, compared to net income of $709,000, or $0.13 per
diluted share, for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2003. Results for the
quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 reflected professional fees and similar costs
associated with the proposed &#147;going-private&#148; transaction totaling
$496,000.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004, revenues totaled $125,980,000 compared
to revenues of $115,225,000 in fiscal 2003. Net income for fiscal 2004
was $3,979,000, or $0.72 per diluted share, compared to net income of
$2,970,000, or $0.54 per diluted share, for fiscal 2003. Results for the
year ended June&nbsp;30, 2004 reflected gains on the sale of real estate assets
totaling $445,000 (approximately $282,000 or $0.05 per diluted share,
after tax) and professional fees related to the proposed &#147;going-private&#148;
transaction totaling $496,000.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Edelbrock Corp. Reports Results for 4Q and Fiscal Year 2004 </B>- Page 2

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Edelbrock attributed its increase in sales for both the fourth quarter and
fiscal 2004 to improvements in the national economy, favorable weather
conditions that encouraged enthusiasts to perform desired upgrades, and
continued strong customer acceptance of the Edelbrock brand and products,
reinforced by Edelbrock&#146;s marketing program. Despite Edelbrock&#146;s strong
performance in the fiscal 2004 periods, however, management noted that
earnings growth continued to be hampered by continuing increases in
worker&#146;s compensation, utilities, and overall insurance costs.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Edelbrock reported that revenues for the fourth quarter were strong across
a broad range of product categories. Sales of Edelbrock&#146;s
high-performance aluminum automotive cylinder heads rose $1.4&nbsp;million, or
32.1% over the year-ago period, while sales of Edelbrock&#146;s aluminum
automotive intake manifolds improved $1.2&nbsp;million, or 17.2%. Other lines
that made notable contributions to Edelbrock&#146;s quarterly sales growth
included its popular automotive carburetors, up 4.7%; exhaust systems, up
20.0%; and shock absorbers rose 7.8% for the quarter. Edelbrock also
received a $798,000, or 49.6% quarterly sales increase from its Russell
Performance Products division. The Edelbrock Russell brand is a leading
provider of performance plumbing and brake lines which includes street
legal brake lines, oil lines, fuel lines, and filters for both automotive
and motorcycle use, as well as other products for domestic and imported
vehicles. In addition, Edelbrock reported an increase in third party
casting work at its aluminum foundry, sales from which improved $263,000,
or 25.9% from the fourth quarter of 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Edelbrock reported that selling, general and administrative (SG&#038;A)
expense, as a percentage of revenues, decreased to 28.0% for the fourth
quarter of fiscal 2004 from 29.0% in the year-ago period. Overall, SG&#038;A
increased 12.3%, or $1.2&nbsp;million, to $10.7&nbsp;million for the fourth quarter
of fiscal 2004 compared to $9.5&nbsp;million for the year-ago period. The
quarterly and year-to-year increase in SG&#038;A was primarily attributable to
increased commissions, other variable selling expenses associated with
higher revenues, and professional fees and similar costs associated with
the proposed &#147;going-private&#148; transaction. Research and development (R&#038;D)
expenses for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004 increased 5.1%, or $61,000,
over the year-ago period, totaling $1.2&nbsp;million. As a percent of
revenues, R&#038;D expenses decreased to 3.3% for the fourth quarter of fiscal
2004 from 3.6% in the same quarter of fiscal 2003.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About Edelbrock Corporation:</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Founded in 1938, Torrance, California-based Edelbrock Corp. is recognized
as one of the nation&#146;s premier designers, manufacturers and distributors
of performance replacement parts for the automotive and motorcycle
aftermarkets. In addition to three production facilities and an automated
distribution center in Torrance, the Company owns and operates a
state-of-the-art aluminum foundry and its motorcycle carburetor division
in San Jacinto, Calif., at which it manufactures many of its quality
products.
