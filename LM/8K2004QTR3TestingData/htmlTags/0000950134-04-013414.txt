<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>URS CORPORATION REPORTS THIRD QUARTER<BR>
RESULTS FOR FISCAL 2004</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Results In line With Guidance, Including Previously<BR>
Announced Charge Related to Note Redemptions</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Debt-to-Capitalization Ratio Reduced To 35%</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>SAN FRANCISCO, CA &#150; September&nbsp;9, 2004 </B>&#150; URS Corporation (NYSE: URS) today
reported revenues of $838.2&nbsp;million for the third quarter of fiscal 2004, an
increase of 8% from $778.0&nbsp;million in revenues reported for the third quarter
of fiscal 2003. Net income was $7.3&nbsp;million, compared to $17.1&nbsp;million
reported for the corresponding third quarter of fiscal 2003. Net income for
the third quarter of fiscal 2004 includes a previously announced pre-tax charge
of $27.4&nbsp;million, equivalent to a $0.37 per share reduction in our earnings per
share (&#147;EPS&#148;), related to the Company&#146;s recent public offering of 8.1&nbsp;million
shares of common stock and note redemptions of $250.0&nbsp;million. The charge
consists of cash payments of $19.1&nbsp;million for call premiums and a non-cash
charge of $8.3&nbsp;million for prepaid financing fees, issuance costs and debt
discounts.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Diluted EPS for the third quarter of fiscal 2004 were $0.17 after the
$0.37 per share charge described above. This compares to diluted EPS of $0.52
per share for the same period last year. Weighted-average shares outstanding
for the third quarter of fiscal 2004 for the purposes of calculating diluted
EPS were 44.2&nbsp;million compared to 33.2&nbsp;million weighted-average shares
outstanding for the third quarter of fiscal 2003. The increase is due
primarily to the Company&#146;s

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">public common stock offering and to additional shares issued pursuant to
the Company&#146;s stock option and purchase plans.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, the Company generated operating
cash flow of $87.1&nbsp;million, and repaid $59.5&nbsp;million in debt. In addition, the
Company used $185.2&nbsp;million from its public stock offering to further repay
debt. As a result, the Company&#146;s debt to total capitalization ratio improved
to 35% at July&nbsp;31, 2004, from 52% at October&nbsp;31, 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As of July&nbsp;31, 2004, the Company&#146;s backlog was $3.692&nbsp;billion, compared
to $3.662&nbsp;billion as of October&nbsp;31, 2003.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Commenting on the Company&#146;s financial results, Martin M. Koffel, Chairman
and Chief Executive Officer, stated: &#147;Our results for the quarter reflect
strong performance by our federal sector business, tempered by continued
softness in the state and local government and private sector markets. Our
federal sector business has benefited from increased demand from the Department
of Defense for equipment repair and maintenance services, as well as continuing
strength in our work on technical support services and facilities and
environmental projects. Overall revenues from both the state and local
government and private sectors, however, were slightly lower than expected,
even as we continue to see indications that portions of these markets have
begun to recover. As these markets recover, our diversified business portfolio
and strong relationships with key clients across the country continue to result
in important new assignments for URS.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, revenues increased 5% to $2.475
billion, from $2.349&nbsp;billion for the same period last year. Net income for
the nine months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 was $35.6&nbsp;million, or $0.91 per share
fully diluted, compared to $38.6&nbsp;million, or $1.18 per share fully diluted,
for the same period last year. Weighted-average shares outstanding for the
first nine months of fiscal 2004 for the purposes of calculating diluted EPS
were 38.9&nbsp;million compared to 32.7&nbsp;million weighted-average shares outstanding
for the same period last year.

EG&#038;G Division. The URS Division includes the Company&#146;s work in the state and
local government market, the private sector and the international business. In
addition, the URS Division includes the Company&#146;s federal business that existed
prior to the acquisition of EG&#038;G, which consists primarily of facilities and
environmental work. The EG&#038;G Division primarily serves the federal government
market, providing a range of

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>URS Division. </B>For the third quarter of fiscal 2004, the URS Division

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Outlook for Fiscal 2004</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;URS reaffirmed its expectation that fiscal 2004 revenues will be
approximately $3.3&nbsp;billion. The Company also expects that its fiscal 2004
earnings per share would be $1.90, excluding the $0.37 per share charge related
to the costs of the Company&#146;s note redemptions.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On a GAAP basis, the Company expects net income of approximately $61.8
million, or $1.53 per share, for fiscal 2004. A reconciliation between the
$1.90 per share and the $1.53 per share, showing the effects of the stock
offering and note redemptions, is attached to this release.
