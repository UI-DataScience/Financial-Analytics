levels, with orders up 18.0% and backlog increasing 24.6%. Net earnings were $14.3

pre-tax restructuring charges, less than $.01 per share net of tax, related to previously
announced actions. The ending cash balance was $155.8 million. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>The company&#146;s consolidated sales

year. On a sequential basis, gross margins declined from the 31.8% achieved in the fourth
quarter. Gross margins were positively affected by efficiencies resulting from the Canton,
Georgia, plant consolidation and were negatively impacted by increased raw material costs,
primarily steel. </FONT></P>

was providing ongoing financial support through loans and/or financial guarantees. This
action increased the current quarter&#146;s net sales by $2.5 million and net earnings by
$0.8 million. Included in the net earnings from FIN 46 dealerships is a net gain of $0.7
million due to one of the entities transitioning its business to another independent
dealer during the quarter. In addition, the effect of the remaining FIN 46 entity on the
company&#146;s balance sheet as of August 28, 2004, was an increase to assets of

year&#146;s cash from operations was a voluntary contribution of $23.0 million to the
company&#146;s employee pension fund. Capital spending for the quarter was $3.8 million
compared to $5.5&nbsp;million for the same period last year. The company also repurchased
approximately 1.4&nbsp;million shares of its stock for $37.5 million, at an average price
