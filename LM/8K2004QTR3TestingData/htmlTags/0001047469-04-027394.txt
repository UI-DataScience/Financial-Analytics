<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To

the most comparable financial measure calculated in accordance with GAAP, diluted earnings per share including tax effected merger integration costs. </FONT></P>

<BR></FONT><FONT SIZE=2><B>SPORTS AUTHORITY ANNOUNCES SECOND QUARTER 2004 RESULTS    <BR>    </B></FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Englewood, CO, August&nbsp;26, 2004&#151;The Sports Authority,&nbsp;Inc. (NYSE:TSA), today announced results for its second fiscal quarter ended
July&nbsp;31, 2004. </FONT></P>

<UL>
<DL compact>
<DT style='margin-bottom:-11pt;'><FONT SIZE=2>&#149;</FONT></DT><DD><FONT SIZE=2>Diluted
EPS of $0.45, excluding merger integration costs.
<BR><BR></FONT></DD><DT style='margin-bottom:-11pt;'><FONT SIZE=2>&#149;</FONT></DT><DD><FONT SIZE=2>Diluted
EPS of $0.25, including merger integration costs. </FONT></DD></DL>
</UL>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On
August&nbsp;4, 2003, Gart Sports Company and The Sports Authority,&nbsp;Inc. (TSA) announced that they had completed a merger of equals. The results for the 13 and 26 weeks ended
July&nbsp;31, 2004 represent the performance of the consolidated company, while the year ago results reflect Gart Sports Company on a stand-alone basis. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net
income for the second quarter was $6.7&nbsp;million, or $0.25 per diluted share, including the effect of after-tax merger integration costs of $5.1&nbsp;million, or
$0.20 per diluted share compared with $0.42 per diluted share in the prior year's second quarter, which included the effect of after-tax merger integration costs of $1.0&nbsp;million, or
$0.08 per diluted share. Excluding merger integration costs, net income for the second quarter was $11.9&nbsp;million, or $0.45 per diluted share, compared with net income of $6.3&nbsp;million, or
$0.50 per diluted share in the prior year's second quarter for the former Gart Sports Company on a stand-alone basis. Pro forma combined earnings for the prior year's second quarter were $0.22 per
diluted share. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Total
sales for the second quarter were $605.0&nbsp;million compared with $267.5&nbsp;million in the prior year's second quarter as reported by the former Gart Sports Company on a
stand-alone basis. Second quarter comparable store sales for the combined company decreased 3.8% from last year's combined company results. </FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net
income for the 26 weeks ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, was $10.8&nbsp;million, or $0.41 per diluted share, including the effect of after-tax merger integration costs of
$10.4&nbsp;million, or $0.39 per diluted share, compared with $0.75 per diluted share in the prior year's comparable period, which included the effect of after-tax merger integration
costs of $1.0&nbsp;million, or $0.08 per diluted share and included income related to non-recurring events and a related tax benefit of $2.0&nbsp;million or $0.15 per diluted share.
Excluding these items, diluted earnings per share for the 26 weeks ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 was $0.80 compared with $0.68 per fully diluted share in the prior year's comparable period for the former
Gart Sports Company on a stand-alone basis. Pro forma combined earnings for the prior year's comparable period were $0.34 per diluted share. </FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Total
sales for the 26 weeks ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 were $1.18&nbsp;billion compared with $495.9&nbsp;million in the prior year's comparable period as reported by the former Gart
Sports Company on a stand-alone basis. Year-to-date comparable store sales for the combined company decreased 1.9% from last year's combined company results. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The
Company opened two stores and closed one store during the quarter to arrive at a total number of stores in operation as of July&nbsp;31, 2004 of 386 stores in 45 states. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doug
Morton, Chairman, Chief Executive Officer and President of The Sports Authority, stated, "As we previously announced, our more seasonal outdoor categories under-performed
expectations due to unusually cool and wet weather in many of our key markets. Also, we continue to experience weak sales of fitness equipment, which represents a significant part of our business. We
expect fitness sales to improve as we complete our new merchandise assortments in this category by the end of the third quarter. Furthermore, we believe we can improve the effectiveness of our
advertising by implementing a number of personnel and procedural changes to address this issue." </FONT></P>


<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Morton
continued, "Despite these challenges, we continue to make progress on many of our initiatives. We are pleased with the initial success of our store remodel program
where the incremental sales increases in the first twenty-seven remodeled stores has approximated seven percent. We are also excited about the rollout of our statement shoe walls, which was
substantially completed at the end of the second quarter. We believe these footwear walls, once fully merchandised, will drive additional sales and further enhance the overall shopping experience.
Additionally, we are well positioned in our historically strong winter sports categories as we head into the second half of the year. The third quarter will be the first quarter since the merger that
we will have complete cold weather assortments, including ski and snowboard equipment, apparel and accessories in the company's Sports Authority stores." </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Morton
concluded, "Although we are disappointed with our most recent results, we believe we have identified the key merchandising and operational issues that contributed to
our performance and we are taking the necessary steps to address these challenges. The necessary changes to address these issues will not have an immediate impact and therefore, we are taking a more
conservative approach with our guidance for the remainder of this year." </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The
Company is currently forecasting comparable store sales to decrease in the low single-digits for the third quarter of fiscal 2004. Gross margins are forecasted to increase only
slightly during the quarter due to markdowns and promotional strategies focused on liquidating summer outdoor inventories. The Company expects to report net income in the range of $0.0 to
$1.3&nbsp;million, and diluted EPS in the range of $0.00 to $0.05 based on 26.4&nbsp;million diluted shares outstanding in the quarter. All earnings estimates are exclusive of merger integration
costs. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For
fiscal year 2004, the Company expects to report net income in the range of $49.8 to $51.1&nbsp;million, and diluted EPS between $1.88 to $1.93, based on an estimated
26.5&nbsp;million diluted shares outstanding. All earnings estimates are exclusive of merger integration costs. The Company currently expects to open 22 new stores during the year and expects to
close up to a total of 13 stores. The number of stores in operation at the end of fiscal 2004 is expected to be 393. </FONT></P>
