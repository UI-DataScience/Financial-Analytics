<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>H&#038;R BLOCK REPORTS FIRST QUARTER FINANCIAL RESULTS</B><BR>
<B><I>Company affirms full-year earnings guidance of $4 to $4.25 per share</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">FOR RELEASE AUGUST 24, 2004 4 P.M. EDT



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;KANSAS CITY, Mo. &#151; H&#038;R Block Inc. (NYSE: HRB) today reported a $44.1
million net loss for its first quarter, compared with earnings of $5.2&nbsp;million
last year. Revenues totaled $482.7&nbsp;million, a 2.6&nbsp;percent decline from last
year. The company reported a loss of 26 cents per diluted share, compared to 3
cents of earnings last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The decline in earnings was primarily due to decreased mortgage segment
income and additional off-season costs associated with expanding the company&#146;s
tax business.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;The first quarter loss is in line with our internal expectations, and
consistent with our annual earnings guidance range of $4 to $4.25 per share,&#148;
said H&#038;R Block Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Mark A. Ernst. &#147;This
quarter we also repurchased 7.5&nbsp;million shares in accordance with our objective
to return excess capital to shareholders.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;In our mortgage unit, loan production increased more than 28&nbsp;percent for
the quarter, while margins declined because of the increase in market interest
rates. We expected margin compression in the first two quarters of our fiscal
year, with improved earnings performance in the second half of the year after
adjusting to the higher rate environment. Our mortgage business is on track to
meet our expectations for the year,&#148; Ernst said.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In its first quarter, H&#038;R Block acquired 7.5&nbsp;million shares of its common
stock at an aggregate cost of $347.4&nbsp;million, or an average price of $46.56 per
share. Over the past year, the number of H&#038;R Block shares outstanding has
declined 6.8&nbsp;percent to 166.2&nbsp;million shares outstanding.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The first quarter results include $4.6&nbsp;million in stock-based compensation
expense, compared with $1&nbsp;million last year. H&#038;R Block began to expense the
costs of all stock-based compensation at the beginning of fiscal year 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The prior year results include a change in accounting principle related to
the adoption of Emerging Issues Task Force No.&nbsp;00-21 (EITF 00-21) as of May&nbsp;1,
2003. This change reduced last year&#146;s first quarter net income by $6.4
million, or 3 cents per diluted share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Tax Services</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Beginning with the first quarter of fiscal year 2005, the company has
aggregated its U.S and International tax services into one segment.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tax services revenues increased 9.7&nbsp;percent to $50.4&nbsp;million. The segment
reported a pretax loss of $113&nbsp;million, which was 13.5&nbsp;percent higher than the
pretax loss of $99.6&nbsp;million in the first quarter of fiscal 2004. Results were
negatively affected by an additional $9&nbsp;million in off-season losses related to
former major franchise territories acquired during the second quarter of fiscal
year 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;We&#146;re on pace to open 500 to 600 new offices in under-penetrated areas
where we see growth opportunities. We also expect to add 400 more Wal-Mart
locations,&#148; Ernst said. &#147;We are confident in the value that clients receive
from H&#038;R Block and are working to make our services even more accessible.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Mortgage Services</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mortgage services, which include Option One Mortgage Corp. and H&#038;R Block
Mortgage Corp., reported first quarter pretax income of $93.5&nbsp;million, a 42.9
percent decrease, compared with $163.8&nbsp;million in the first quarter last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First quarter revenues declined 8.4&nbsp;percent to $268.1&nbsp;million, from $292.6
million the prior year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The mortgage segment&#146;s total gains on sales were $137.5&nbsp;million for the
first quarter, a 24.6&nbsp;percent decline compared with $182.3&nbsp;million in the prior
year&#146;s first quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Loan production increased to a record $6.8&nbsp;billion for the quarter, a 28.4
percent increase over the previous year&#146;s quarter. An increase in the number
of loan applications, improvements in the company&#146;s closing ratio, and an
increase in the average loan size all contributed to this growth.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Included within these results, H&#038;R Block Mortgage Corp., H&#038;R Block&#146;s
retail mortgage subsidiary, reported a 7.8&nbsp;percent decline in originations.
About 48&nbsp;percent of H&#038;R Block Mortgage&#146;s loans were made to retail clients of
other H&#038;R Block businesses.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;I&#146;m confident in the actions we have taken to position the business for
success in this changing rate environment. We are increasing our distribution
capability and improving our ability to serve clients and sustain our
competitive

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Business Services</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Business services reported a first quarter pretax loss of $10.1&nbsp;million,
compared with a pretax loss of $6.7&nbsp;million in the same quarter last year. The
segment reported revenues of $109.1&nbsp;million, an increase of 10.8&nbsp;percent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A significant backlog in the capital markets business at the end of fiscal
year 2003 increased first quarter revenue and earnings last year, which

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Our core accounting and tax services enjoyed a strong off-season quarter.
It&#146;s encouraging that most of our increased revenue is the result of our
strategic growth initiatives, including the development and implementation of a
marketing and sales infrastructure. We&#146;re well-positioned to capitalize on the
opportunity this market offers,&#148; Ernst said.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Investment Services</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Investment services reported a first quarter pretax loss of $18.3&nbsp;million,
an increased loss of $4.5&nbsp;million compared with last year&#146;s $13.8&nbsp;million loss.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Investment services reported first quarter revenues of $53.6&nbsp;million, a 6
percent decrease from the same quarter last year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;We&#146;re disappointed with investment services&#146; first quarter results, which
are consistent with the weak investment climate affecting the entire industry.
However, I&#146;m confident that our business has been positioned in a way that will
allow us to grow in support of the investment needs of our clients,&#148; Ernst
said.
