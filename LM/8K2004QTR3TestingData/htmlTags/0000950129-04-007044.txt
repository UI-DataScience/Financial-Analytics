<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>MITCHAM INDUSTRIES REPORTS SECOND QUARTER RESULTS</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">HUNTSVILLE, Texas &#150; September&nbsp;13, 2004 &#150; Mitcham Industries, Inc. (NASDAQ:
MIND) today reported revenues for its second quarter ended July&nbsp;31, 2004 of
$6.4&nbsp;million, up 63% from revenues of $3.9&nbsp;million recorded in the comparable
quarter of the previous year. Net income for the second quarter totaled
$155,000, or $0.02 per diluted share, as compared to a net loss in the prior
year of $4.1&nbsp;million, or ($0.47) per diluted share (inclusive of discontinued
operations). During the quarter, the Company incurred one-time
severance-related charges of approximately $0.7&nbsp;million related to the
resignation of its former chief financial officer.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are very encouraged by the improvement in our second quarter results and in
the seismic exploration market as a whole,&#148; said Billy F. Mitcham, Jr.,
President and CEO of Mitcham Industries. &#147;Historically, the second quarter is
our seasonally slowest quarter, yet revenues increased significantly in our
core land and marine leasing business and we experienced stronger used
equipment sales. Various indicators, including recent bid activity, equipment
movement and public announcements of companies adding crew capacity, suggest
that the worst of the downturn may be behind us and that the seismic
exploration market is in an uptrend.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the six months ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, the Company recorded net income of $1.5
million, $0.17 per share, as compared to a net loss (inclusive of discontinued
operations) of $5.6&nbsp;million, or ($0.64) per share, for the prior year period.
Revenues totaled $14.6&nbsp;million as compared to $9.8&nbsp;million for the comparable
six-month period of the prior year.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Mitcham Industries, Inc., a geophysical equipment supplier, offers for lease or
sale, new and &#147;experienced&#148; seismic equipment to the oil and gas industry,
seismic contractors, environmental agencies, government agencies and
universities. Headquartered in Texas, with sales and services offices in
Calgary, Canada, Brisbane, Australia and associates throughout Europe, South
America and Asia, Mitcham conducts operations on a global scale and is the
largest independent exploration equipment lessor in the industry.
