<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>UTi WORLDWIDE REPORTS RECORD RESULTS FOR FISCAL 2005 SECOND QUARTER</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Rancho Dominguez, California &#150; September&nbsp;7, 2004 </B>&#150; UTi Worldwide Inc. (NASDAQ
NM: UTIW) today reported strong financial results for its three- and six-month
periods ended July&nbsp;31, 2004, posting another record quarter of gross and net
revenues and net income.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Gross revenues for the fiscal 2005 second quarter increased 49&nbsp;percent to
$540.4&nbsp;million from $362.0&nbsp;million in the prior-year comparable period. Net
revenues for the current second quarter rose 29&nbsp;percent to $186.5&nbsp;million from
$144.9&nbsp;million in the fiscal 2004 second quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the second quarter of
fiscal 2005, UTi achieved a 27&nbsp;percent increase in
airfreight forwarding net revenues; a 32&nbsp;percent rise in ocean freight
forwarding net revenues; and a 29&nbsp;percent improvement in contract logistics net
revenues, compared with the prior-year second quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are pleased with the growth in UTi&#146;s global business since the launch of
our <B><I>NextLeap </I></B>initiative 10 quarters ago,&#148; said Roger I. MacFarlane, chief
executive officer of UTi Worldwide. &#147;We believe this record underscores the
soundness of our five-year strategic plan and the customer-centric focus of
UTi&#146;s global team.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Operating income increased 56&nbsp;percent to $23.5&nbsp;million in the fiscal 2005
second quarter from $15.0&nbsp;million in the prior-year comparable period.
Operating margin, which is operating income expressed as a percentage of net
revenues, rose to 12.6&nbsp;percent in the second quarter for fiscal 2005, compared
with 10.4&nbsp;percent in the fiscal 2004 second quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Our focus on improved operating margins is evident in the results as each of
our geographic regions made solid contributions to the growth in operating
income for the second quarter,&#148; MacFarlane said.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">UTi&#146;s operations in Asia Pacific continued to deliver strong performance. The
region accounted for the largest contribution to operating income, with
operating income in the region increasing 32&nbsp;percent in the second quarter of
fiscal 2005 over the prior-year second quarter on a 22&nbsp;percent improvement in
net revenues. Europe posted a 140&nbsp;percent increase in operating income in the
quarter, compared with the year-earlier second quarter, reflecting particularly
strong performances in Spain and Germany. The company attributed the
more-than-doubling of operating income in Europe, on a 42&nbsp;percent increase in
net revenues, to improved operating efficiencies resulting from increased
volumes through UTi&#146;s network of offices. Operating income in the second
quarter of fiscal 2005 in the Americas rose 34&nbsp;percent on a 4&nbsp;percent increase
in net revenues, compared with the fiscal 2004 second quarter. The company
attributed the improvement in operating margin in the Americas to increases in
freight forwarding in the region during the quarter and to the shedding of

business model as we achieved excellent results, primarily through organic
growth, across all business categories and geographic regions, despite a global
environment teeming with challenges, both geopolitical and economic,&#148;
MacFarlane said. &#147;We are particularly pleased with the improvement in
operating margins and strong net earnings gains for UTi. As we continue to
strengthen our global platform and expand our contract logistics capabilities,
we believe we are better positioned than ever to deliver greater value as we
become the primary logistics partner to more of our customers.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For the first half of fiscal 2005, gross revenues rose 50&nbsp;percent to $1.0
billion from $688.8&nbsp;million for the same prior-year period. Net revenues were
up 29&nbsp;percent for the first half of fiscal 2005, totaling $356.5&nbsp;million,
compared with $276.3&nbsp;million in the comparable fiscal 2004 period. Operating
income for the year-to-date period rose 62&nbsp;percent to $42.2&nbsp;million from $26.1
million in the prior year comparable period. Operating income as a percentage
of net revenues improved to 11.8&nbsp;percent for the current six-month period from
9.5&nbsp;percent during the prior year comparable period. Net income for the first
half of fiscal 2005 advanced 50&nbsp;percent to $29.0&nbsp;million, or $0.91 per diluted
share, from $19.4&nbsp;million, or $0.62 per diluted share, for the first six months
of fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">At July&nbsp;31, 2004, the company reported total cash and cash equivalents, net of
outstanding bank lines of credit and short-term bank borrowings, of $123.2
million, compared with $137.2&nbsp;million at January&nbsp;31, 2004. This decrease
reflects the cash used for the IHD acquisition, which was partially offset by
the $24.3&nbsp;million of cash that the company generated through operating
activities in the year-to-date period ended July&nbsp;31, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">MacFarlane added: &#147;We have enjoyed a healthy first half to the year,
reflecting our team&#146;s focus on meeting customer needs while not losing sight of
our operating margin objectives. We have also benefited from generally
improved economic conditions which we currently expect to continue for the
remainder of the year. However, our cautious optimism for prospects in the
second half of fiscal 2005 is tempered by several factors that will impinge on
costs, including our continued investment in rolling out eMpower<sup>5</sup> to our
customers worldwide as well as the escalating fees associated with meeting the
testing, compliance and control aspects of Sarbanes-Oxley.&#148;
