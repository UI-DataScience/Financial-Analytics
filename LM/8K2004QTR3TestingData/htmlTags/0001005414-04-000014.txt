<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p align="center" style="margin-bottom: 0"><b><u><font size="2" color="black">TOYS &ldquo;R&rdquo; US ANNOUNCES SECOND QUARTER 2004 RESULTS</font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin-top: 0"><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Company Ends Quarter With $1.1 Billion in Cash</font></i></b></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><font size="2" color="black">WAYNE, NEW JERSEY, August 23, 2004 </font></b><font size="2" color="black">- Toys &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us, Inc. today announced results for its second quarter ended July 31, 2004.&nbsp; The company reported net
earnings of $61 million, or $0.28 per share, for the second quarter of 2004, compared with a net loss of $11 million, or $0.05 per share, for the second quarter of 2003.&nbsp; For the six-month period ended July 31, 2004, the company reported net earnings of $33
million, or $0.15 per share, versus a net loss of $37 million, or $0.17 per share for 2003.</font> <font size="2">(All per share figures
<font color="black">refer to diluted per share amounts.)&nbsp; Net earnings in the second quarter and first six months include the
reversal of $200 million of income tax reserves as a result of the conclusion of the Internal Revenue Service&rsquo;s review of proposed adjustments to the company&rsquo;s tax filings for 1997 through 1999.&nbsp; This reversal of income tax reserves was previously
disclosed by the company in a Form 8-K filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission on July 6, 2004.</font></font></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">Total sales for the second quarter of 2004 were $2.0 billion, down 3.9% from $2.1 billion for the second quarter of 2003.&nbsp; For the six months ended July 31, 2004, sales were $4.1 billion, down 3.2% from last
year&rsquo;s $4.2 billion.</font></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">Excluding the impact of currency translation that increased 2004 second quarter sales by $30 million, and a $68 million decline in sales associated with the previously announced Kids &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us (KRU)
store closings, total sales decreased 2.2% for the second quarter.&nbsp; For the first six months of 2004 currency translation had an $81 million favorable impact, while the KRU closings resulted in a $140 million decline.&nbsp; Excluding these amounts, sales for the
first six months of 2004 were down 1.9% compared with the same period in 2003.</font></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">Operating loss for the second quarter of 2004 was $192 million versus operating earnings of $14 million for the prior year.&nbsp; For the six months ended July 31, 2004, operating loss was $207 million compared
with operating earnings of $2 million for 2003.&nbsp;</font></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">Operating loss for the second quarter included pre-tax charges totaling $228 million, of which $38 million were included in restructuring and other charges, while $154 million were included in cost of sales and
$36 million were included in SG&amp;A.&nbsp; Results for the six months ended July 31, 2004 included charges totaling $246 million, of which $53 million were included in restructuring and other charges, while $157 million were included in cost of sales and $36
million were included in SG&amp;A.&nbsp; Details of these charges are described below.</font></p>

<ul>
  <li><font size="2" color="black">Severance and other related charges of $14 million associated with the restructuring of the company&rsquo;s corporate headquarter operations in Wayne, New Jersey.</font></li>
</ul>

<p align="center">&nbsp;</p>

<p align="center"><b><font size="2" color="black">1</font></b></p>

<div align="center">
<hr size="2" width="100%" noshade color="navy" align="center" />
</div>

<ul>
  <li>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 20"><font size="2" color="black">Markdowns of $147 million primarily to liquidate selected older toy store inventory and therefore enhance store productivity and supply chain efficiency.&nbsp; This is also intended to accelerate inventory turnover and generate
additional cash flow.</font></li>
  <li>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 20"><font size="2" color="black">Other charges of $48 million, including reserves established for unclaimed property and charges related to lease commitments on idle facilities.</font></li>
  <li><font size="2" color="black">Continuing costs and charges of $19 million in the second quarter, and a total of $37 million for the first half associated with the previously announced Kids &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us (KRU) store closings.</font></li>
</ul>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Balance Sheet, Liquidity and Inventory Management</font></i></b></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">The company ended the second quarter of 2004 with $1.1 billion in cash and cash equivalents.&nbsp; Net capital expenditures were $82 million for the first six months of 2004 up from $71 million last year as a
result of increased investment in new and renovated Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us stores.&nbsp; We expect annual net capital expenditures for the company to come in at or below last year&rsquo;s level. In addition, capital expenditures for the global toy business are
expected to be significantly below anticipated annual depreciation and amortization of approximately $300 million.&nbsp; At July 31, 2004, total inventories were down 14% versus last year&rsquo;s first half. After adjusting for factors including currency translation
(-2%), EITF 02-16 and EITF 03-10 (+2%), inventory markdowns (+6%) and the impact of the KRU store closings (+4%); inventory was down 4% versus the first half of last year.&nbsp;</font></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">John Eyler, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, said, &ldquo;Although the U. S. toy stores had a very challenging second quarter, we were pleased with the operating performance of our International business,
Toysrus.com, and Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us.&nbsp; Before the charges described above, both the International and Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us divisions achieved meaningful increases in operating earnings for the quarter, while losses at Toysrus.com narrowed.&nbsp; We
have also maintained good discipline in inventory management this year.&rdquo;</font></p>

<p><font size="2">Mr. Eyler continued<font color="black">, &ldquo;Maintaining an exceptionally strong balance sheet, ample liquidity and significant financial flexibility continue to be among Toys &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us Inc.&rsquo;s highest
priorities.&nbsp; We are confident that these priorities will enable us to meet all of our outstanding and ongoing financial commitments, and continue to expect that we will generate significant cash from operations this year and going forward.&nbsp; In addition, as
we have said previously, we pursue our business strategies with a commitment to fulfill our obligations to all of our constituents, including our equity and debt holders, vendors, associates and guests.&rdquo;</font></font></p>

<p><font size="2">Mr. Eyler concluded, "Therefore, for the balance of the year, everyone throughout our global toy organization is focused on maximizing our performance during the holiday season, our peak period for cash generation.&nbsp; Our
plans for all of our stores are in place, and the steps we are taking to clear selected older inventory will further improve the appearance of our stores and allow us to provide an even better showcase for offering the widest assortment of toys available to our
guests in each and every Toys "R" Us store as well as on Toysrus.com."</font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Global Toy Business:&nbsp; Segment Performance</font></i></b></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">In the <b>U.S. Toy Store</b> division, comparable store sales decreased 7.7% for the second quarter of 2004 and were down 6.6% for the six months ended July 31, 2004.&nbsp; Total sales were $1 billion for the
second quarter versus $1.1 billion for the same period last year.&nbsp; Total sales for the first half were $2.1 billion versus $2.3 billion last year.&nbsp; This decrease was primarily attributable to the video game business, which declined 20% versus last
year&rsquo;s second quarter.&nbsp; Operating loss for the second quarter of 2004 was $138 million versus operating earnings of $9 million in the second quarter of 2003.&nbsp; Operating loss for the six months ended July 31, 2004 was $152 million versus an operating
loss of $2 million for 2003.&nbsp; Results for the second quarter and the first half of 2004 include $132 million of the aforementioned charges.</font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p align="center"><b><font size="2" color="black">2</font></b></p>

<div align="center">
<hr size="2" width="100%" noshade color="navy" align="center" />
</div>

<font size="2" color="black"><br clear="all" />
In the <b>International</b> division, comparable toy store sales in local currencies were up 1.7% during the second quarter of 2004 and down 0.6% for the six months ended July 31, 2004.&nbsp; Excluding the impact
of currency translation, total sales were $442 million, up 2.3% from the same quarter last year, and were $807 million for the six months ended July 2004 as compared to $808 million for the comparable period in 2003.&nbsp; Operating earnings were $6 million for the
second quarter of 2004 compared to $4 million in 2003.</font><font size="2">&nbsp; Operating loss of the six months ended July 31, 2004 and August 2, 2003 were $8 million.&nbsp; Second quarter and first half operating results for the International division include
$15 million of the aforementioned markdowns.&nbsp;</font>

<p><font size="2" color="black">At <b>Toysrus.com</b>, second quarter 2004 sales remained flat at $51 million, as compared to the second quarter of 2003.&nbsp; S</font><font size="2">econd quarter sales increased 10% from last year after
excluding the second quarter 2003 sales for Toysrus.com Japan which was sold to Toys &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us Japan during the third quarter of 2003.&nbsp; <font color="black">Sales for the six months ended July 31, 2004 decreased 3.7% to $104 million from $108 million in
2003.&nbsp; Sales for the six months ended July 31, 2004 increased 6.2%</font> after excluding the 2003 sales of Toysrus.com Japan.&nbsp; <font color="black">Operating loss for the second quarter of 2004 decreased to $5 million from $9 million in 2003.&nbsp; For the
six months ended July 31, 2004, the operating loss was $9 million versus an operating loss of $17 million for the comparable period in 2003.</font></font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us Performance</font></i></b></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">The Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us business posted a 5.8% increase in total sales to $454 million, which represents a 1.8% increase in comparable store sales versus the second quarter of 2003.&nbsp; Year to date
comparable store sales have increased 3.2%.&nbsp; During the first half of 2004 total sales increased to $945 million, up from $877 million in 2003.&nbsp; Operating earnings increased 17% to $54 million from $46 million for the second quarter of 2004.&nbsp;
Operating earnings for the six months ended July 31, 2004 were $117 million, up from $100 million in 2003.</font> <font size="2">Two
<font color="black">new Babies &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us stores opened
during the first six months of 2004.&nbsp; Approximately 18 additional new store openings, along with additional store renovations, are planned this year.</font></font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Sale of Kids &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us Stores</font></i></b></p>

<p><font size="2" color="black">The company previously announced an agreement under which Office Depot, Inc. will acquire the majority of the former Kids &ldquo;R&rdquo; Us stores.&nbsp; As previously indicated, this transaction is closing in
phases.&nbsp; More than half of the closings are complete at July 31, 2004, and cash proceeds of more than $100 million have been received.&nbsp; The company currently anticipates that the remaining closings will be completed, and the resulting gain recorded, over
the next several months as the transaction is concluded.</font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b><i><font size="2" color="black">Implementation of EITF 03-10 and EITF 02-16</font></i></b></p>

<p><font size="2">In 2004, the company adopted the provisions of Emerging Issues Task Force (EITF) Issue No. 03-10, &ldquo;Application of EITF Issue No. 02-16, &lsquo;Accounting by a Customer (Including a Reseller) for Certain Consideration
Received from a Vendor,&rsquo; by Resellers to Sales Incentives Offered to Consumers by Manufacturers&rdquo; (EITF 03-10).&nbsp; Accordingly, 2004 sales are recorded net of coupons redeemed.&nbsp; <font color="black">The company&rsquo;s 2003 financial results as
permitted by the provisions of EITF 03-10 have been adjusted to the current year&rsquo;s presentation.</font>&nbsp; Accordingly, results for the first half of 2003 have been adjusted down by $31 million ($19 million net of tax), or by $0.09 per share, of which $30
million ($19 million net of tax), or by $0.09 per share, relates to the first quarter.</font></p>

<p><font size="2">The company&rsquo;s 2003 results also include the implementation of EITF No. 02-16, &ldquo;Accounting by a Customer (Including a Reseller) for Certain Consideration Received from a Vendor&rdquo; (EITF 02-16), which considers
amounts received from vendors to be a reduction of product cost that should be recognized as merchandise is sold, unless certain restrictive provisions are met.&nbsp;</font></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p align="center"><b><font size="2">3</font></b></p>

<div align="center">
<hr size="2" width="100%" noshade color="navy" align="center" />
</div>

<p><font size="2">The implementation of the provisions of EITF 02-16 unfavorably impacted the company&rsquo;s results for the second quarter of 2003 by $9 million ($6 million net of tax), or by $0.03 per share, and for the six months ended
August 2, 2003 by $23 million ($15 million, net of tax), or by $0.07 per share.</font></p>

<p><font size="2">The provisions of EITF 03-10 and EITF 02-16 did not permit adoption through a cumulative effect adjustment at the beginning of 2003. If we had been permitted to record a cumulative effect adjustment at the beginning of 2003,
the unfavorable impact on 2003 second quarter and first six months results would have been reduced by $14 million ($9 million after tax), or $0.04 per share; and by $27 million ($17 million after tax), or $0.08 per share, respectively related to the non-recurring
initial implementation of the provisions of EITF 03-10 and EITF 02-16.</font></p>

<p><font size="2">The implementation of the provisions for EITF 03-10 and EITF 02-16 had no impact on the company&rsquo;s cash flow.&nbsp;</font></p>

licensed and franchise stores as well as through its Internet sites at <u>www.toysrus.com,</u> <u>www.imaginarium.com</u> and <u>www.sportsrus.com.</u>&nbsp; Babies "R" Us is the</font></b> <b><font size="2">largest baby product specialty store chain in the world and
a leader in the juvenile industry, and sells merchandise through 200 stores in the U.S. as well as on the Internet at</font></b> <b><u><font size="2">www.babiesrus.com</font></u><font size="2">.</font></b></p>
