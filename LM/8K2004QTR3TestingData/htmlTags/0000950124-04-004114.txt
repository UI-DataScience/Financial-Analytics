<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;2.02 Results of Operations and Financial Condition.</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On August&nbsp;31, 2004, the Company released its sales and earnings for the 2004
second quarter. The text of that release is attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1
and is incorporated herein by reference. The press release should be read
together with the information contained in the reports that we file with the
Securities and Exchange Commission, including the financial statements and
related notes contained in those reports.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The press release contains the non-GAAP financial measures of adjusted earnings
from continuing operations on a per share basis, adjusted identical store sales
and adjusted comparable store sales. Pursuant to the requirements of
Regulation&nbsp;G, the Company has provided reconciliations within the release of
these measures to the most directly comparable GAAP financial measures. The
Company presents adjusted sales computations because it believes a comparison
of actual to adjusted store sales data is useful to investors to communicate
management&#146;s belief of the continuing impact of the southern California labor
dispute on trends in sales experienced during the 13&nbsp;weeks ended July&nbsp;29, 2004.
The Company presents adjusted per share earnings from continuing operations
because it believes that excluding the special items and charges identified in
the release results in a useful financial measure that will facilitate
comparisons of the Company&#146;s operating results before, during and after such
items and charges are incurred. Also, the Company believes that communicating
adjusted earnings per share facilitates comparisons of performance with that of
other companies that did not have an event similar to the southern California
labor dispute.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">This information is being furnished under Item&nbsp;12 and shall not be deemed
&#147;filed&#148; for purposes of Section&nbsp;18 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as
amended, or otherwise subject to the liability of such section.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ALBERTSONS ANNOUNCES SECOND QUARTER RESULTS</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Earnings Exceed First Call Consensus</I></B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Southern California Recovery Ahead of Schedule</I></B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>New Labor Contracts Negotiated for 19,000 Associates in Four Divisions</I></B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Company Reaffirms $1.40 to $1.50 EPS Guidance for 2004</I></B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Cost Savings Program Reaches $820 Million</I></B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>BOISE, Idaho (August&nbsp;31, 2004) &#150; </B>Albertson&#146;s, Inc. (NYSE: ABS) reported second
quarter 2004 earnings from continuing operations today of $125&nbsp;million or $0.34
per diluted share, exceeding the $0.33 per share First Call consensus of
industry analysts. In the prior year&#146;s second quarter, earnings from
continuing operations totaled $162&nbsp;million or $0.44 per diluted share.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Second quarter earnings from continuing operations were affected by two unusual
items as follows:

reduced earnings by $0.13 per share or $50&nbsp;million.</TD>

of funding deficits. This reduced earnings per share by $0.01 or $5
million on an after-tax basis.</TD>
</TR>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Excluding these items, second quarter earnings from continuing operations would
have been $0.48 per share or $180&nbsp;million.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In addition, the Company&#146;s exits from the Omaha and New Orleans markets reduced
net earnings by $0.06 per share or by $21&nbsp;million primarily due to non-cash
impairment write-downs associated with the sale of property and equipment, as
these items were classified as discontinued operations.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">All financial results for the second quarter include the results from
operations at Shaw&#146;s for the entire quarter. This acquisition was closed on
April&nbsp;30, 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total Company sales reached $10.2&nbsp;billion for the quarter versus last year&#146;s
second quarter sales of $9.0&nbsp;billion. This increase was due primarily to sales
volume from the Shaw&#146;s acquisition and was partially offset by the residual
effect of the Southern California labor dispute, which the Company estimates
negatively impacted total sales by approximately $182&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total Company comparable store sales for the quarter (including the continued
effects of the Southern California labor dispute) declined 1.3% and identical
store sales declined 1.5%. Excluding the Southern California stores involved
in the labor dispute, total Company comparable store sales for the quarter
increased 0.2% and identical store sales were flat. Comparable and identical
store sales will not include the Shaw&#146;s stores until the second quarter of
2005.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Larry Johnston, Chairman, CEO and President, commented on the results. &#147;Despite
a challenging environment that existed for all retailers during the last
quarter, we continued to make progress on a number of key initiatives.
Comparable store sales continued to grow outside of Southern California and we
are very pleased to report that in our Southern California division, we have
retained and recovered more of our business than originally anticipated,
keeping us ahead of our plans there for both sales and profitability. We also
continued making progress to improve our total asset portfolio during the
quarter, evidenced by our acquisition of Shaw&#146;s and the Omaha market exit. On
the labor front, I am pleased to report that following quarter-end, we
successfully renegotiated new labor contracts covering 19,000 associates in
four of our divisions, which will help to significantly improve our future
competitiveness in these important markets.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Total gross profit for the quarter was $2.9&nbsp;billion, including the continuing
impact of the labor dispute, versus $2.6&nbsp;billion a year ago. Gross profit as a
percent of total sales declined to 28.25% from 29.36% in the same period of
last year. The gross profit rate declined primarily as a result of the
continuing effects of the labor dispute. The Company estimates that these
effects negatively impacted gross profit by $114&nbsp;million, or 61 basis points.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the quarter, selling, general and administrative (SG&#038;A) expenses totaled
25.01% of sales versus 25.33% in the second quarter of 2003. The decrease in
SG&#038;A was primarily related to improved sales leverage as a result of the Shaw&#146;s
acquisition, as well as lower costs for Company-sponsored health and welfare
plans and lower workers compensation costs. Decreases were partially offset by
the continuing negative effect of the labor dispute, which impacted SG&#038;A in the
second quarter by approximately 10 basis points.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In a major cost control program initiated in mid-2001, the Company continued to
stay ahead of schedule to reduce costs by $1&nbsp;billion before the end of 2005.
Through the second quarter of 2004, $820&nbsp;million in savings had been realized.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cash flow from operations in the second quarter grew 44% to $729&nbsp;million, up
$223&nbsp;million from $506&nbsp;million in the prior year.
