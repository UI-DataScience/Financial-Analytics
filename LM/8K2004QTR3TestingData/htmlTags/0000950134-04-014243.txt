<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Solectron Reports Improved Fourth Quarter Results</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For Immediate Release: Sept. 28, 2004


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">MILPITAS, Calif. &#151; Solectron Corporation (NYSE: SLR), a leading provider of
electronics manufacturing and integrated supply chain services, today reported
sales of $3.01&nbsp;billion in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004, up 23.4&nbsp;percent
from $2.44&nbsp;billion in the fourth quarter of last year. Sales in the third
quarter of 2004 were $3.04&nbsp;billion.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The company reported a GAAP net loss from continuing operations in the fourth
quarter of $45&nbsp;million, or 5 cents per share, compared with a GAAP net loss
from continuing operations of $163&nbsp;million, or 20 cents per share, in the
year-earlier quarter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Non-GAAP net income from continuing operations was $41&nbsp;million, or 4 cents per
share on a fully diluted basis, in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Our strong performance in the fourth quarter, including non-GAAP net income of
$41&nbsp;million and positive operating cash flow of approximately $124&nbsp;million,
capped a turnaround year for Solectron,&#148; said Mike Cannon, president and chief
executive officer. &#147;We improved our financial metrics throughout the year, and
we enter fiscal 2005 a much stronger company.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">During the quarter, the company completed the sale of Force Computers and its
interest in U.S. Robotics. The company also announced an agreement to sell its
Microtechnology business, which is expected to be completed in the first
quarter of fiscal 2005. With that, Solectron will have completed its
divestiture plans announced a year ago.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Non-GAAP Information</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">In addition to disclosing results determined in accordance with generally
accepted accounting principles (GAAP), Solectron also discloses non-GAAP
results of operations that exclude certain items. By disclosing this non-GAAP
information, management intends to provide investors with additional
information to further analyze the company&#146;s performance, core results and
underlying trends. Management utilizes a measure of net income and earnings per
share on a non-GAAP basis that excludes certain charges to better assess
operating performance. Each excluded item is considered to be of a
non-operational nature in the applicable period. Earnings guidance is provided
only on a non-GAAP basis due to the inherent difficulty in forecasting such
charges. Consistent with industry practice, management has historically applied
these non-GAAP measures when discussing earnings or earnings guidance and
intends to continue doing so.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Non-GAAP information is not determined using GAAP; therefore, the information
is not necessarily comparable to other companies and should not be used to
compare the company&#146;s performance over different periods. Non-GAAP information
should not be viewed as a substitute for, or superior to, net income or other
data prepared in accordance with GAAP as measures of our profitability or
liquidity. Users of this financial information should consider the types of
events and transactions for which adjustments have been made. See the tables to
the press release for a reconciliation of non-GAAP amounts to amounts reported
under GAAP.
